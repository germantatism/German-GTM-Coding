# SDR Research Brief — Farfetch
**Date:** 2026-04-07 | **Analyst:** Yuno GTM Intelligence

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Farfetch is a global luxury fashion marketplace operating in 190+ countries, acquired by Coupang (NYSE: CPNG) in January 2024 for ~$500M. Their confirmed payment stack includes **Adyen** (primary PSP since 2012), **Checkout.com** (enterprise processor), **Volt** (open banking, launched Sept 2024), and **Triple-A** (crypto in 66 countries), plus 4 BNPL providers and 20+ APMs. Despite this diversified stack, **no payment orchestration layer was detected** — all integrations appear direct/in-house. The Yuno opportunity centers on orchestrating their multi-PSP setup (Adyen + Checkout.com + Volt), improving approval rates across 190+ markets, and simplifying their complex, fragmented APM routing — especially as the post-Coupang restructuring creates pressure to optimize costs and performance.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Multi-PSP Without Orchestration = Optimization Gap
**Evidence:** Section 3 — Adyen (primary) + Checkout.com (secondary) + Volt + Triple-A + 4 BNPL providers, all integrated directly with no orchestration layer.
**Pain Point:** Managing 8+ payment processor integrations in-house across 190+ countries creates engineering overhead, suboptimal routing, and no unified analytics.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single API to orchestrate all existing PSPs + smart routing to optimize approval rates across markets. No need to rip and replace — Yuno sits on top.
**Best Case:** InDrive achieved 90% approval rates across 10 LATAM markets in <8 months with Yuno orchestration.
**Outreach Angle:** "You've built impressive payment coverage with Adyen, Checkout.com, and Volt — Yuno can unify that stack under one API with smart routing to boost approvals without changing any processor."

### Insight #2: Cross-Border Acquiring in Top Markets
**Evidence:** Section 2 — Japan (#3), Brazil (#4), Germany (#5) show significant traffic but no confirmed local entities. Section 1 — these 3 markets represent ~14% of total traffic.
**Pain Point:** Cross-border card transactions face higher decline rates (5-15% higher) and FX fees vs. local acquiring.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart Routing can direct transactions to local acquirers where available (+7% approval uplift), reducing cross-border declines and FX costs.
**Best Case:** Reserva saw +4% approval uplift in <3 months by optimizing local vs. cross-border routing.
**Outreach Angle:** "With ~14% of your traffic in Japan, Brazil, and Germany, local acquiring optimization could meaningfully lift approvals — Yuno's smart routing does this automatically."

### Insight #3: Post-Acquisition Cost Pressure → Payment Optimization
**Evidence:** Section 6 — Coupang eliminated $1.6B in debt, executive shakeup, Italian subsidiary loan defaults, Q4 2024 revenue down 25% vs Q4 2022 but EBITDA turning positive (+$30M vs -$34M).
**Pain Point:** Under cost pressure to optimize every basis point. Payment processing is one of the largest cost centers for a $2.3B GMV luxury marketplace.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart routing reduces processing costs by directing transactions to cheapest qualified processor. Transaction recovery captures revenue from soft declines (50% recovery rate).
**Best Case:** Livelo achieved +5% approval uplift and 50% transaction recovery with Yuno.
**Outreach Angle:** "With the turnaround underway, optimizing payment costs and recovering failed transactions could add millions to your bottom line — Yuno clients see 50% recovery on soft declines."

### Insight #4: Marketplace Refund Friction = Monitoring Opportunity
**Evidence:** Section 5 — Refund delays (up to 14 days), lost returns, and crypto refund failures are the top payment complaints across Trustpilot and Reddit.
**Pain Point:** Marketplace model with 1,400+ boutiques makes payment reconciliation and refund management complex. Slow resolution = customer churn in luxury segment where experience is everything.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Real-time transaction monitoring with ms-level detection (vs. 5-10 min manual). Unified dashboard across all PSPs for faster dispute resolution.
**Best Case:** Rappi achieved zero implementation time and 80% less analyst resolution time with Yuno monitoring.
**Outreach Angle:** "Luxury customers expect flawless experiences — Yuno's real-time monitoring catches payment issues in milliseconds across all your processors, cutting resolution time by 80%."

### Insight #5: APAC/MENA Expansion Requires Local Payment Expertise
**Evidence:** Section 6 — South Korea R.LUX launch, GCC localization, APAC expansion. Section 4 — KaKao Pay (iOS only), Tamara (GCC), but Japan unverified.
**Pain Point:** Each new market requires new PSP integrations, local APMs, and regulatory compliance. Current in-house approach doesn't scale efficiently.
**Yuno Value Prop:** New market live in weeks with no-code PSP enablement. 1,000+ payment methods already integrated across 200+ countries.
**Best Case:** InDrive went live in 10 LATAM markets in <8 months using Yuno's pre-built integrations.
**Outreach Angle:** "As you expand into Korea and the GCC, Yuno can activate local payment methods in weeks instead of months — with 1,000+ methods already integrated across 200+ countries."
