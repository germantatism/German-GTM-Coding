## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Arrive Outdoors (arriveoutdoors.com) is a Santa Monica, CA-based company founded in 2017 that has undergone a significant business pivot — from a D2C outdoor gear rental marketplace to **Arrive Recommerce**, a B2B SaaS platform licensing rental and resale technology to major brands (Eddie Bauer, YETI, Burton, Public Lands/Dick's Sporting Goods). The consumer-facing site now operates as a WordPress/Elementor content site redirecting all purchases to Amazon, meaning **no native payment processing occurs on arriveoutdoors.com**. Total funding is ~$25.2M (Arrive Recommerce entity). The Yuno opportunity is limited on the consumer site but could exist within the **Arrive Recommerce B2B platform**, which handles multi-party rental/resale payment flows for brand partners — a use case where payment orchestration, smart routing, and marketplace payouts add value.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: B2B Recommerce Platform = Real Payment Opportunity

**Evidence:** Arrive Recommerce powers branded rental and resale for Eddie Bauer, YETI, Burton, Public Lands (S6). The platform handles full rental lifecycle including payment collection, damage charges, and multi-party payouts.
**Pain Point:** Complex payment flows — rental deposits, incremental charges (late fees, damage), refund processing, and brand partner payouts — across multiple merchant sites.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Payment orchestration for marketplace/platform models; smart routing across brand partners' preferred PSPs; unified reporting.
**Best Case:** Arrive Recommerce's payment stack is outgrowing a single PSP integration and needs orchestration as it scales brand partners.
**Outreach Angle:** "As Arrive Recommerce scales brand partners, managing payment flows across rental deposits, damage charges, and partner payouts across different PSPs gets complex — that's exactly what payment orchestration solves."

### Insight #2: Amazon Dependency Limits Consumer Site Monetization

**Evidence:** All consumer purchases redirect to Amazon (S3, S4, S8). Arrive earns affiliate commissions, not transaction revenue.
**Pain Point:** Zero control over checkout experience, payment methods, or customer data on the consumer side.
**Yuno Value Prop:** If Arrive ever rebuilds D2C checkout, Yuno provides instant access to 1,000+ payment methods via single API.
**Best Case:** Arrive decides to re-internalize checkout for higher margins and customer data ownership.
**Outreach Angle:** Lower priority — the Amazon redirect model suggests the consumer site is not the current focus.

### Insight #3: No Payment Orchestration in Outdoor Rental/Recommerce Vertical

**Evidence:** No competitors (REI Rentals, Trove, Archive, Recurate) show evidence of payment orchestration (S11). Rental SaaS platforms use basic Stripe/PayPal integrations.
**Pain Point:** Entire vertical runs on single-PSP setups, missing approval uplift and routing optimization.
**Yuno Value Prop:** First-mover advantage in recommerce vertical payment orchestration; InDrive case study (marketplace model) translates well.
**Best Case:** Arrive becomes a reference customer that brings the entire recommerce ecosystem to Yuno.
**Outreach Angle:** "The recommerce space is scaling fast but payment infrastructure hasn't kept up — brands like InDrive saw 90% approval rates after moving to orchestrated payments."

### Insight #4: Refund/Billing Policy Friction Creates Churn Risk

**Evidence:** Multiple complaints about no early-return refunds and steep penalty fees ($50/day + full retail price) (S5).
**Pain Point:** Rigid billing policies hurt customer satisfaction (3.7/5 rating) and could increase chargebacks.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Flexible billing/recurring payment management; transaction recovery (50% recovery rate from Yuno success cases).
**Best Case:** Arrive Recommerce implements more granular billing logic powered by orchestrated payment flows.
**Outreach Angle:** Secondary angle — pair with insight #1 for a comprehensive platform payment pitch.
