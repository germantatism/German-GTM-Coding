# Super.com — SDR Research Brief
*Yuno Payment Orchestrator | Date: 2026-06-02*

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Super.com (formerly SnapTravel / Snapcommerce) is a US savings super-app that books $1B+ in annual travel GMV and layers a fintech stack on top: a Super+ membership ($15/mo), a secured Mastercard, and cash advances, reaching 30M+ registered users (~95% US). Its card program runs on **Qolo** (issuing, processing, acquiring, ledger — publicly confirmed via Qolo's own case study), but the **acquiring gateway for the $1B+ hotel-booking flow is not publicly identified** — a single-stack dependency and a clean orchestration entry point. Combined with an active, public **"Director, Payments" hire** ($175K–$264K, spring 2026), a named payments leader (Rick Galasiewski, GM of Payments & Fintech Innovation), and heavy public friction around subscription billing, refunds, and card declines, Super.com is a 🔴 **High-priority Yuno target (score 15)**.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: The $1B+ travel-booking flow has no publicly identified acquirer
**Evidence**: §3A/§3B — Qolo runs the card program; the hotel-checkout acquiring gateway is unidentified, and no orchestrator is public.
**Pain Point**: Single-PSP / in-house dependency on the highest-GMV flow — no routing, no failover, no cross-acquirer auth optimization.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Smart Routing + failover across acquirers on the booking flow; +7% approval uplift typical.
**Best Case**: InDrive (10 LATAM markets <8 months, 90% approval, 4.5% recovery).
**Outreach Angle**: "Qolo clearly owns your card program. On the hotel-booking side, who's routing the $1B+ in travel GMV, and what's the fallback when an acquirer declines?"

### Insight #2: Recurring-billing and decline friction is the loudest complaint
**Evidence**: §5 — Super+ enrollment/cancellation friction (highest frequency), refund delays, and **false "wrong CVV/billing" declines on previously-working cards**.
**Pain Point**: Dunning/retry logic and auth optimization on a $15/mo subscription across a 30M+ user base; false declines kill conversion and trust.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Intelligent retries + network tokenization + 50% transaction recovery on declined attempts; unified dunning.
**Best Case**: Livelo (+5% approval, 50% recovery).
**Outreach Angle**: "We see a pattern of card declines flagged as wrong CVV/billing on cards that worked before — usually an auth-routing issue. Worth comparing how much of that is recoverable?"

### Insight #3: A payments org is being built right now
**Evidence**: §6 — public **"Director, Payments"** req ($175K–$264K) + a named GM of Payments & Fintech Innovation (Rick Galasiewski).
**Pain Point**: N/A — this is timing and access. New leaders re-evaluate the stack.
**Yuno Value Prop**: No-code PSP enablement, new flows live in weeks — ideal for a team scaling its payments function.
**Outreach Angle**: Time outreach to the Director, Payments hire; lead with Insight #1.

### Insight #4: International is the stated next move, but coverage is US-only today
**Evidence**: §1/§6 — 95% US, no local entities in UK/DE/ES, BNPL absent, wallets unconfirmed; expansion flagged as future growth.
**Pain Point**: Expanding internationally with a US-centric stack means per-country PSP integrations and missing local methods.
**Yuno Value Prop**: One integration to add local methods + acquirers as they enter new markets.
**Outreach Angle**: Position for the international-expansion conversation, not as a current gap.

### Insight #5: Paid-but-declined and double charges signal reconciliation gaps
**Evidence**: §5 — bookings paid but missing at the hotel; "forced to pay twice."
**Pain Point**: Fragmented reconciliation between the booking flow, the card program, and supply partners (MoR model).
**Yuno Value Prop**: Real-time monitors (Rappi: ms detection vs. 5–10 min manual) + unified reconciliation.
**Best Case**: Rappi (zero implementation time, 80% less analyst resolution).
