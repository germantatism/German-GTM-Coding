## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

DeepL is a Cologne-based AI language company (200K+ paying customers across 228 markets, $185.2M revenue in 2024 +31% YoY, $2B valuation, reportedly targeting a $5B IPO in late 2025/early 2026). Payment processing is confirmed as **Stripe-only** via DeepL's own Help Center — no secondary PSP, no orchestration layer, no public evidence of local acquiring in Japan (their #1 desktop market) or other non-EU geographies. The opportunity window is extraordinary: **in November 2025 DeepL hired Martino Cadoni (ex-Klarna) as CFO** and is actively recruiting a Finance Technology leader to own ERP, **billing, and payments** — all classic pre-IPO payment-stack modernization signals. Yuno's angle: single-PSP risk, missing local APMs for 228-market global subscriber base, and subscription retry/decline recovery against documented auto-renewal and chargeback complaints.

### Insight #1 — The CFO Window Is Open
**Evidence**: Martino Cadoni (ex-Klarna) appointed CFO Nov 11, 2025 [S13]. Cadoni ran Klarna Checkout divestment and pre-IPO raise — he speaks payments fluently.
**Pain point**: Pre-IPO DeepL needs audit-grade billing, acceptance-rate analytics, and PSP redundancy. Single-Stripe exposure is a material disclosure risk.
**Yuno value prop**: Orchestration layer + real-time monitors (Rappi-style ms detection) + smart routing (+7% approval uplift) — all directly addressable at the CFO level.
**Best case**: Rappi (zero implementation time, 80% less analyst resolution) + InDrive (10 LATAM markets in <8 months, 90% approval).
**Outreach angle**: "Martino — I saw your announcement. I spent the last 3 years helping CFOs at InDrive and Rappi harden the payment stack ahead of scale events. One question: on the DeepL IPO timeline, is single-PSP a stated risk factor?"

### Insight #2 — Japan Is #1 Desktop Audience, Stripe Is Single PSP
**Evidence**: SimilarWeb Dec 2025 shows Japan as #1 desktop country [S1]. DeepL Help Center confirms Stripe as sole PSP [S6]. Japan's local payment ecosystem (JCB, Konbini, PayPay) is notoriously underserved by EU-centric Stripe setups.
**Pain point**: Lost conversion in DeepL's largest desktop market from mismatched APM coverage and cross-border decline rates.
**Yuno value prop**: Local Japanese acquiring + Konbini/JCB/PayPay routing, new market live "in weeks."
**Best case**: InDrive's 10-market LATAM rollout in <8 months at 90% approval.
**Outreach angle**: "Japan is your #1 desktop audience — and your only PSP is based in Dublin. Worth a 20-min convo?"

### Insight #3 — Finance Tech Job Req Says "Billing and Payments" Out Loud
**Evidence**: Active Ashby posting for Finance Technology leader owning ERP, **billing, payments**, procurement, FP&A; "cloud-native, AI-enabled finance stack with automation and governance" [S18].
**Pain point**: DeepL is literally hiring to rebuild the payments layer. This is a once-per-company moment.
**Yuno value prop**: Be the orchestration layer this person builds around instead of hardcoding to a single PSP.
**Outreach angle**: Get in front of the hiring manager (likely CFO Cadoni or VP Finance) before the req closes.

### Insight #4 — Subscription Recovery Against Documented Complaints
**Evidence**: Multiple complaint aggregator threads on double charges, silent auto-renewal, and post-cancel billing [S8–S10]. "DEEPL-AI" has enough chargeback volume to support a dedicated dispute landing page [S12].
**Pain point**: Involuntary churn + chargeback leakage on a 200K+ subscriber base at €8–$34+/mo.
**Yuno value prop**: Smart retries, account updater, 3DS routing — Livelo hit **+5% approval, 50% transaction recovery**.
**Outreach angle**: "If even 2% of your 200K subs fail a renewal each month, that's 4K at-risk customers a month — Livelo recovered 50% of those."

### Insight #5 — 228 Markets, One Payment Processor
**Evidence**: DeepL serves 200K+ customers across 228 markets [S15]. Stripe is the only named PSP [S6].
**Pain point**: Zero redundancy, suboptimal local acceptance, and a de-facto cross-border architecture from a German SE entity.
**Yuno value prop**: One API → 1,000+ payment methods and multi-PSP failover without a PCI rescope.
