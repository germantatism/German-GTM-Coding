## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Kabam is a mobile gaming company (wholly owned by Netmarble since 2017 for $800M) generating an estimated $150–250M annually, primarily through Marvel Contest of Champions. Their payment infrastructure is fragmented across four intermediaries: **Xsolla** (web store), Apple (iOS IAP), Google (Android IAP), and Steam (PC since June 2025) — with no unified orchestration layer. The key Yuno opportunity lies in the web store channel where Kabam has direct billing control via Xsolla but faces documented payment reliability issues, plus the broader reconciliation complexity of managing revenue across four platforms in 180+ countries.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights & Outreach Angles

**Insight #1: Four-Way Payment Fragmentation with No Orchestration**
**Evidence**: Section 3 — Revenue flows through Xsolla, Apple, Google, and Steam with no unified layer
**Pain Point**: Reconciling revenue, managing disputes, and optimizing payment performance across four separate platforms creates operational overhead and blind spots
**Yuno Value Prop**: Single API to unify all payment channels, real-time monitoring across all platforms, unified reconciliation (Rappi reduced analyst resolution time by 80%)
**Best Success Case**: Rappi — zero implementation time for new providers, 80% reduction in analyst resolution time
**Outreach Angle**: "Managing payments across Xsolla, Apple, Google, and Steam — are you able to get a unified view of approval rates and failed transactions across all four channels?"

**Insight #2: Documented Xsolla Bank Card Acceptance Failures**
**Evidence**: Section 5 & 7 — Forum threads documenting Xsolla bank card acceptance issues on the web store, with players unable to complete purchases
**Pain Point**: Direct revenue loss on the only channel where Kabam controls the billing experience; players forced to use alternative methods or abandon purchases
**Yuno Value Prop**: Smart Routing across multiple PSPs to maximize approval rates; transaction recovery for failed payments (up to 50% recovery rate)
**Best Success Case**: InDrive — 90% approval rate, 4.5%+ recovery rate across 10 LATAM markets
**Outreach Angle**: "I noticed some MCOC players have reported bank card acceptance issues on your web store — with smart routing you could automatically retry failed transactions through alternative processors."

**Insight #3: Web Store is the D2C Lifeline — But Relies on a Single Payment Provider**
**Evidence**: Section 3A — Web store runs exclusively on Xsolla with no backup PSP or orchestration
**Pain Point**: Single-PSP dependency on the only channel where Kabam avoids 15–30% app store fees. Any Xsolla outage or processing issue directly impacts the web store's revenue
**Yuno Value Prop**: Multi-PSP orchestration eliminates single-provider dependency; no-code PSP enablement means adding backup processors in weeks, not months
**Best Success Case**: InDrive — scaled to 10 markets in <8 months with multi-PSP orchestration
**Outreach Angle**: "Your web store is a smart move to capture more margin — have you considered adding PSP redundancy beyond Xsolla to protect against processing issues?"

**Insight #4: Punitive Refund System Signals Chargeback Pain**
**Evidence**: Section 5 — "Contest Credits" negative balance system, 4% support resolution rate, account bans after refunds
**Pain Point**: The aggressive anti-refund measures suggest high chargeback rates and dispute costs. Players going through Apple/Google for refunds instead of Kabam creates adversarial dynamics
**Yuno Value Prop**: Unified dispute management and chargeback prevention tools; better authorization-level data to reduce false declines and legitimate refund needs
**Best Success Case**: Rappi — real-time anomaly detection vs. 5–10 min manual resolution
**Outreach Angle**: "Many gaming companies we work with have reduced chargebacks significantly by improving authorization rates and providing better transaction visibility to support teams."

**Insight #5: Netmarble Parent = Land-and-Expand Opportunity**
**Evidence**: Section 2 & 6 — Kabam is wholly owned by Netmarble (Seoul), one of the largest mobile gaming companies globally
**Pain Point**: Netmarble's portfolio likely faces similar multi-platform payment fragmentation across all their titles
**Yuno Value Prop**: Enterprise-grade orchestration that can scale across an entire gaming portfolio; proven with large multi-market companies
**Best Success Case**: InDrive — scaled across 10+ markets rapidly
**Outreach Angle**: Position Kabam as a pilot that could expand to the broader Netmarble portfolio
