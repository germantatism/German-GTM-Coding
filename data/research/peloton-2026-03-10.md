## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Peloton Interactive (NASDAQ: PTON) is a connected fitness platform with ~$2.5B annual revenue, 2.88M paid hardware subscribers, and operations in 6 markets (US, UK, Canada, Germany, Austria, Australia). Their payment stack is fragmented — different BNPL providers per market (Affirm in US/Canada, Klarna in UK/Germany, Zip Money in Australia) with no evidence of a payment orchestration layer. With international expansion planned for 2026 via Precor's 60-country footprint, a $100M cost reduction mandate, and high-AOV hardware ($1,445–$5,995) plus recurring subscriptions ($44/month), Peloton presents a strong Yuno opportunity around smart routing, involuntary churn reduction, and unified payment infrastructure for multi-market scale.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights & Outreach Angles

**Insight #1: Fragmented BNPL/Financing Stack Across 5 Markets**
**Evidence**: Section 3A — Affirm (US, Canada), Klarna (US, UK, Germany), Citizens Pay (US), Zip Money (Australia)
**Pain Point**: Managing 4+ separate BNPL integrations across markets creates operational overhead, inconsistent customer experience, and limited ability to optimize conversion rates across providers.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Unified Checkout Builder enables single integration to orchestrate all BNPL/financing providers, with smart routing to the highest-converting option per market.
**Best Success Case**: InDrive — 10 LATAM markets unified through a single integration, reducing operational complexity while improving approval rates.
**Outreach Angle**: "Managing Affirm, Klarna, Citizens Pay, and Zip Money across 5 markets — a single orchestration layer could simplify operations and optimize conversion per region."

**Insight #2: International Expansion Without Payment Infrastructure**
**Evidence**: Section 6 — CEO signals new markets in 2026, leveraging Precor's 60-country footprint + 3PL partnerships.
**Pain Point**: Each new market requires local PSP integrations, APM support, and regulatory compliance. Without orchestration, this means separate integrations per country.
**Yuno Value Prop**: New market live in weeks with no-code PSP enablement. 1,000+ payment methods across 200+ countries via single API.
**Best Success Case**: InDrive — launched in 10 LATAM markets in under 8 months through Yuno's orchestration.
**Outreach Angle**: "As Peloton leverages Precor's global footprint for new markets in 2026, a payment orchestration layer could enable local payment methods without building country-by-country integrations."

**Insight #3: Involuntary Churn on 2.88M Recurring Subscribers**
**Evidence**: Section 5 — billing complaints; Section 12 — 2.88M subscribers at $44/month = ~$1.5B/year in subscription revenue.
**Pain Point**: Even 1% involuntary churn on 2.88M subscribers = ~$15M/year lost revenue. Standard retry logic misses recovery opportunities.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Up to 50% transaction recovery through intelligent retry + smart routing across multiple PSPs. Rappi case: 80% reduction in analyst resolution time for payment anomalies.
**Best Success Case**: Livelo — +5% approval rate, 50% transaction recovery.
**Outreach Angle**: "With 2.88M monthly renewals, even a 2% improvement in retry success translates to significant recovered revenue."

**Insight #4: $100M Cost Reduction Mandate + $65M Tariff Headwind**
**Evidence**: Section 6 — FY2026 targets $100M in cost savings; FCF guidance reduced by $65M due to tariffs.
**Pain Point**: Payment processing fees on ~$2.5B in revenue represent a significant cost line. Without smart routing, Peloton may be overpaying on interchange and processing fees.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Smart routing optimizes for cost and approval rate simultaneously, reducing processing costs while improving authorization rates.
**Best Success Case**: Reserva — +4% approval rate in under 3 months, directly improving unit economics.
**Outreach Angle**: "With a $100M cost savings target for FY2026, optimizing payment routing across your $2.5B in transactions could meaningfully contribute to that goal."

**Insight #5: No Known Payment Orchestrator — Greenfield Vertical**
**Evidence**: Section 3B — no orchestrator signals; Section 11C — no connected fitness company has publicly adopted orchestration.
**Pain Point**: The connected fitness vertical is entirely greenfield for payment orchestration. Peloton's dual-model (high-AOV hardware + recurring subscriptions) is precisely the complexity that orchestration solves.
**Yuno Value Prop**: First-mover in vertical. Single API replaces fragmented PSP/BNPL integrations. Real-time monitoring catches anomalies in milliseconds vs. minutes.
**Best Success Case**: Rappi — zero implementation time for new providers, real-time anomaly detection.
**Outreach Angle**: "Connected fitness is moving toward global scale, but no major player has unified their payment infrastructure yet. There's an opportunity to lead."
