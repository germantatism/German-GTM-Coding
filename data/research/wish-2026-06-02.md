# Wish (wish.com / ContextLogic) — SDR Research Brief
*Yuno Payment Orchestrator | Date: 2026-06-02 | Framework v8.0*

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Wish (wish.com) is a mobile-first global discount marketplace that once peaked at ~$2.87B revenue and ~90M MAUs (2021), then collapsed ~99% and was sold by ContextLogic to Singapore's **Qoo10 for ~$173M in April 2024**. The most important payment finding for Yuno: Wish runs a **confirmed multi-PSP stack — Stripe (primary card processor), Adyen, Braintree/PayPal, and Ebanx (LATAM)** — with **no third-party orchestrator detected**, and went so far as to obtain its **own EU Payment Institution license** (ContextLogic B.V., DNB-regulated, 2021) explicitly to "control the payments chain" and "reduce reliance on third-party processors." That is a textbook DIY-orchestration posture. **The major caveat: Wish is a distressed asset.** Its parent Qoo10 fell into insolvency in late 2024 (TMON/WeMakePrice defaulted on ~$152M in seller payouts; Singapore's MAS suspended Qoo10's payment services). Net: payments-architecture fit is high, but commercial health and decision-making stability are low — treat as a **🟡 watch/educational target**, not an active high-priority pursuit.

---

## ⭐ DIRECT ANSWER — Does Wish have payment orchestration?

**No named third-party orchestrator (Spreedly, Primer, Gr4vy, CellPoint, APEXX, Yuno) was found.** What Wish has instead is a **self-built multi-PSP orchestration posture**:

- **4+ card/wallet PSPs run in parallel** — Stripe, Adyen, Braintree, PayPal (per Wish's own Payment Method FAQ).
- **Ebanx** handles LATAM rails (cards, Boleto, OXXO, Pix).
- **Own EU Payment Institution license** via ContextLogic B.V. (De Nederlandsche Bank, July 2021) to act as its own merchant-payout entity in Europe and "reduce reliance on third-party processors."

So Wish is effectively orchestrating in-house across multiple processors and rails. That is exactly the workload Yuno productizes — but the in-house build plus the company's distressed state means the buying conversation is unusual (cost-out / consolidation, not greenfield growth).

**Analysis:** Wish openly documents a **naive retry mechanism** — failed first capture triggers a 2nd attempt, producing duplicate auths it says "self-clear in ~10 business days." That is a textbook smart-retry / orchestration use case (idempotency + intelligent retries). Debit-as-credit routing constraints and decline friction point to a routing-optimization gap. Yuno's smart routing, idempotent retries, and real-time monitors (Rappi: ms detection vs. 5–10 min manual) map directly.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Multi-PSP stack with no orchestrator = the core fit
**Evidence:** §3A — Stripe + Adyen + Braintree + PayPal + Ebanx confirmed; §3B — no third-party orchestrator.
**Pain Point:** Routing, fallback, and reconciliation logic spread across 5 processors + a self-built EU payment entity.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single API in front of all PSPs/APMs with smart routing and unified reconciliation.
**Best Case:** InDrive (10 LATAM markets <8 months, 90% approval).
**Outreach Angle:** "You're running Stripe, Adyen, Braintree and Ebanx in parallel and built your own EU payment entity to control the chain. A single orchestration layer is usually the cheaper way to keep that control without maintaining five integrations."

### Insight #2: Self-admitted retry → duplicate charges
**Evidence:** §5 — Wish documents a 2nd capture attempt creating duplicate auths.
**Pain Point:** Naive retries, no idempotency, customer-facing duplicate-charge complaints.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Idempotent smart retries + real-time monitors.
**Best Case:** Rappi (ms detection vs. 5–10 min manual, 80% less analyst resolution).

### Insight #3: LATAM rails already live via a single regional PSP
**Evidence:** §4A — Boleto, Pix, OXXO all through Ebanx.
**Pain Point:** Single-PSP dependency on Ebanx for all of LATAM = no failover, no local-acquirer optimization.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Multi-acquirer routing in LATAM; keep Ebanx, add local acquirers where they price/approve better.
**Best Case:** Livelo (+5% approval, 50% recovery).

### Insight #4: Distressed parent = the disqualifier (read carefully)
**Evidence:** §6 — Qoo10 insolvency, $152M seller-payout default, MAS payment-services suspension.
**Pain Point (for us):** Budget, decision-makers, and even payment-licensing are unstable.
**Implication:** High architectural fit, low commercial readiness. Pursue only as an educational/whitespace conversation, or pivot to the healthier competitor set below.
