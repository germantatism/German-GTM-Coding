# Crocs — Yuno SDR Research Brief
**Date:** 2026-04-10 | **Parent:** Crocs, Inc. (NYSE: CROX) | **Brands:** Crocs + HEYDUDE

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Crocs, Inc. is a $4.04B global footwear company (FY2025) with DTC >50% of revenue and international growing +11.9% YoY led by APAC/China. The Crocs payment stack is **fragmented across multiple PSPs by region** — Adyen historically consolidates EU/APAC card processing per Adyen's own case study, but **live storefront verification confirms Braintree on US + Japan, Pagaleve handling Pix in Brazil, and Mercado Pago in Mexico**. There is **no public evidence of an orchestration layer**, and the recent appointment of CFO Patraic Reagan (ex-Nike CFO APAC/LatAm, Sept 2025) creates a classic "new CFO infra-review" trigger. Strongest Yuno wedge: unify the multi-PSP regional patchwork under one orchestration layer with smart routing, retries, and LATAM/APAC APM expansion.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Multi-PSP fragmentation without orchestration
**Evidence:** S3 — Adyen (EU/APAC), Braintree (US/JP), Pagaleve (BR), Mercado Pago (MX) all confirmed in parallel | **Pain:** No smart routing, no failover, no consolidated reporting, multiple integrations to maintain | **Yuno value:** Single integration above all regional PSPs, smart routing for +7% approval uplift | **Best case:** InDrive — 10 LATAM markets <8 months, 90% approval | **Angle:** "We noticed Crocs runs Adyen in Europe, Braintree in the US/Japan, and Pagaleve in Brazil — each integration is a separate pipeline. One layer above means 7% lift in approvals and weeks-not-quarters market launches."

### Insight #2: New CFO from Nike — infra review window
**Evidence:** S6 — Patraic Reagan, EVP & CFO since Sept 2025, ex-Nike CFO APAC/LatAm | **Pain:** New CFOs scrutinize payment costs, MDR, and chargeback leakage in first 6 months | **Yuno value:** Cost optimization through routing + 50% transaction recovery | **Best case:** Livelo (+5% approval, 50% recovery) | **Angle:** "Patraic ran Nike's APAC/LatAm books — he knows what local-acquirer routing is worth versus cross-border. Worth a 20-min comparison vs. your current setup."

### Insight #3: Documented checkout-failure-after-auth pattern
**Evidence:** S5 — Trustpilot recurring complaints "payment goes through, cart empty, logged out" + Apple Pay address overwrite | **Pain:** Lost sales after successful auth, chargeback escalation, brand damage | **Yuno value:** Smart retry, network tokens, unified wallet abstraction | **Best case:** Rappi (ms-level monitor detection) | **Angle:** "Your Trustpilot has a recurring 'paid but empty cart' pattern. That's a token-handoff issue Yuno's retry layer fixes without touching your SFCC build."

### Insight #4: HEYDUDE on Shopify = standalone entry point
**Evidence:** S6 — VP Digital Commerce HEYDUDE LinkedIn JD lists Shopify | **Pain:** HEYDUDE in turnaround, separate brand president, separate stack from Crocs SFCC | **Yuno value:** Shopify integration, faster wedge into the org via the smaller brand | **Angle:** Lead with HEYDUDE's Rupert Campbell or VP Digital Commerce — easier first conversation than corporate Crocs.

### Insight #5: APAC growth +11.9% but APM gaps unverified
**Evidence:** S1 + S6 — international DTC fastest-growing segment, China/APAC leading | **Pain:** Likely missing local APMs (Alipay, WeChat Pay, KakaoPay, PayPay, Konbini, UPI on crocs.in not verified) | **Yuno value:** Local APM coverage + local acquirer routing | **Angle:** "International grew 11.9% — what's your APM coverage in China and India today? Yuno launches new APMs without engineering tickets."
