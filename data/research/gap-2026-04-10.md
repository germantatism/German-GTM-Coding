# Gap Inc. — SDR Research Brief
*Yuno Payment Orchestrator | Date: 2026-04-10*

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Gap Inc. (NYSE: GAP) is a ~$15B global apparel holding company (Gap, Old Navy, Banana Republic, Athleta) operating 2,474 company-owned stores and ~1,000 franchise locations across 35+ countries. In the last 12 months, Gap has aggressively modernized its payments stack — adding Klarna (Sept 2025) on top of existing Afterpay and PayPal Pay Later, signing a multi-year Barclays/Mastercard co-brand credit card deal, and naming Kevin Meiners as Head of Loyalty & Payments. The company is now running **3 BNPL rails simultaneously** without any public evidence of a payment orchestrator — a textbook routing/reconciliation pain point. Combined with a CEO-driven digital transformation (new CTO Sven Gerjets, Google Cloud + Gemini partnership), an active beauty + DoorDash expansion creating new payment flows, and verified refund-friction complaints, Gap is a 🔴 **High-priority Yuno target**.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Three BNPL rails, no orchestrator
**Evidence**: §3A + §7 — Gap runs Klarna (Sept 2025) + Afterpay + PayPal Pay Later in the US simultaneously, with no public orchestrator disclosed.
**Pain Point**: Routing logic, fallback when one BNPL declines, conversion attribution across 3 providers, fragmented reconciliation.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Smart Routing routes BNPLs by basket size, geo, and approval probability — single reconciliation layer.
**Best Case**: InDrive (10 LATAM markets <8 months, 90% approval, 4.5% recovery)
**Outreach Angle**: "You just added Klarna on top of Afterpay and PayPal Pay Later — how are you handling routing and attribution across 3 BNPL rails? Our merchants typically see 7% approval uplift once those rails sit behind a single orchestration layer."

### Insight #2: Refund friction is global, not regional
**Evidence**: §5 — Trustpilot complaints span US, UK, EU, Canada with the same theme: 8+ week refund delays, unexplained cancellations, forced chargebacks.
**Pain Point**: Fragmented post-purchase ops across PSPs and BNPLs.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Unified dispute/refund layer + real-time monitors (Rappi: ms detection vs. 5–10 min manual).
**Best Case**: Rappi (zero implementation time, 80% less analyst resolution)
**Outreach Angle**: "Refund delays are showing up across every Gap region on Trustpilot — usually a sign post-purchase ops are split per PSP. Want to see how Rappi cut analyst resolution time by 80%?"

### Insight #3: Active digital transformation = budget is live
**Evidence**: §6 — New CTO (Sven Gerjets), Google Cloud + Gemini deal, beauty launch, DoorDash partnership, Q4 FY25 explicitly framing payments as a growth lever.
**Pain Point**: New flows (beauty, same-day delivery) demand new fraud surface and new routing logic.
**Yuno Value Prop**: New market / new flow live in weeks, no-code PSP enablement.
**Outreach Angle**: "With beauty launching across Old Navy and DoorDash same-day live, every new flow opens fraud and routing surface. Worth a 20-min look at how we ship new flows in weeks?"

### Insight #4: UK/EU operated by a partner (Next plc) — no Gap-side payment control
**Evidence**: §2 — Gap.co.uk fully migrated to Next plc's platform Aug 2022.
**Pain Point**: Gap can't control approval rates or APM mix in its 2nd-largest historical market.
**Yuno Value Prop**: As Gap re-evaluates international, Yuno enables direct re-entry without owning every PSP integration per country.
**Outreach Angle**: Position for international re-entry conversations.

### Insight #5: Buyer persona is named and public
**Evidence**: §7 — Kevin Meiners, Head of Loyalty & Payments, publicly quoted on Klarna deal.
**Pain Point**: N/A — this is access.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Direct mapping for outbound.
**Outreach Angle**: Direct LinkedIn + email to Kevin Meiners with insights #1 + #2.
