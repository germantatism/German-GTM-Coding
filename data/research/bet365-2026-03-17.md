## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

bet365 is the world's largest private online gambling company (GBP 4.0B revenue, FY2024-25), headquartered in Stoke-on-Trent, UK, and operating in 20+ regulated markets globally. The company manages a heavily regionalized payment stack across the US, UK, Australia, Brazil, India, and Europe with 29+ unique APMs — yet shows no evidence of a third-party payment orchestration platform, likely handling PSP complexity in-house at significant cost. With 9+ new market launches in the last 12 months (6 US states + Brazil, Peru, Serbia), an active AVP Payments hire in Denver ($175K-$250K), and profit margins squeezed 44% by expansion costs ($1.2B in direct costs), bet365 is a high-priority Yuno prospect facing exactly the kind of multi-market payment complexity that orchestration solves.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights & Outreach Angles

**Insight #1: 9+ New Markets in 12 Months with No Orchestration Layer**
Evidence: Section 6 — 6 US states + Brazil, Peru, Serbia, Argentina launched in 2024-2025. France planned for 2026.
Pain Point: Each new market requires new PSP integrations, local payment method setups, regulatory compliance. Direct costs jumped to $1.2B (Section 7). Without orchestration, each integration is bespoke.
Yuno Value Prop: Single API to enable new markets in weeks. No-code PSP enablement means bet365 could cut integration time and cost for France, remaining LATAM, and future markets.
Best Success Case: InDrive — 10 LATAM markets in <8 months via Yuno's single integration.
Outreach Angle: "bet365 launched in 9+ markets last year and is targeting France next. InDrive used Yuno to go live in 10 LATAM markets in under 8 months through one integration."

**Insight #2: Profit Squeeze from Expansion Costs — Payment Optimization is a Lever**
Evidence: Section 7 — FY2024-25 profit dropped 44% (GBP 349M from GBP 627M) while revenue grew 9%. Direct costs hit $1.2B.
Pain Point: Revenue is growing but profitability is being consumed by market-by-market infrastructure buildout. Payment costs are a significant portion of direct costs.
Yuno Value Prop: Smart Routing can uplift approval rates by up to +7%, and 50% transaction recovery reduces lost revenue. Consolidated PSP management reduces operational cost.
Best Success Case: Livelo — +5% approval rate, 50% transaction recovery.
Outreach Angle: "With profit margins tightening from expansion costs, even a 3-5% approval rate improvement across GBP 4B in volume translates to significant recovered revenue."

**Insight #3: Payment Reliability Issues Under Peak Traffic**
Evidence: Section 5 — Champions League cash-out failures (Jan 2025), widespread withdrawal complaints on Trustpilot and GPWA.
Pain Point: Payment infrastructure failed during peak demand, directly impacting customer experience and revenue. Wire transfer failures leave funds stuck in limbo.
Yuno Value Prop: Real-time monitoring (Rappi: anomaly detection in milliseconds vs. 5-10 min manually) + automatic failover routing to backup PSPs when primary PSP degrades.
Best Success Case: Rappi — zero implementation time for new providers, 80% reduction in analyst resolution time, real-time anomaly detection.
Outreach Angle: "The Champions League cash-out outage in January is exactly the kind of issue Rappi solved with Yuno's real-time monitoring — anomaly detection in milliseconds instead of minutes."

**Insight #4: Active Payments Leadership Hire = Stack Under Review**
Evidence: Section 3 — AVP, Payments (US) hiring in Denver at $175K-$250K, role involves managing PSP relationships and evaluating emerging payment technologies.
Pain Point: bet365 is actively investing in payments capability for North America, suggesting the current setup is insufficient for their growth trajectory.
Yuno Value Prop: Yuno provides the payments infrastructure layer that a new AVP Payments would need — unified PSP management, routing intelligence, and analytics — without building it in-house.
Best Success Case: InDrive — scaled from single-market to 10+ markets through Yuno's orchestration layer.
Outreach Angle: "Saw you're building out the payments team in Denver. The companies we work with — like InDrive and Rappi — found that orchestration gives their payments leaders the control they need without the build time."

**Insight #5: 29+ APMs Across 20+ Markets with No Unified Layer**
Evidence: Section 4 — Confirmed APMs range from Venmo/PayNearMe (US) to PIX (Brazil) to UPI/PhonePe (India) to Trustly/Pay by Bank App (UK).
Pain Point: Managing 29+ payment methods across regionalized stacks without orchestration creates operational complexity, reconciliation challenges, and inconsistent customer experience.
Yuno Value Prop: Unified Checkout Builder adapts per region, single dashboard for all PSPs and APMs, consolidated reconciliation.
Best Success Case: InDrive — single API, multi-market APM management across LATAM.
Outreach Angle: "Managing PIX in Brazil, UPI in India, Trustly in the UK, and PayNearMe in the US through separate integrations is exactly the complexity payment orchestration was built to solve."
