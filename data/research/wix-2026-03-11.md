## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Wix.com Ltd. (NASDAQ: WIX) is an Israeli-headquartered, publicly traded website-building and commerce platform with $1.76B in FY2024 revenue and 260M+ registered users globally. Critically, Wix is not merely a merchant — it operates **Wix Payments**, its own proprietary PSP layer, built on top of Stripe and PayPal as underlying processors, serving millions of merchants across 15 countries. This is a **multi-PSP-without-orchestration architecture**: two confirmed PSP dependencies (Stripe + PayPal) with no public evidence of smart routing or a vendor-neutral orchestration layer. Wix is actively expanding Wix Payments geographically (11 new European markets, Dec 2025) and into financial services (Wix Capital, Wix Checking), making cross-border PSP complexity a fast-growing operational challenge. The primary Yuno opportunity is at the **platform level** — helping Wix orchestrate its own Stripe/PayPal stack to improve approval rates, unify reconciliation, and accelerate market expansion for the benefit of their millions of downstream merchants.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights & Outreach Angles

**Insight #1: Multi-PSP Without Orchestration — The Core Opportunity**
**Evidence**: Section 3B — Stripe + PayPal confirmed as underlying processors under Wix Payments umbrella; no third-party orchestrator detected. Wix co-heads of Payments explicitly described their goal as "gaining more control over the user experience" by bringing PayPal under the Wix Payments brand.
**Pain Point**: Wix has two PSP dependencies (Stripe + PayPal) with no confirmed smart routing layer. As they expand to 11+ new markets, each new PSP integration adds engineering overhead, reconciliation complexity, and approval rate variability across their millions of downstream merchants.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Single orchestration layer sitting above Stripe and PayPal — smart routing, unified reconciliation dashboard, approval rate uplift across all Wix merchant transactions globally.
**Best Success Case**: Rappi — zero implementation time for new providers, 80% reduction in analyst resolution time. Rappi, like Wix, is a platform aggregating transactions from millions of end users and needed to centralize their PSP visibility.
**Outreach Angle**: "You've brought PayPal and Stripe under the Wix Payments umbrella — the next step is routing between them intelligently. Platforms like Rappi added an orchestration layer above their PSPs and cut reconciliation time by 80%."

---

**Insight #2: 11 New European Markets — Cross-Border PSP Complexity Just Spiked**
**Evidence**: Section 6 — Wix Payments expanded to 11 European countries in Dec 2025 via Stripe. Further EMEA and APAC expansion planned.
**Pain Point**: Each new market adds local regulatory requirements, acquiring costs, and potential need for local acquiring vs. cross-border — complexity that grows non-linearly without orchestration.
**Yuno Value Prop**: New market live in weeks with no-code PSP enablement; Unified Checkout Builder per region; 190+ countries covered.
**Best Success Case**: InDrive — 10 LATAM markets in under 8 months, 90% approval rate maintained throughout expansion.
**Outreach Angle**: "With 11 new European markets live, the cost of cross-border acquiring vs. local acquiring is now a meaningful P&L line. Orchestration determines which PSP routes each transaction based on BIN, cost, and approval probability."

---

**Insight #3: Subscription Billing APM Gap — Revenue Leak on Recurring**
**Evidence**: Section 8 — Wix Payments confirmed to support cards only for recurring/subscription billing; no APMs (PIX, iDEAL, wallets) for recurring. Section 5 — Trustpilot dominated by auto-renewal complaints and refund friction.
**Pain Point**: Wix has millions of subscribers paying for its own plans (plus merchant stores with subscription products). APM-only users in Brazil (PIX), Netherlands (iDEAL), and other markets cannot use recurring billing natively — forcing card-only flows with higher churn and authorization failure rates.
**Yuno Value Prop**: 50% transaction recovery; subscription management supporting APMs in recurring flows; real-time anomaly detection for failed recurring transactions.
**Best Success Case**: Livelo — +5% approval rate, 50% transaction recovery. Recurring points/subscription platform with similar APM complexity.
**Outreach Angle**: "In markets like Brazil and the Netherlands, your subscription flows are likely running card-only — which means every PIX or iDEAL user is a forced card fallback with higher decline rates."

---

**Insight #4: India as High-Traffic Market with No Native Payments**
**Evidence**: Section 1 — India is #3 in traffic at 5.97%; Section 2 — Indian entity confirmed (SEC 20-F); Section 3 — No native Wix Payments in India, only Razorpay/PayU/Cashfree/Paytm via manual merchant integration.
**Pain Point**: Wix has significant user penetration in India but zero native payment infrastructure. Indian merchants building on Wix face a fragmented, manually-configured PSP experience, with no Wix Payments consolidation.
**Yuno Value Prop**: APAC expansion capabilities; local acquiring via orchestration; Unified Checkout Builder enabling UPI/NetBanking/wallets through a single API.
**Outreach Angle**: "India is your #3 traffic market globally and one of the fastest-growing e-commerce markets — but Wix Payments doesn't cover it. Orchestration could give your Indian merchant base the same native experience your US and EU merchants get."

---

**Insight #5: PSP Plugin API Signals Orchestration Readiness**
**Evidence**: Section 3A — Wix exposes a Payment Service Provider Service Plugin API, meaning third-party PSPs can integrate directly into the platform.
**Pain Point**: Wix is already thinking in multi-PSP terms for its merchant base, but this is a bottom-up, merchant-driven model — not a platform-level smart routing layer. The architecture is set up for orchestration but hasn't been unified.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Yuno's API plugs directly above the PSP layer that Wix already has, adding intelligence (routing, monitoring, recovery) without replacing existing integrations.
**Outreach Angle**: "You've already built the multi-PSP infrastructure — PSP Plugin API, Stripe, PayPal, 60+ third-party providers. Orchestration is the intelligence layer that optimizes routing across everything you've already connected."
