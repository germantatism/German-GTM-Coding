# Costco Wholesale — Yuno SDR Research Brief
**Date:** 2026-04-08 | **Analyst:** Claude (Payments Intelligence)

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Costco Wholesale is a $270B Fortune 11 membership warehouse club operating **900+ warehouses in 14 countries** with e-commerce in 8 markets. Their payment stack is unusually rigid — **exclusive single-card-brand partnerships per market** (Visa in US, Mastercard in Canada/Japan, Hyundai Card in Korea, Amex in UK) with **no payment orchestrator detected**. The main Yuno opportunity lies in unifying Costco's fragmented, market-by-market payment architecture, adding local APMs (OXXO, SPEI, PayPay, Kakao Pay are all absent), enabling digital wallet checkout online, and providing smart routing/failover across their exclusive card-brand relationships. The **Citi/Visa co-brand expiration in 2026** creates a potential inflection point for payment infrastructure re-evaluation.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Fragmented Card-Brand Exclusivity Creates Orchestration Need
**Evidence:** S4 — Different exclusive card brands per market (Visa US, MC Canada/Japan, Hyundai Korea, Amex UK)
**Pain Point:** Each market requires separate PSP relationships, routing logic, and integration maintenance. No unified payment layer.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single API connecting all markets with smart routing across card brands and local acquirers.
**Best Case:** InDrive expanded to 10 LATAM markets in <8 months with 90% approval rates.
**Outreach Angle:** "Your payment stack changes in every market — Visa here, Mastercard there, Hyundai in Korea. One API could unify routing and boost approvals across all 8 e-commerce markets."

### Insight #2: Zero Local APMs in High-Growth International Markets
**Evidence:** S4 — No OXXO/SPEI in Mexico, no PayPay/konbini in Japan, no Kakao Pay/Naver Pay in Korea. PayPal only in Mexico.
**Pain Point:** Customers in these markets expect local payment methods. Absence means lost conversions.
**Yuno Value Prop:** No-code PSP enablement — add local APMs in weeks without rebuilding each regional stack. 1,000+ payment methods available.
**Best Case:** New market live in weeks with no-code configuration.
**Outreach Angle:** "Mexico, Japan, and Korea are growing markets for Costco, but none of them offer the local payment methods shoppers expect. OXXO in Mexico, PayPay in Japan, Kakao Pay in Korea — these could go live in weeks."

### Insight #3: Refund Friction Points to Brittle Payment Infrastructure
**Evidence:** S5 — 60+ day refund waits, phantom charges, $0 transaction errors, checkout failures.
**Pain Point:** No intelligent failover or real-time monitoring. Payment failures go undetected or unresolved for weeks.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Real-time monitoring (Rappi: ms detection vs. 5–10 min manually), 50% transaction recovery rate.
**Best Case:** Rappi achieved 80% less analyst resolution time with zero implementation overhead.
**Outreach Angle:** "Some of the checkout errors and refund delays your members report online could be caught in milliseconds with real-time payment monitoring — and recovered transactions mean recovered revenue."

### Insight #4: Citi/Visa Co-Brand Expiration = Payment Architecture Inflection
**Evidence:** S7 — Citi/Visa deal expiring 2026. Reports of degraded card benefits. Potential network switch.
**Pain Point:** If Costco switches from Visa to another network, the entire US payment infrastructure must be rebuilt.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Orchestration makes network switches seamless — route to any acquirer/processor without rebuilding integrations.
**Best Case:** No-code PSP enablement means new payment rails go live in weeks, not months.
**Outreach Angle:** "With the Citi/Visa deal up for renewal, having a flexible payment layer means you can switch or add networks without re-platforming."

### Insight #5: BNPL Limited to US, $500+ Only — Leaving Money on the Table
**Evidence:** S4, S7 — Affirm is US-only, online-only, $500–$17,500 minimum. No BNPL in Canada, UK, Mexico, Japan, Korea.
**Pain Point:** Most Costco.com baskets fall below $500, excluding them from flexible payment. International members have no BNPL option at all.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Orchestration enables multiple BNPL providers per market with flexible thresholds — Klarna in UK, Kueski Pay in Mexico, Paidy in Japan.
**Best Case:** Broader BNPL coverage drives higher AOV and conversion across all markets.
**Outreach Angle:** "Affirm covers $500+ US orders, but what about the rest? Local BNPL in each market could unlock conversion for the majority of your basket sizes."
