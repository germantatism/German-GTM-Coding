# SDR Research Brief — Mercor
*Yuno Payment Orchestrator · Framework v8.0 · 2026-07-23*

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Mercor (mercor.com) is a San Francisco AI talent and expert-data marketplace that matches ~300,000 vetted experts to AI labs for data labeling, evaluation and RLHF, billing enterprise customers and paying out to a large global contractor workforce. The money story is a payouts story: Mercor pays **$2M+ per day** to **30,000+ weekly active contractors across 45+ countries**, all on USD-denominated contracts, and it runs a fragmented dual-rail payout stack (**Stripe Connect** as the primary rail plus **Wise** as a manual fallback for countries Stripe Connect does not cover). At a ~$2B annualized run-rate and a valuation that jumped from $2B to $10B in eight months (with a ~$20B round in talks), the payout volume and cross-border corridor complexity are scaling faster than the finance org, which has no named Head of Payments yet. The Yuno wedge is payout orchestration: one layer above Stripe, Wise and local rails to add local payout methods (Pix in Brazil, UPI/IMPS in India, local rails in Nigeria/Turkey/UAE), route each corridor to the best provider, unify FX, and remove the manual Wise-accept friction that stalls international contractor funds.

> Note: this is a PAYOUTS opportunity, not a consumer-checkout one. Every angle below is about paying contractors, not collecting from buyers.

## SECTION 3 — Payment Stack (payouts)

**3A. Payout rails (CONFIRMED, self-disclosed in Mercor's contractor docs)**
| Rail | Role | Notes | Source |
|------|------|-------|--------|
| Stripe Connect (+ Stripe Express, Instant Payouts) | Primary payout rail, all Stripe-supported countries | Standard free (2 to 5 days) or Instant (1% fee, ~30 min); 7-day hold on first payout; KYC/1099 handled inside Stripe | [S3][S4] |
| Wise | Fallback rail only where Stripe Connect does not support payouts | Confirmed Wise-only regions: Brazil, Georgia, Kosovo, UAE, Nepal, Turkey. Contractor must manually accept each transfer by email, or funds stall | [S3][S5] |

- Provider is auto-assigned by bank-account country; the contractor cannot choose.
- Explicitly NOT offered as payout methods (Mercor's own stated position): PayPal, Payoneer, cryptocurrency.
- All contracts denominated in USD; Stripe or Wise converts to local currency at payout (no unified FX control or spread visibility across corridors).
- Weekly cadence (processed by Wednesday PST). Excluded sanctioned countries: Russia, Iran, North Korea, Belarus.
- Stripe partnership announced April 1, 2026 ("Mercor Selects Stripe to Power Global AI Expansion").

**3B. Orchestrator**: No independent payout orchestrator. The stack is a two-vendor split (Stripe Connect + Wise) stitched by geography, which is the orchestration gap, not an existing orchestration layer.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

**Insight #1: Fragmented dual-rail payout stack across 45+ countries.**
Evidence: S3 (Stripe Connect + Wise split by geography). Pain: two disconnected rails, manual Wise acceptance, no best-per-corridor routing. Yuno value: one payout-orchestration API above Stripe, Wise and local providers, routing each corridor to the best rail. Best case: inDrive (10 LATAM markets live in under 8 months). Angle: "You just committed to Stripe for payouts; the next problem is every corridor Stripe Connect does not cover, where you fall back to a manual Wise flow that stalls contractor funds."

**Insight #2: Top contractor markets lack local instant payout methods.**
Evidence: S1 (India, Nigeria, Brazil are top markets), S3 (Brazil is Wise-only; no Pix/UPI/mobile money referenced). Pain: emerging-market contractors get USD converted at payout with no local instant option. Yuno value: add Pix (Brazil), UPI/IMPS (India), local bank/mobile-money rails (Nigeria), improving speed and cutting FX cost. Best case: inDrive / Rappi LATAM. Angle: give your largest contractor markets a local, instant, lower-FX payout.

**Insight #3: FX is fully delegated with no unified control.**
Evidence: S3 (USD converted by Stripe/Wise at payout). Pain: no visibility or control over FX spread across corridors at $2M/day. Yuno value: unified FX and cost visibility across payout providers. Angle: at $2M/day, a few basis points of FX and the 1% instant-payout fee are real money; orchestration gives you control over both.

**Insight #4: Payout volume is outrunning a thin finance org.**
Evidence: S8/S10 ($2B run-rate, $10B to ~$20B valuation), S6 (payout ops complaints), open Strategic Finance Advisor role. Pain: infrastructure and ops maturity lagging scale. Yuno value: a payout control plane with monitoring and reconciliation before the stack ossifies. Angle: good moment to standardize the payout layer while the finance function is being built.
