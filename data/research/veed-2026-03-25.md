# SDR Research Brief — VEED (veed.io)
**Date:** 2026-03-25 | **Analyst:** Yuno Payments Intelligence

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

VEED is a London-based online video editing platform (~$45M ARR, ~200K paying subscribers, 15M monthly visits) with top traffic from India, the US, and Brazil. They run entirely on **Stripe** as their sole PSP — using Billing, Payment Element, Smart Retries, Radar, and Adaptive Acceptance — with no payment orchestration layer detected. The primary Yuno opportunity lies in VEED's single-PSP dependency across a global user base, documented payment complaints (charges after cancellation, refund friction), India's monthly billing restriction due to RBI regulations, and limited APM coverage in high-traffic markets like Brazil and India where local payment methods dominate.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Single-PSP Dependency at Scale
**Evidence:** S3 — Stripe is the sole PSP across all markets. No orchestrator detected.
**Pain Point:** At $45M ARR and 200K subscribers, VEED has zero fallback if Stripe experiences downtime, rate changes, or policy shifts. Single-PSP risk increases with scale.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Payment orchestration with Stripe as primary + backup PSPs. Smart routing for redundancy and improved acceptance. No-code PSP enablement to add alternatives without re-engineering.
**Best Case:** InDrive achieved 90% approval rates across 10 LATAM markets in <8 months with Yuno.
**Outreach Angle:** "With $45M flowing through a single provider, even a 1% improvement in acceptance or a few hours of downtime avoidance translates to hundreds of thousands in revenue impact."

### Insight #2: Cross-Border Revenue Leakage in Top Markets
**Evidence:** S1 + S2 — India (#1 traffic), US (#2), Brazil (#3) have no local entities. All transactions are cross-border from UK.
**Pain Point:** Cross-border acquiring typically carries higher decline rates and interchange fees vs. local acquiring. India already has RBI restrictions forcing annual-only billing.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart Routing to local acquirers in India, US, and Brazil. Local acquiring reduces declines and fees without requiring local entities.
**Best Case:** Reserva achieved +4% approval uplift in <3 months through local routing optimization.
**Outreach Angle:** "Your top 3 markets are all cross-border from the UK — local acquiring in these regions typically unlocks 3-7% approval improvement and lower interchange."

### Insight #3: APM Gap in High-Growth Markets
**Evidence:** S4 — Only cards, PayPal, Apple Pay, Google Pay, iDEAL confirmed. No Pix (Brazil), UPI (India), or LATAM/APAC local methods.
**Pain Point:** India's UPI processes 12B+ monthly transactions; Brazil's Pix handles 40%+ of digital payments. Missing these methods means lost conversions in VEED's #1 and #3 markets.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single integration to enable 300+ APMs including UPI, Pix, Boleto, OXXO, and more. No-code APM enablement per market.
**Best Case:** 20% of VEED's revenue already comes from non-card methods — expanding APM coverage in top markets could significantly increase this.
**Outreach Angle:** "You've already proven that APMs drive 20% of revenue. Expanding local methods like UPI and Pix in your top markets could unlock the next wave of conversions."

### Insight #4: Payment Complaint Pattern → Involuntary Churn
**Evidence:** S5 — Recurring reports of charges after cancellation, 3DS failures, refund friction.
**Pain Point:** Failed payment retries and billing errors erode subscriber trust and increase churn and chargeback risk.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Real-time payment monitoring (like Rappi's ms-level detection), smart retry logic, and multi-PSP routing to reduce false declines.
**Best Case:** Rappi achieved 80% less analyst resolution time with Yuno's real-time monitors. Livelo recovered 50% of failed transactions.
**Outreach Angle:** "Payment complaints show up in public reviews — smart retry and real-time monitoring can turn failed payments into recovered revenue while reducing support load."

### Insight #5: Monetization Maturity Creates Openness
**Evidence:** S6 + S7 — VEED runs ~50 monetization experiments/year, has a dedicated Payments team, and actively invested in Stripe's advanced features (Smart Retries, Adaptive Acceptance, Network Tokens).
**Pain Point:** They've optimized within Stripe's ecosystem but may be hitting diminishing returns from a single-provider optimization ceiling.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Orchestration unlocks optimization beyond any single PSP's capabilities — multi-provider routing, A/B testing across PSPs, and data-driven acceptance optimization.
**Best Case:** VEED already recovered $1.8M with Smart Retries — orchestration could compound these gains with additional routing intelligence.
**Outreach Angle:** "Your payments team clearly thinks about optimization — orchestration is the next lever beyond what any single PSP can offer."
