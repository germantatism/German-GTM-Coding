## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

H&M (Hennes & Mauritz AB) is a Swedish multinational fast-fashion retailer with ~$23B annual revenue, ~4,000 stores across 77 markets, and online operations in 60+ countries — generating ~$6.5–7.0B in online sales. **Adyen** is confirmed as their primary PSP, and **Klarna** is their strategic BNPL partner (H&M invested $20M in Klarna). With active expansion into Brazil (2025), Paraguay, and Venezuela, a new CTO driving technology transformation, and significant payment complaints around error handling and refunds, H&M presents a strong orchestration opportunity — particularly for LATAM local payment enablement, smart routing across 77 markets, and approval rate optimization.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: LATAM Expansion Creates Immediate Payment Complexity
**Evidence:** S6 — Brazil launch (2025), Paraguay (2026), Venezuela (2025). S4B — Brazil payment methods completely unverified.
**Pain Point:** Entering Brazil, Paraguay, and Venezuela requires support for Pix, Boleto, OXXO, local installment cards, and other LATAM-specific methods. Adyen alone may not offer optimal local acquiring rates in all markets.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single API to enable Pix, Boleto, OXXO, and 300+ LATAM payment methods. New market live in weeks with no-code PSP enablement. InDrive launched 10 LATAM markets in <8 months with Yuno.
**Best Case:** InDrive — 10 LATAM markets, <8 months, 90% approval rate, 4.5% transaction recovery.
**Outreach Angle:** "H&M just entered Brazil — Yuno can enable Pix, Boleto, and local cards through a single integration, the same way we helped InDrive go live in 10 LATAM markets in under 8 months."

### Insight #2: Single-PSP Dependency on Adyen Across 77 Markets
**Evidence:** S3 — Adyen confirmed as primary/sole PSP. No orchestration layer detected.
**Pain Point:** Relying on a single PSP across 77 markets means no failover if Adyen experiences outages, no ability to route to cheaper local acquirers, and limited leverage in pricing negotiations.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart Routing across multiple PSPs → +7% approval uplift. 50% transaction recovery on failed payments. Maintain Adyen as primary while adding local acquirers where they perform better.
**Best Case:** Livelo — +5% approval rate, 50% recovery with Yuno's smart routing.
**Outreach Angle:** "With $6.5B+ in online sales flowing through a single PSP, even a 1% approval uplift would mean $65M+ in recovered revenue. Yuno's smart routing does this without replacing your existing stack."

### Insight #3: Severe Payment Complaint Patterns Indicate Monitoring Gaps
**Evidence:** S5 — Trustpilot 1.3/5 stars, recurring RM1 errors, double charges, 15-day refund waits.
**Pain Point:** Vague error codes, duplicate charges from retry behavior, and slow refunds suggest limited real-time payment monitoring and reconciliation.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Real-time payment monitoring (Rappi: millisecond detection vs. 5–10 min manually). Idempotency controls prevent duplicate charges. Automated reconciliation accelerates refunds.
**Best Case:** Rappi — zero implementation time, 80% less analyst resolution time.
**Outreach Angle:** "H&M's Trustpilot reviews highlight recurring payment errors and slow refunds. Yuno's real-time monitoring catches these issues in milliseconds — the same capability that cut Rappi's analyst resolution time by 80%."

### Insight #4: New CTO Signals Technology Transformation Window
**Evidence:** S6 — George Mudie appointed CTO. SEK 11–12B CapEx for tech/supply chain.
**Pain Point:** New CTO typically reviews and modernizes the tech stack. With $1B+ in CapEx and a mandate for digital transformation, payment infrastructure is likely under review.
**Yuno Value Prop:** No-code PSP enablement means payment modernization without heavy engineering lift. Fits a CTO's mandate to modernize while minimizing risk.
**Best Case:** Integration alongside existing Adyen setup with minimal disruption.
**Outreach Angle:** "With a new CTO and $1B+ in tech investment, H&M is clearly modernizing. Yuno layers on top of your existing Adyen setup — no rip-and-replace needed."

### Insight #5: Multi-Brand Portfolio Multiplies Orchestration Value
**Evidence:** S2 — 8 brands (H&M, COS, Weekday, & Other Stories, ARKET, H&M HOME, Monki, Afound) across 77+ markets.
**Pain Point:** Each brand may have different payment configurations, approval rates, and local method requirements — multiplying complexity.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single orchestration layer across all brands, with brand-specific routing rules and unified analytics.
**Outreach Angle:** "Managing payments across 8 brands in 77 markets is complex. Yuno provides a single orchestration layer with brand-specific configurations — one integration, full visibility."
