## ⚠️ VERTICAL-FIT FLAG (READ FIRST)

FeetFinder is an **adult-adjacent creator marketplace** (foot-content monetization). Its confirmed PSP — **Segpay** — is an adult-vertical specialist; mainstream acquirers (Stripe, Adyen, Checkout.com) do not onboard MCC 5967 / adult-content merchants. **Before any outreach, confirm Yuno's commercial appetite for high-risk MCCs in this vertical.** If Yuno does not have adult-friendly acquirer integrations live (e.g., CCBill, Segpay, Epoch, RocketGate, Verotel) the conversation will end on the first qualifying call. Pipeline scoring below is included for completeness, but vertical fit is the gating decision.

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

FeetFinder is a US-headquartered (Mercer Island, WA), founder-led (Patrick Nielson) adult-adjacent creator marketplace launched in 2019; per third-party data it has processed $100M+ in lifetime GMV across ~8M verified users with ~3.4M monthly visits concentrated in the US, UK, Germany, Mexico, and France ([Ecommerce Fastlane](https://ecommercefastlane.com/feet-pics-statistics/), [SimilarWeb](https://www.similarweb.com/website/feetfinder.com/)). Their **only publicly confirmed PSP is Segpay** (announced by FeetFinder's own X account in Feb 2022), with PayPal enabled through that same Segpay partnership and Paxum reportedly handling non-US creator payouts — i.e., **a single-acquirer dependency on a vertical-specialist PSP, with no orchestration layer detected**. The clearest Yuno angle (assuming adult-MCC commercial fit) is single-PSP concentration risk + documented payout/checkout friction across Trustpilot/PissedConsumer + EU + Mexico expansion that exposes the single Segpay rail to APM and local-acquiring gaps.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Single-PSP concentration on an adult-vertical specialist
- **Evidence:** S3 — Segpay is the only confirmed PSP; PayPal runs through Segpay; no orchestrator detected.
- **Pain Point:** A Segpay outage, MCC-policy change, or de-risking event = inbound revenue stops. The OnlyFans Aug-2021 episode is a textbook precedent for how fast card-network pressure can cascade through a single-PSP merchant.
- **Yuno Value Prop:** Multi-acquirer routing with adult-friendly fallback PSPs; instant failover (Rappi-style ms detection); no-code addition of a redundant acquirer.
- **Best Case (with caveat):** InDrive's failover narrative is directionally relevant but is a non-adult case study — DO NOT use it as a vertical match. Lead with the general failover capability, not the case study.
- **Outreach Angle:** "What's your plan if Segpay has a 24-hour incident — does inbound revenue stop?"

### Insight #2: Cross-border-only acquiring in 5 material markets
- **Evidence:** S1 + S2 — UK, DE, MX, FR, CA together = ~20–25% of traffic, all appear served from a US merchant of record via Segpay (US-based facilitator).
- **Pain Point:** Cross-border interchange + FX margin + lower auth rates on US-acquired EU/MX traffic; missing local APMs (SEPA, OXXO Pay, Cartes Bancaires) for buyers who self-route to prepaid Visa as a workaround (per [Rewarble](https://rewarble.com/insight/what-is-feetfinder-and-how-does-it-work)).
- **Yuno Value Prop:** Local acquiring routing + APM enablement in 200+ countries through one API.
- **Best Case:** InDrive went live in 10 LATAM markets in <8 months with 90% approval and 4.5% recovery — closest LATAM acquiring-expansion proof point.
- **Outreach Angle:** "Your top non-US markets (UK, DE, MX, FR) are likely processing cross-border on Segpay — what's the lift if we route locally?"

### Insight #3: Documented checkout friction — stuck loading screens
- **Evidence:** S5 Trustpilot recurring theme of "payment screens stuck on loading."
- **Pain Point:** Each stuck checkout = lost subscription + a refund/chargeback risk + Trustpilot review.
- **Yuno Value Prop:** Real-time monitors (Rappi case — ms detection vs. 5–10 min manually) + automatic retry/failover.
- **Outreach Angle:** "Your Trustpilot has a recurring 'stuck checkout' complaint — that's the exact moment a secondary acquirer earns its keep."

### Insight #4: 50% transaction recovery story on declines
- **Evidence:** Yuno's 50%-recovery and 7% approval-uplift benchmarks vs. an environment where every declined card on Segpay = lost lifetime value (high LTV: $4.99–$14.99/mo subscriptions × retention curve).
- **Pain Point:** Adult-MCC transactions have notoriously elevated decline rates from issuer-side rules; recovery is mechanical revenue.
- **Yuno Value Prop:** Smart routing + retries + intelligent 3DS to lift authorization rates.
- **Outreach Angle:** "On a $100M+ lifetime base, a 5–7 point approval lift compounds into meaningful 8-figure incremental revenue."

### Insight #5 [VERTICAL-FIT QUESTION — surface internally before sending outreach]
- **Evidence:** All four insights above presume Yuno's commercial team is willing to onboard an MCC 5967 / adult-content merchant and has at least one adult-friendly acquirer live in its connector library.
- **Pain Point (Yuno's, not FeetFinder's):** If Yuno does not support adult MCCs, the qualifying call will end inside 10 minutes and damage outbound credibility in this vertical.
- **Action:** Confirm with Yuno commercial leadership BEFORE outreach. If "no," shelve and re-pipeline a non-adult merchant.
