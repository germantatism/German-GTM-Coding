# SDR Research Brief — HBO Max (Warner Bros. Discovery)
*Yuno Payment Orchestrator · Framework v8.0 · 2026-07-23*

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
HBO Max is Warner Bros. Discovery's global DTC streaming service: **140M+ subscribers (Q1 2026)**, guided to ~150M by end of 2026, live in **100+ countries** after a large 2026 Europe/UK rollout. **HBO Max already runs payment orchestration on Spreedly** (confirmed via Spreedly's own Jan 2026 press release + customer roster naming "HBO Max" and "Warner"), so the Yuno angle is **not** "you lack an orchestrator" — it is incremental optimization on top of / as an alternative routing layer to Spreedly: auth-rate uplift on 140M renewals, recovery of failed renewals (a documented dunning gap), and regional acquirer + local-method expansion for the active 2026 EU/UK/LATAM footprint. Main pains: documented **involuntary churn / failed reissued-card retries**, **international ARPU ($3.85) at ~35% of domestic ($11.15)**, and fresh **multi-entity, multi-currency billing complexity** from the WBD split into a standalone "Warner Bros." company that holds HBO Max.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

**Insight #1: Already orchestrated on Spreedly → sell the upgrade, not the category.**
Evidence: S3 (Spreedly roster). Pain: incumbent orchestration but declining ARPU + failed renewals. Yuno value: alternative/parallel routing layer, +7% auth uplift, no-code new acquirers. Best case: NYT / Super.com (orchestrate already → optimize). Angle: "You already orchestrate with Spreedly — the question is whether every renewal on 140M subs is routed to the best-performing acquirer per market, and whether failed renewals are being recovered."

**Insight #2: Involuntary churn / failed reissued-card retries.**
Evidence: S6 (dunning complaints since Mar 2026). Pain: revenue leakage + churn from failed renewals, no visible account-updater/network-tokenization. Yuno value: up to 50% recovery, network tokenization, smart retries. Best case: Livelo (+50% recovery). Angle: recover the renewals Spreedly's current routing drops.

**Insight #3: 2026 EU/UK + LATAM expansion = local-method + acquirer sprawl.**
Evidence: S9, S1 (Poland #3, Brazil #2, Spain #5). Pain: each new market needs local acquiring + APMs (Poland BLIK, Brazil Pix, Spain Bizum, Mexico SPEI/OXXO). Yuno value: one API, 1,000+ methods, new market live in weeks. Best case: inDrive (10 LATAM markets <8 months). Angle: turn on regional acquiring + APMs for the markets they just launched.

**Insight #4: Post-split multi-entity billing.**
Evidence: S2, S8. Pain: WarnerMedia Direct (US/EMEA) + WarnerMedia Direct Latin America LLC + new standalone Warner Bros. entity = fragmented reconciliation. Yuno value: one orchestration + reconciliation layer across entities/currencies. Angle: unify billing as the company stands up as independent "Warner Bros."
