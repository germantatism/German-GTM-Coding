# SDR Research Brief — 1Password (1password.com)
*Yuno Payment Orchestrator — Framework v8.0 | Date: 2026-06-16*

**Target:** 1Password — password manager evolving into Extended Access Management (XAM) / identity security. Operating entity **AgileBits Inc.**, HQ Toronto, Canada (fully remote; staff across Canada/US/Europe). Co-CEO structure: **Jeff Shiner + David Faugno**. ~$400M ARR, IPO-bound (2026–2027). Backed by ICONIQ, Tiger Global, Lightspeed, Accel, plus Ryan Reynolds / Matthew McConaughey.

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

1Password runs a global subscription business (>$400M ARR, 180k business customers + millions of consumer/family users, ~8.5M monthly site visits across US/UK/DE/JP/CA) on a **single confirmed web PSP, Stripe Billing**, plus an Apple App Store rail for mobile, with **no orchestration layer**. It already uses Stripe-native Radar, Card Account Updater and network tokens, so the Yuno wedge is not "you lack tooling" but **cross-PSP redundancy, routing and local payment methods that Stripe-only cannot provide**, exactly as a serial-IPO CFO (Greg Henry) and a dedicated in-house Billing & Payments engineering team prepare the revenue stack for a public listing. The March 2026 price increase, which in Europe forces account-owner re-approval or cancellation, adds a live involuntary-churn and re-authorization risk at the worst possible moment for ARR durability. Score: **🔴 17/15 (High).**

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

**Insight #1: Single-PSP dependency on Stripe (lead)**
Evidence: S3/S7 — Stripe is the only confirmed web processor for 1M+ subscriptions; no orchestrator. Pain Point: single point of failure (an outage or approval dip has no plan B) and no ability to A/B or route across providers for cost/auth leverage. Yuno Value Prop: add a backup acquirer beside Stripe with smart routing and auto-failover, ~7% approval uplift and fee-negotiation leverage, no rip-and-replace. Best Case: Whop (added the redundancy layer they were missing for 24/7 uptime). Outreach Angle: keep Stripe, add resilience and cross-PSP routing before the IPO spotlight.

**Insight #2: Involuntary churn at the March 2026 price hike**
Evidence: S5 — up to 33% increase; EU requires account-owner re-approval or the subscription cancels. Pain Point: forced re-consent + higher prices = a renewal/re-auth cliff that quietly erodes ARR. Yuno Value Prop: cross-PSP smart retries, dunning and consistent 3DS/SCA handling protect renewals (Stripe network tokens only work within Stripe). Best Case: Livelo (+5% approval, recovered ~50% of failed transactions). Outreach Angle: protect renewal revenue through the price transition, when ARR durability matters most.

**Insight #3: Local payment methods for the international base**
Evidence: S1/S4 — ~49% of traffic is non-US (UK, Germany, Japan, Canada); web confirms cards + US-only ACH. Pain Point: card-and-ACH-centric checkout converts below local rails in DE/JP/UK. Yuno Value Prop: one API to 1,000+ methods, activating SEPA, Bacs/Open Banking, Konbini/PayPay as a config change. Best Case: InDrive (10 markets <8 months on one integration). Outreach Angle: lift international conversion as you scale toward $1B ARR.

**Insight #4: Offload the in-house billing build ahead of IPO**
Evidence: S6 — dedicated Billing & Payments eng team hiring; new IPO-veteran CFO + President. Pain Point: building payment processing and global financial compliance in-house is costly eng spend and audit surface right before a listing. Yuno Value Prop: orchestration handles routing, retries, reconciliation and new-market enablement, freeing engineers and tightening payment-cost economics the CFO is now measured on. Outreach Angle: the IPO-prep efficiency story for finance and engineering at once.
