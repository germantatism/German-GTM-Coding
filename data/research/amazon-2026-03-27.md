# Amazon — SDR Research Brief (Yuno Payment Orchestrator)
**Date:** 2026-03-27 | **Framework:** v8.0

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Amazon (NASDAQ: AMZN) is the world's largest e-commerce company with $716.9B in FY2025 revenue, operating 23 marketplace websites across 20+ countries. Amazon runs a **fully proprietary payment stack** — processing cards via Chase Paymentech, operating Amazon Pay as its own merchant-facing PSP, and building its own AI-based "Cognitive Payments Director" on AWS Bedrock. **Amazon is not a Yuno prospect for core checkout orchestration.** However, Amazon's 3P marketplace sellers (~$300B GMV) who also sell on other platforms represent an indirect opportunity, and its competitor/peer set (particularly Coupang, SHEIN, and Temu) includes companies that may lack payment orchestration. Amazon Payment Services in MENA also positions Amazon as a **competitor** to Yuno in that region.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Amazon Is Its Own Orchestrator — Not a Direct Prospect
**Evidence:** S3 — Chase Paymentech as acquirer, Worldpay for Amazon Pay, no third-party orchestration found; AWS "Cognitive Payments Director" confirms in-house AI routing capability.
**Pain Point:** None identified — Amazon has the engineering resources to build and operate proprietary payment infrastructure at scale.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Not applicable for direct sale.
**Best Case:** N/A.
**Outreach Angle:** Do not target Amazon directly for payment orchestration.

### Insight #2: Amazon Payment Services Is a Yuno Competitor in MENA
**Evidence:** S7 — Mastercard Gateway partnership across 9 MEA countries (Bahrain, Egypt, Jordan, Kuwait, Lebanon, Oman, Qatar, South Africa, UAE).
**Pain Point:** N/A — Amazon is the provider, not the buyer.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Yuno competes with Amazon Payment Services for MENA merchant business.
**Best Case:** Position Yuno as multi-PSP alternative vs. Amazon's single-provider lock-in in MENA.
**Outreach Angle:** Target merchants currently evaluating Amazon Payment Services with Yuno's multi-PSP flexibility message.

### Insight #3: Amazon's 3P Sellers Are the Indirect Opportunity
**Evidence:** S12 — ~$300B in 3P marketplace GMV; sellers also sell on Shopify, Walmart, eBay and need multi-channel payment orchestration.
**Pain Point:** 3P sellers operating across multiple platforms face fragmented payment processing, inconsistent approval rates, and no unified reporting.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single API for payment orchestration across all sales channels beyond Amazon.
**Best Case:** Target high-volume Amazon 3P sellers who also operate DTC Shopify stores or sell on other marketplaces.
**Outreach Angle:** "You've optimized for Amazon. Now optimize payments everywhere else."

### Insight #4: Competitor/Peer Set Contains Orchestration Prospects
**Evidence:** S11 — Coupang ($8B revenue, no fintech buildout), SHEIN (massive cross-border complexity), Temu ($22B GMV, rapid global expansion) lack confirmed payment orchestration.
**Pain Point:** Fast-growing cross-border e-commerce platforms without mature payment infrastructure face approval rate challenges and APM coverage gaps.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart Routing for cross-border transactions, local APM coverage, rapid market entry.
**Best Case:** Coupang exploring payment infrastructure for the first time; SHEIN/Temu need local payment methods across 50+ markets.
**Outreach Angle:** Reference Amazon's deep local APM integration as the benchmark these companies need to match — and Yuno as the fastest path to do it.

### Insight #5: Amazon Pay Expansion Into LATAM Creates Competitive Dynamics
**Evidence:** S6 — Amazon Pay planning 10 new markets with LATAM/Southeast Asia focus; S7 — Global Partner Program opening PSP ecosystem.
**Pain Point:** Amazon Pay entering LATAM markets where Yuno has strong positioning (InDrive, Rappi success cases).
**Yuno Value Prop:** Established LATAM expertise with proven results — position Yuno merchants ahead of Amazon Pay's LATAM entry.
**Best Case:** Win LATAM merchants before Amazon Pay gains traction in the region.
**Outreach Angle:** "Amazon is coming to LATAM payments. Make sure your stack is ready."
