# SDR Research Brief — Grubhub
*Yuno Payment Intelligence Report | Framework v6.0 | Date: 2026-03-05*

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Grubhub is a US-focused online food ordering and delivery marketplace, now owned by Wonder Group (acquired January 2025 for $650M), operating across 4,000+ US cities with 355,000+ restaurant partners. Its payment stack is heavily concentrated on a single provider: **more than 95% of all Grubhub transactions are processed through Braintree (PayPal's enterprise offering)**, representing a critical single-PSP dependency at massive scale. The company has no confirmed international operations or LATAM/APAC/MENA presence, is undergoing significant post-acquisition restructuring (500 layoffs, Feb 2025), and recently added Cash App Pay via a new Block partnership (October 2025) — signaling active APM expansion. The primary Yuno opportunity is addressing the **single-PSP concentration risk** (Braintree/PayPal dependency), the documented **approval rate and refund friction** complaints at scale, and the potential to enable Grubhub/Wonder's ambitions as a "mealtime super app" requiring a more resilient, multi-provider payment architecture.

### 3B. Payment Orchestrator

**No public evidence found of a dedicated payment orchestration platform in use.** Grubhub operates with Braintree as its primary (95%+) payment processor. The recent addition of Cash App Pay via Block (October 2025) represents a new second payment acceptance channel, but there is no confirmed orchestration layer (Spreedly, Primer, Gr4vy, Yuno, etc.) sitting above the stack.

> This is a **critical Yuno signal**: Grubhub is deeply concentrated on a single PSP (Braintree/PayPal) with no orchestration layer. Any Braintree outage, pricing change, or strategic shift creates existential payment risk. The October 2025 Block/Cash App Pay addition suggests the company is beginning to diversify — but without an orchestrator, each new integration requires bespoke engineering work.

**Analysis:**
The most persistent complaint pattern is **refund routing to Grubhub credit instead of the original payment method** — documented across BBB (1,013 complaints in 3 years), Trustpilot (~800+ reviews), and ConsumerAffairs. This is not a technical PSP failure per se, but a deliberate (and unpopular) refund policy that frustrates customers. A secondary issue is **checkout payment errors** ("problem with your payment method") that block conversion.

**Yuno relevance:**
- Smart routing with fallback logic can reduce false declines that lead to frustrated users
- Account Updater and tokenization improvements (already partially implemented via Braintree Vault) reduce card-on-file failures
- Better visibility into transaction-level failure codes would allow Grubhub to differentiate between "genuine decline" and "technical error" — reducing unnecessary refund situations

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights & Outreach Angles

---

**Insight #1: Single-PSP Concentration at Massive Scale — The Highest-Priority Signal**
**Evidence**: Section 3 — "More than 95% of Grubhub transactions are processed by Braintree (PayPal)" (PayPal Case Study, verified).
**Pain Point**: 95%+ dependency on a single PSP (Braintree/PayPal) at 15M+ monthly users creates catastrophic concentration risk. Any Braintree outage, rate renegotiation, or strategic deplatforming causes full payment system failure. Wonder Group, which now owns Grubhub and has $2B+ in combined revenue, cannot afford this risk as it scales toward a "super app" model.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Yuno's orchestration layer sits above Braintree as a primary route while activating secondary PSPs for failover, approval rate optimization, and cost reduction. Smart routing can route around Braintree when it underperforms without any consumer-facing disruption.
**Best Success Case**: **Rappi** — Rappi, also a food delivery super app in LATAM, partnered with Yuno to integrate new payment providers in zero implementation time, achieving 80% reduction in analyst resolution time. Direct parallel to Grubhub's situation.
**Outreach Angle**: "Grubhub/Wonder processes over 95% of transactions through a single PSP. When Rappi faced the same concentration issue, Yuno's orchestration layer gave them provider redundancy and cut anomaly response time from 5 minutes to milliseconds — worth a 15-minute call?"

---

**Insight #2: Authorization Rate Uplift Opportunity Post-Wonder Acquisition**
**Evidence**: Section 3 — PayPal's optimization suite gave Grubhub a 250 BPS uplift and $89.4M in revenue. Section 5 — Persistent checkout errors ("problem with your payment method") and false declines documented across BBB and support forums.
**Pain Point**: Even with Braintree's optimization, there are documented false declines and payment errors at checkout. The 250 BPS uplift already claimed by PayPal means marginal improvements from Braintree alone will be harder to achieve. A multi-PSP strategy (Braintree as primary + backup PSP with smart routing) could unlock an additional 3–7% approval rate uplift per Yuno benchmarks.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Yuno's Smart Routing dynamically routes transactions to the PSP most likely to approve, in real-time. Fallback logic automatically retries on a secondary PSP when the primary declines.
**Best Success Case**: **Reserva** achieved +4% approval rate in under 3 months with Yuno smart routing. **Livelo** achieved +5% approval rate.
**Outreach Angle**: "Your PayPal case study claims 250 BPS from their optimization suite — but that's optimization within a single PSP. With multi-PSP routing, InDrive hit 90% approval rates across 10 markets. Happy to share the model?"
