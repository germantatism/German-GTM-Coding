# DeepSeek — SDR Research Brief
**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Prepared for:** Yuno Sales Team

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

DeepSeek is a Chinese AI company (founded July 2023, HQ Hangzhou) building large language models, funded entirely by High-Flyer Capital hedge fund (~$10B AUM). It operates a prepaid top-up API billing model serving ~350M monthly visits globally — with heavy traffic from China (31%), India (14%), Indonesia (7%), and the US (4–20% depending on period). Its payment stack uses **Antom (Ant Group)** for international card processing, **PayPal** for wallet payments, and **CMB UnionPay** for domestic Chinese rails (Alipay/WeChat Pay). **No payment orchestrator detected.** The key Yuno opportunity lies in DeepSeek's limited international payment coverage — users in countries without PayPal support cannot pay, and there is no local APM optimization across its global traffic footprint.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Payment Coverage Gap Is Blocking Revenue
**Evidence:** S5 — GitHub Issue #347 documents users in countries without PayPal who cannot pay; S4B — India (UPI), Indonesia (GoPay/OVO/DANA), Brazil (Pix) have no confirmed local APMs despite being top traffic markets.
**Pain Point:** DeepSeek is losing paying API customers in its fastest-growing markets (APAC, LATAM, Africa) because its payment stack only supports PayPal + cards + Chinese domestic methods.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single API → 1,000+ payment methods in 200+ countries. Enable UPI, Pix, GoPay, M-Pesa etc. without separate integrations per market.
**Best Case:** InDrive-style rollout — 10+ markets live in <8 months.
**Outreach Angle:** "Developers in India, Indonesia, and Brazil are publicly requesting more payment options on your GitHub. Yuno can activate local APMs in those markets in weeks."

### Insight #2: No Payment Orchestrator + Multi-PSP Setup
**Evidence:** S3 — Antom (international cards), PayPal (wallet), CMB UnionPay (China domestic) = 3 separate payment integrations with no orchestration layer.
**Pain Point:** No smart routing between PSPs, no automatic failover, no unified dashboard across payment rails. Adding new markets/methods requires custom integration work.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single integration replaces 3+ PSP connections. Smart Routing → +7% approval uplift. No-code PSP enablement for new markets.
**Best Case:** Rappi-style deployment — zero implementation time, 80% less analyst resolution time.
**Outreach Angle:** "You're already managing Antom, PayPal, and CMB UnionPay separately. One Yuno integration could unify those and add 50+ local payment methods across your top markets."

### Insight #3: Cross-Border Operations at Scale
**Evidence:** S2 — No local entities in any top market outside China; S1 — 70%+ of traffic comes from outside China.
**Pain Point:** All international transactions are cross-border from China. Cross-border acquiring means higher fees, lower approval rates, and currency conversion costs for users.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart Routing to local acquirers in each market → lower costs + higher approval rates. 50% transaction recovery on declines.
**Best Case:** Livelo — +5% approval uplift, 50% transaction recovery.
**Outreach Angle:** "With 70% of your traffic coming from outside China but no local entities, you're processing everything cross-border. Local acquiring through Yuno could significantly improve approval rates."

### Insight #4: Explosive Growth in Underserved Markets
**Evidence:** S1 — Africa shows 2–4x higher interest than other regions; SE Asia (Indonesia) at 7% traffic; S6 — active expansion into these markets.
**Pain Point:** Africa and SE Asia have complex, fragmented payment ecosystems (M-Pesa, MTN MoMo, QRIS, GoPay, bank transfers). Building these integrations in-house is prohibitively complex.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Pre-built integrations for Africa and SE Asia payment methods. New market live in weeks, no-code PSP enablement.
**Best Case:** InDrive — 10 LATAM markets <8 months, 90% approval rate.
**Outreach Angle:** "Your fastest-growing regions — Africa and SE Asia — have the most fragmented payment landscapes. Yuno already has those integrations built."

### Insight #5: Lean Team, Limited Payment Expertise
**Evidence:** S6 — 160–200 employees, no payment-related job postings, no CFO/VP Payments hires. Team is focused on AI research, not payment infrastructure.
**Pain Point:** With a hyper-lean team of AI researchers, building and maintaining payment integrations across 200+ countries is not a core competency and diverts engineering resources.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Fully managed payment orchestration — no need to hire payment engineers. Real-time monitoring (Rappi: ms detection vs. 5–10 min manually).
**Best Case:** Rappi — zero implementation time for new PSPs.
**Outreach Angle:** "Your 200-person team is building world-class AI — not payment infrastructure. Yuno handles the payment complexity so your engineers don't have to."
