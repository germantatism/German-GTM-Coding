# Arajet — Yuno SDR Research Brief
*Date: 2026-04-10 | Framework v8.0*

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Arajet is the Dominican Republic's ultra-low-cost carrier (ULCC), founded 2021, backed by Bain Capital + Griffin Global, flying ~1.48M passengers in 2025 across 16 countries in the Americas with aggressive 2025–2026 expansion into the US, Mexico, Colombia, Argentina, and Brazil. **Critical finding: Arajet already runs payment orchestration via CellPoint Digital (announced Sep 2024)** — this is a competitive-displacement opportunity, not greenfield. Yuno's strongest angles: superior LATAM APM coverage (PIX, OXXO/SPEI, PSE, Mercado Pago) for their declared 2026 expansion markets, documented refund-to-source friction on Trustpilot, two parallel BNPLs (Laytrip + Fizupay) with a standing checkout-error FAQ, and their planned 2026 co-branded credit card + loyalty program launches.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: CellPoint orchestration is ~18 months old — incumbent risk window is open
**Evidence:** Sep 2024 partnership announcement [S3]. **Pain Point:** CellPoint is airline-vertical specialist but historically weaker on LATAM-local APM rails (PIX/OXXO/PSE/Nequi/Mercado Pago). Arajet's own press release named Mexico, Brazil, Colombia as APM expansion targets — exactly Yuno's home turf. **Yuno Value Prop:** Deeper LATAM-native APM coverage + smart routing +7% approval. **Best Case:** InDrive (10 LATAM markets <8 months, 90% approval). **Outreach Angle:** "You named Mexico, Brazil, and Colombia as APM priorities 18 months ago — how's PIX/OXXO/PSE coverage tracking against plan?"

### Insight #2: Refund-to-source is broken — Trustpilot evidence
**Evidence:** Multiple Trustpilot reviews documenting customers asked for bank details instead of card refunds [S10]. **Pain Point:** Single-route refund failures, manual workarounds, brand damage on a public review platform. **Yuno Value Prop:** Multi-rail recovery + 50% transaction recovery (Livelo case). **Outreach Angle:** "Saw the Trustpilot pattern around refunds — when CellPoint can't refund-to-source, what's the fallback path costing the team?"

### Insight #3: Two parallel BNPLs + a standing checkout-error FAQ = leaking conversion
**Evidence:** Laytrip + Fizupay + PagoVoy live in parallel; Zendesk article "Payment errors at checkout with a Laytrip reservation" [S6][S7][S9][S11]. **Pain Point:** BNPL fragmentation + documented friction. **Yuno Value Prop:** Single API for installments + real-time monitors (Rappi case: ms detection vs 5–10 min manually). **Outreach Angle:** "Three BNPL providers in parallel and a dedicated FAQ for Laytrip checkout errors — there's room to consolidate the installment flow without losing coverage."

### Insight #4: 2026 co-branded credit card + loyalty launch = new payment surface area
**Evidence:** Public roadmap for co-branded card, loyalty, cargo [S18]. **Pain Point:** New tokenization, recurring billing, points-redemption flows that orchestration platforms must support. **Yuno Value Prop:** No-code PSP enablement; new market live in weeks. **Outreach Angle:** "With the loyalty + co-brand card landing this year, how is the payments stack scoping for tokenized recurring + points redemption?"

### Insight #5: Aggressive 2025–2026 expansion is outpacing in-market acquiring
**Evidence:** 5 new US cities in 2025; 6+ new LATAM destinations in 2026; passenger volume +37% YoY [S12][S13][S14][S15]. **Pain Point:** Cross-border acquiring on US/AR/BR/MX/CO routes is expensive vs local acquiring. **Yuno Value Prop:** Smart routing to local acquirers + new market live in weeks. **Best Case:** InDrive (10 LATAM markets <8 months). **Outreach Angle:** "When 78% of your traffic is outside the DR and you're entering 6 new markets in 2026, every basis point of cross-border interchange becomes real money."
