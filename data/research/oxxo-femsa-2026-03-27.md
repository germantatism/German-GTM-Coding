## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

OXXO, a subsidiary of Fomento Economico Mexicano (FEMSA, NYSE: FMX), is Latin America's largest convenience store chain with **25,587 stores** across Mexico, Colombia, Chile, Brazil, Peru, Ecuador, and now the United States (242 stores). FEMSA's consolidated revenue grew **11.2% YoY in FY 2024** and **5.7% YoY in Q4 2025**. The Proximity Americas division (OXXO's parent segment) delivered **12% operating margin** and **4.2% same-store sales growth** in FY 2024. OXXO's fintech arm **Spin by OXXO** has **8.6M active digital wallet users** and **24.6M Spin Premia loyalty members**. FEMSA recently restructured Spin, replacing its CEO, cutting ~1,300 jobs, and pivoting Spin from a standalone fintech play to a tool embedded in OXXO's core retail engine. The company processes payments through **OXXO Pay, NetPay, and Spin**, and is actively exploring banking license options. The Yuno opportunity lies in **payment orchestration across their multi-market LATAM footprint** (6+ countries), **optimizing digital payment acceptance** as cash-to-digital transition accelerates, and **smart routing for their growing e-commerce/bill-pay network** (OXXO Pay handles millions of cash-to-digital transactions monthly).

## SECTION 5 — Key Signals for Outreach

1. **Multi-market payment complexity:** OXXO operates in 7+ countries (Mexico, Brazil, Colombia, Chile, Peru, Ecuador, US), each with different payment regulations, local payment methods, and acquiring infrastructure. Payment orchestration could unify routing across markets.

2. **Spin restructuring = openness to partners:** With Spin pivoting from standalone fintech to embedded retail tool, and banking license deferred in favor of "the right partnership," FEMSA is explicitly signaling willingness to partner on payment/lending infrastructure rather than build everything in-house.

3. **Rapid Brazil/Colombia growth:** ~100 net new stores in Brazil and 20% store base growth in Colombia in 2026 means new payment infrastructure must be deployed at scale in markets with complex local payment ecosystems.

4. **OXXO Pay as cash-to-digital bridge:** Already integrated by Stripe, Rapyd, dLocal, Conekta — but OXXO could benefit from orchestrating the digital side (card, wallet, APM acceptance) more efficiently as cash share declines.

5. **Cost optimization mandate:** MXN 1.8B restructuring savings target by 2027, 20% Spin loss reduction in 2026, and capital discipline (2x leverage target) all point to payment cost optimization as a priority.
