# BetterMe — Yuno SDR Research Brief
**Date:** April 7, 2026 | **Analyst:** Yuno Payments Intelligence

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

BetterMe is a Ukrainian-founded, Cyprus-registered health & fitness app company with **150M+ lifetime downloads**, **~$75–80M ARR**, and operations across **5 continents in 30+ languages**. Their privacy policy confirms a multi-PSP setup with **Adyen, Checkout.com, and PayPal** — and explicitly states they work with too many payment providers to list. No payment orchestration layer was detected, representing a significant whitespace opportunity. The combination of multi-PSP complexity, heavy subscription billing, global cross-border operations, and elevated chargeback risk from billing complaints makes BetterMe a strong Yuno prospect.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Multi-PSP Complexity Without Orchestration
**Evidence:** S3 — Adyen + Checkout.com + PayPal + 3 app store channels + Shopify, with the company stating they work with too many providers to list
**Pain Point:** Managing routing, failover, and reconciliation across 6+ payment channels manually is operationally expensive and error-prone
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single API to orchestrate all PSPs with Smart Routing → +7% approval uplift, unified dashboard
**Best Case:** InDrive — 10 LATAM markets in <8 months, 90% approval, 4.5% recovery
**Outreach Angle:** "You already work with Adyen and Checkout.com — Yuno sits on top to route each transaction to whichever PSP gives you the best approval rate in each market, no code changes needed."

### Insight #2: Cross-Border Risk in Top Markets
**Evidence:** S1 + S2 — UK (#2 traffic) and Mexico (#3 traffic) with no confirmed local entities
**Pain Point:** Cross-border acquiring means higher interchange fees and lower approval rates in their 2nd and 3rd largest markets
**Yuno Value Prop:** Local acquiring via 300+ PSPs → reduced cross-border costs, higher approvals
**Best Case:** Reserva — +4% approval uplift in <3 months
**Outreach Angle:** "Your UK and Mexico users are likely being processed cross-border through Cyprus. Yuno can route those transactions through local acquirers, cutting costs and boosting approvals."

### Insight #3: Elevated Chargeback & Billing Complaint Risk
**Evidence:** S5 — High volume of dual-billing, unauthorized charge, and refund friction complaints across BBB, Trustpilot, Apple Community
**Pain Point:** Chargebacks from dual-channel billing and post-cancellation charges increase processing costs and risk PSP account termination
**Yuno Value Prop:** Real-time transaction monitoring (like Rappi: ms detection vs 5–10 min manually) + unified billing visibility across channels
**Best Case:** Rappi — zero implementation time, 80% less analyst resolution time
**Outreach Angle:** "We noticed some billing friction reports online. Yuno's real-time monitoring catches issues in milliseconds — one of our clients cut analyst resolution time by 80%."

### Insight #4: B2B Expansion Adds Billing Complexity
**Evidence:** S6 — BetterMe Business launched (B2B corporate wellness in 30+ languages)
**Pain Point:** B2B invoicing + consumer subscriptions across multiple currencies = exponentially more complex reconciliation
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single platform handling both B2C and B2B payment flows across all markets
**Best Case:** N/A — position as future-proofing for scale
**Outreach Angle:** "With BetterMe Business expanding into enterprise clients alongside your consumer app, having one payment layer that handles both is critical."

### Insight #5: No Local APMs in Key LATAM Market
**Evidence:** S4 — Mexico is #3 traffic market, but no local APMs (OXXO, SPEI) were verified on checkout
**Pain Point:** Card-only checkout in Mexico significantly limits TAM — 60%+ of Mexican consumers prefer alternative payment methods
**Yuno Value Prop:** No-code enablement of 1,000+ APMs including OXXO, SPEI, Mercado Pago
**Best Case:** InDrive — 10 LATAM markets activated in <8 months
**Outreach Angle:** "Mexico is one of your top markets. Enabling OXXO and SPEI alone could unlock a massive segment of users who don't have international credit cards."
