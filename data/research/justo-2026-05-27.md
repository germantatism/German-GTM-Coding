## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Jüsto is a Mexico-only online grocery (post-OMNi relaunch May 2026) with a **single-PSP stack confirmed as Conekta** (per their own privacy notice). The current payment menu is bare: Visa/MC/Amex + four Mexican meal-voucher providers (Pluxee, Up Sí Vale, Edenred, Toka) + Cash on Delivery, with **no BNPL** despite operating in a market where Kueski Pay and Mercado Pago Cuotas are increasingly table-stakes for AOV >MX$500. Combined with the OMNi rescue mandate to "redesign the business model and focus on profitability over volume," this is a textbook orchestration moment. Strongest Yuno wedge: replace Conekta single-rail dependency with smart routing across Conekta + Mercado Pago + Stripe MX, layer in Kueski/MercadoPago Cuotas BNPL, and own the meal-voucher abstraction as one tokenized vault.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Single-PSP dependency (Conekta) at a relaunch moment
**Evidence:** S3 — Conekta named in privacy notice as the sole aggregator; no Stripe/Mercado Pago/Openpay rails confirmed | **Pain:** Single point of failure on authorization, no failover, MDR locked to one acquirer, no smart routing across BIN ranges | **Yuno value:** Drop a routing layer above Conekta + add Mercado Pago and Stripe MX as parallel rails for redundancy and approval lift (+7% typical) without ripping out Conekta | **Best case:** Livelo (+5% approval, 50% transaction recovery on a similar single-rail-to-orchestrated migration) | **Angle:** "Your privacy notice names Conekta as the aggregator. With OMNi's $100M reinvest in tech, this is the cheapest moment to add a second rail and run routing above both."

### Insight #2: OMNi relaunch mandate = profitability over volume
**Evidence:** S6 — relaunch explicitly framed as "redesign the business model to stop depending only on volume and focus on profitability" | **Pain:** MDR + decline leakage are the highest-leverage costs to attack in online grocery (single-digit margins) | **Yuno value:** Cost optimization through least-cost routing + 50% transaction recovery + analyst time savings | **Best case:** Rappi (ms-level monitor detection, 80% less analyst resolution time) | **Angle:** "Profitability over volume means every declined transaction matters. Yuno's recovery flow gets back ~50% of soft declines without touching your Conekta integration."

### Insight #3: Bankaool owner = payments-fluent buyer
**Evidence:** S6 — Grupo OMNi also owns Bankaool, a Mexican digital bank | **Pain:** OMNi understands acquirer economics, network token cost, MDR composition, and acquirer routing — this is not a payments-naive buyer | **Yuno value:** Orchestration framed as the equivalent of a "payment OS" — same vocabulary OMNi already speaks | **Angle:** "Given OMNi runs Bankaool, you already know MDR is bigger than most line items in grocery. We sit above your acquirers and route by BIN — same logic Bankaool sees inbound, but on the merchant side."

### Insight #4: BNPL gap at the wrong AOV
**Evidence:** S4 — confirmed no Kueski Pay, Aplazo, Atrato, MP Cuotas in homepage footer or FAQ; pre-closure Marketing4eCommerce review flagged "limited payment methods" as Jüsto's weakness | **Pain:** Mexican online grocery basket sizes routinely exceed MX$500 — Kueski and Aplazo are increasingly table-stakes for that AOV bracket | **Yuno value:** Single API to enable Kueski Pay, Aplazo, MP Cuotas, and other BNPL with no-code routing | **Angle:** "I wasn't able to verify any BNPL on your checkout. Adding Kueski/Aplazo through one API could lift conversion on baskets >MX$500 in weeks, not quarters." (Frame as observation, not accusation.)

### Insight #5: Four-voucher-provider complexity = orchestration use case
**Evidence:** S4 — Pluxee, Up Sí Vale, Edenred, Toka all live on the footer + Edenred-specific voucher decline complaint surfaced in third-party review | **Pain:** Each voucher rail is a separate integration with its own auth model and decline pattern. A single Edenred rail failure today bounces a customer to PayPal/COD with no smart retry | **Yuno value:** Unified tokenized vault and retry layer above the voucher providers; ability to add/remove voucher rails (Sí, BBVA Wallet, etc.) without engineering tickets | **Angle:** "You're running four different meal-voucher providers and there's a known Edenred failure pattern. We can tokenize and retry across all four as one rail."
