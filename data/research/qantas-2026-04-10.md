## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Qantas Airways is Australia's flag carrier and one of APAC's largest airlines, with **A$23.8B FY25 revenue**, **55.9M passengers**, and operations spanning 100+ countries via group entities (Qantas, QantasLink, Jetstar AU/NZ/JP, TripADeal). The airline is in an aggressive 2026 expansion phase: **+800K seats**, new ultra-long-haul routes (Perth-Johannesburg, Perth-Rome, Perth-Auckland, Brisbane-Wellington, Samoa) and a leadership reshuffle following its 2024 cyber breach. **No public evidence of a payment orchestrator** was found, and Qantas's APM stack is heavily AU-anchored (BPAY, PayID, Zip, Apple Pay AU-only, Alipay CN-only) with no confirmed iDEAL/Klarna/Google Pay/WeChat Pay across its 7+ regional domains — a strong orchestration + cross-border opportunity for Yuno.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Cross-Border Acquiring Risk in Newly Launched Long-Haul Markets
**Evidence**: Section 6 — Perth–Johannesburg (Dec 2025), Perth–Rome (2026 expansion), no confirmed local entity in South Africa or Italy.
**Pain point**: Cross-border MIDs typically incur 0.5–1.5% higher decline rates and FX markups vs. local acquiring.
**Yuno value prop**: Smart Routing +7% approval uplift + local acquirer onboarding in weeks (no-code).
**Best case**: InDrive — 10 LATAM markets <8 months, 90% approval rate.
**Outreach angle**: "As you ramp Perth–Joburg and Perth–Rome, the difference between cross-border and local ZAR/EUR acquiring can be ~7% on approvals."

### Insight #2: APM Coverage Skewed to AU — Cross-Border Buyers Underserved
**Evidence**: Section 4 — Apple Pay, PayID, BPAY, Zip all gated to AU departures. Alipay limited to CN-language site. No Google Pay confirmed on mainline. No iDEAL/Klarna/UPI/WeChat Pay confirmed.
**Pain point**: Foreign buyers booking Qantas long-haul with home-country payment habits face limited choice → checkout drop-off.
**Yuno value prop**: 1,000+ APMs via single API; geo-rendered checkout by buyer country (not just departure country).
**Best case**: Reserva — +4% approval in <3 months via APM expansion.
**Outreach angle**: "Your APM logic is gated by departure country — that leaves a German tourist booking SYD-LHR paying with Visa instead of Klarna or SEPA."

### Insight #3: Refund & Card-Hold Friction = Recovery Opportunity
**Evidence**: Section 5 — recurring Trustpilot complaints about Qantas refund processing and Qantas Money holds.
**Pain point**: Manual recovery costs analyst hours and damages NPS.
**Yuno value prop**: 50% transaction recovery + real-time monitors (ms-level detection).
**Best case**: Rappi — zero implementation time, 80% reduction in analyst resolution.
**Outreach angle**: "The refund-friction signal on Trustpilot is a known orchestration win — we recover 50% of failed transactions automatically and cut resolution time by 80%."

### Insight #4: Digital Leadership Vacuum + Post-Breach Reshuffle = Buying Window
**Evidence**: Section 6 — Catriona Larritt exits Dec 2025; new Group CIO/CISO post cyber breach; CFO Rob Marcolina also owns Strategy & Transformation.
**Pain point**: New stakeholders typically re-evaluate critical infra in their first 6 months. Payment stack is rarely audited unless prompted.
**Yuno value prop**: PCI Level 1 vault, single API, no-code PSP enablement → minimal lift to evaluate.
**Outreach angle**: "With the digital leadership reset and the cyber breach in the rearview, Q1–Q2 2026 is the natural moment to audit the payment stack for vendor concentration risk."

### Insight #5: TripADeal Acquisition = Multi-Currency OTA Complexity
**Evidence**: Section 6 — TripADeal fully acquired 2024 (A$211M); A$450M+ bookings, holiday packages, multi-currency.
**Pain point**: OTAs have higher payment complexity than airline tickets (multi-leg, multi-currency, installments, refunds).
**Yuno value prop**: Single API across air + holiday packages; routing by transaction type/currency.
**Best case**: Livelo — +5% approval, 50% recovery.
**Outreach angle**: "TripADeal added a full holiday-package OTA to your stack — that's a different payment profile than ticket sales and a strong candidate for orchestration ahead of Loyalty's 2026 push."
