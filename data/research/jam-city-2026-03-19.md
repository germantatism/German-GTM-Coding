## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Jam City is a privately held mobile gaming company headquartered in Culver City, CA, with studios across 6 countries (US, Canada, Germany, Colombia, Argentina, Uruguay). The company has generated over $4 billion in lifetime bookings across titles like Harry Potter: Hogwarts Mystery ($500M+ alone), Cookie Jam, Bingo Pop, and Disney Emoji Blitz. Their payment model relies primarily on Apple/Google/Amazon app store billing, but they have launched a direct-to-consumer (DTC) web store for Bingo Pop powered by **Appcharge** — signaling a strategic shift toward bypassing the 30% app store commission. The main Yuno opportunity lies in (1) expanding DTC web stores to their other major titles with a more flexible payment orchestration layer vs. a single-vendor solution like Appcharge, and (2) cross-border payout optimization for their multi-country studio operations.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights & Outreach Angles

**Insight #1: DTC Web Store Expansion Opportunity**
**Evidence**: Section 3 — Bingo Pop has a DTC web store powered by Appcharge; no other titles have web stores yet.
**Pain Point**: Jam City pays 30% commission to Apple/Google on all IAP revenue. At $4B+ lifetime bookings, even shifting 10% of transactions to DTC saves tens of millions. Currently only Bingo Pop has a web store — their biggest title (Hogwarts Mystery, $500M+) still runs 100% through app stores.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Yuno can power DTC web stores for all titles with a single integration — smart routing across PSPs, 1,000+ payment methods, geo-adapted checkout. Unlike Appcharge (single-vendor), Yuno orchestrates across multiple PSPs for better approval rates and redundancy.
**Best Success Case**: InDrive — 10 LATAM markets in <8 months, 90% approval rate. Relevant because Jam City has LATAM studios (Colombia, Argentina, Uruguay) and a global player base.
**Outreach Angle**: "Saw you launched a DTC web store for Bingo Pop — smart move to recapture app store margins. Curious if you're planning to roll that out across Hogwarts Mystery and Cookie Jam."

**Insight #2: Single-Vendor DTC Risk with Appcharge**
**Evidence**: Section 3 — Appcharge is the sole payment processor for the Bingo Pop web store.
**Pain Point**: Relying on a single payment vendor for DTC creates dependency risk — no failover if Appcharge has downtime, no ability to route to alternative PSPs for better rates or approval rates in specific markets.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Yuno sits above PSPs to provide smart routing, failover, and multi-PSP optimization. InDrive achieved 4.5% transaction recovery rate through multi-PSP routing.
**Best Success Case**: InDrive — multi-PSP orchestration across 10 markets with failover and recovery.
**Outreach Angle**: "Running DTC through a single payment vendor means no failover and no rate optimization across markets. We orchestrate across multiple PSPs so you get the best approval rates per market."

**Insight #3: Cost Optimization Under Financial Pressure**
**Evidence**: Section 6 — Two rounds of layoffs (2022, 2024), Ludia divestiture, failed SPAC. Company is in contraction mode.
**Pain Point**: Payment processing costs are a major line item at $4B+ lifetime bookings scale. Every basis point saved drops directly to the bottom line.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Smart routing optimizes for lowest-cost processor per transaction. Livelo achieved +5% approval rate improvement — at Jam City's scale, that represents significant incremental revenue.
**Best Success Case**: Livelo — +5% approval rate, 50% transaction recovery.
**Outreach Angle**: "At your transaction volume, even small improvements in approval rates or processing costs add up fast."

**Insight #4: Jam City Arcade Expansion to Cards/Wallets**
**Evidence**: Section 3 — Jam City Arcade currently uses carrier billing with stated plans to add credit cards and PayPal.
**Pain Point**: Expanding from carrier billing to cards and wallets requires a payment stack. Building it from scratch is slow and expensive.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Single API to enable credit cards, PayPal, Apple Pay, Google Pay, and 1,000+ methods globally — go live in weeks, not months.
**Best Success Case**: InDrive — zero to 10 markets in <8 months.
**Outreach Angle**: "Saw Jam City Arcade plans to add card and PayPal payments. We can get you live with those plus 1,000+ methods in weeks through a single integration."

**Insight #5: Cross-Border Payout Complexity**
**Evidence**: Section 2 — Studios in 6 countries (US, Canada, Germany, Colombia, Argentina, Uruguay).
**Pain Point**: Managing payouts across LATAM and Europe involves multiple currencies, banking systems, and compliance requirements. Argentina has complex FX regulations.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Cross-border disbursements with a single integration across LATAM.
**Best Success Case**: Rappi — zero implementation time for new providers, 80% reduction in analyst resolution time.
**Outreach Angle**: "Managing payouts to studios across Colombia, Argentina, and Uruguay involves navigating complex FX and banking requirements."
