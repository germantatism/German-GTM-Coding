*Yuno Payment Orchestrator · Framework v8.0 · Generated 2026-03-12*

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Figma is a $1B+ ARR SaaS design platform (NYSE: FIG, IPO July 2025) with ~116M monthly visits and a global user base where 85% of users and ~50% of revenue come from outside the US. Their entire payment infrastructure runs exclusively on Stripe — one PSP handling all subscription billing, multi-currency invoicing, and card processing across 190+ countries. No payment orchestration layer is in place. The primary Yuno opportunities are single-PSP concentration risk at material scale ($1B+ revenue through one vendor), international checkout friction confirmed by user complaints about currency routing errors, and growing billing complexity as Figma introduces hybrid usage-based AI credit billing alongside traditional seat subscriptions in March 2026.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights & Outreach Angles

**Insight #1: Single-PSP Concentration Risk at $1B+ Scale**
**Evidence**: Section 3 — Stripe is the sole PSP for all global transactions (confirmed Stripe case study); no secondary PSP or orchestration layer found
**Pain Point**: 100% of $1B+ annual revenue flows through a single vendor. Any Stripe outage, fee renegotiation, or regional restriction is a direct revenue risk with zero fallback. Post-IPO, this concentration risk should be flagged by risk/finance teams.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Multi-PSP architecture with smart routing — maintains Stripe while adding local acquirers as backup and cost optimization layer
**Best Success Case**: Rappi — zero implementation time for new providers, full redundancy across LATAM
**Outreach Angle**: "With $1B+ in annual revenue running through a single PSP post-IPO, a single point of failure in your payment stack is a material operational risk. We helped Rappi add full PSP redundancy across 9 countries with zero disruption to their existing setup."

---

**Insight #2: International Checkout Friction Confirmed — 85% of Users Are Outside the US**
**Evidence**: Section 5 — confirmed "can't pay in USD" currency routing error reported by multiple international users on Figma Forum; Section 1 — 85% of user base and ~50% of revenue is international
**Pain Point**: International users hitting currency routing errors during checkout = direct conversion loss. With India as #2 traffic market and Brazil fully localized, missed conversions in high-growth markets are compounding.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Local acquiring in key markets (India, Brazil, LATAM) — eliminates cross-border interchange, reduces declines, improves approval rates for local cards
**Best Success Case**: InDrive — 10 LATAM markets in <8 months, 90% approval rate
**Outreach Angle**: "Your India traffic is now your #2 global market — but local card declines on cross-border acquiring can run 15-20% higher than domestic rates. InDrive added local acquiring across 10 markets via a single Yuno integration."

---

**Insight #3: Hybrid Billing Complexity Starting March 2026**
**Evidence**: Section 6 — AI credit usage-based billing launching March 2026 alongside existing seat subscriptions (confirmed in Q4 2025 earnings)
**Pain Point**: Combining subscription billing with real-time usage metering (AI credits) introduces mid-cycle charges, credit top-ups, and proration complexity that pure subscription orchestration (Stripe Billing) was not designed to handle elegantly at scale.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Yuno's unified payment layer can handle mixed billing models — subscription + usage triggers — with a single reconciliation layer
**Best Success Case**: Rappi — real-time anomaly detection across complex transaction types
**Outreach Angle**: "Adding AI credit usage billing on top of seat subscriptions creates reconciliation complexity that compounds fast at $1B+ scale. Worth a conversation on how others have structured this."

---

**Insight #4: Post-IPO Vendor Scrutiny = Window for Payment Infrastructure Review**
**Evidence**: Section 6 — IPO completed July 2025; now subject to public company reporting, procurement scrutiny, and operational efficiency mandates
**Pain Point**: Post-IPO, CFOs and boards scrutinize single-vendor dependencies, payment costs, and approval rate optimization. This is the exact moment payment infrastructure gets reviewed.
**Yuno Value Prop**: PSP fee negotiation leverage (multi-PSP = bidding power), approval rate uplift (up to +7%), and operational consolidation
**Best Success Case**: Livelo — +5% approval rate; Reserva — +4% approval rate in <3 months
**Outreach Angle**: "Post-IPO finance teams routinely audit single-vendor payment dependencies. We recently helped a $500M+ SaaS company add a second acquirer and increase approval rates by 5% without touching their existing Stripe setup."
