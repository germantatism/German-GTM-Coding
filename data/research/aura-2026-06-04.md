# SDR Research Brief — Aura (aura.com)
*Yuno Payment Orchestrator — Framework v8.0 | Date: 2026-06-04*

**Target:** Aura, Inc. — US consumer digital safety / identity-theft protection subscription company. HQ 250 Northern Avenue, Boston MA. Founder/CEO Hari Ravichandran. Founded 2017 (legacy entity "iSubscribed"). *(Not the meditation app, Oura ring, or Aura Frames.)*

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Aura is a US-focused, recurring-subscription consumer security business (~$216M standalone ARR, +30% YoY; >9M families post-merger; ~2M monthly site visits, 81% US). Its payment profile is a **card-on-file, free-trial auto-convert, monthly + annual recurring-billing model** — and its single biggest public pain is **billing friction** (auto-renewal failures, refund disputes, BBB 1.06/5 with ~33 billing complaints). No public PSP or orchestrator could be identified, but PCI DSS / SOC 2 / ISO 27001 are confirmed. **The decisive trigger is the binding Qoria merger** (announced Feb 2026, ASX re-listing under ticker AXQ ~July 2026), which turns Aura into a **multi-geography (US / AU / UK / NZ) >$300M ARR subscription group that must unify two separate billing stacks** — the cleanest Yuno wedge: orchestration consolidation + involuntary-churn recovery on recurring subscriptions.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

**Insight #1: Post-merger billing-stack unification (the lead)**
Evidence: S2/S6 — Qoria becomes a wholly-owned subsidiary; combined US+AU+UK+NZ group, >$300M ARR, ASX-listing ~July 2026. Pain Point: Two independently built subscription/payment stacks across four countries must be consolidated; integration is a CFO-level systems project right now. Yuno Value Prop: One API to orchestrate multiple PSPs/acquirers across all four geographies — no per-market re-integration, new market live in weeks. Best Case: **InDrive** (10 LATAM markets live <8 months on one integration). Outreach Angle: Time the conversation to the integration window — consolidating onto an orchestration layer now avoids welding two legacy stacks together.

**Insight #2: Involuntary churn on recurring subscriptions**
Evidence: S5 — auto-renewal failures, surprise annual rebills, trial-to-paid conversion issues; S8 — card-on-file auto-convert model. Pain Point: Failed renewal payments quietly cap net revenue retention on a >9M-family subscription base, especially as traffic (new-customer acquisition) declines (S1). Yuno Value Prop: Smart retries + card-account-updater + 50% transaction recovery + ~7% approval uplift — grow revenue from the existing base without raising CAC. Best Case: **Livelo** (+5% approval, 50% recovery). Outreach Angle: When site traffic is flat-to-down, the cheapest growth is recovering the renewals you're already losing.

**Insight #3: New international acquiring (AU/UK/NZ) via Qoria**
Evidence: S1/S2 — core product is US-only today; Qoria brings real AU/UK/NZ operations. Pain Point: Serving non-US subscribers well means local acquiring and local payment methods, not cross-border US acquiring (worse approval, FX, higher cost). Yuno Value Prop: Local acquiring + APM enablement per market through one integration. Best Case: **Reserva** (+4% approval <3 months). Outreach Angle: As the combined entity bills internationally, route locally to lift approval and cut cross-border cost.

**Insight #4: Greenfield — no incumbent orchestrator**
Evidence: S3/S7 — no orchestrator and no PSP partnership found publicly. Pain Point: Likely single-PSP dependency (concentration + single point of failure). Yuno Value Prop: Add redundancy + routing with no rip-and-replace. Outreach Angle: A clean orchestration layer added beneath the existing PSP, not a migration.
