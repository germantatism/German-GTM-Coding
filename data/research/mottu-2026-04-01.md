# Mottu — SDR Research Brief
**Date:** 2026-04-01
**Analyst:** Yuno Payments Intelligence

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Mottu is a high-growth Brazilian motorcycle rental platform (BRL 1B+ revenue, 100K+ active contracts, 4,000+ employees) serving gig/delivery workers across 100+ cities in Brazil and Mexico, with plans for further international expansion. No public payment orchestration partner or PSP has been confirmed — the company appears to run payments in-house via its app, accepting Pix, credit card, authorized debit, and boleto. The combination of high-volume recurring billing (weekly/monthly), cross-border operations (BRL/MXN), a new rent-to-own product, and the launch of "Mottu Pay" fintech wallet creates a strong Yuno opportunity around smart routing, multi-PSP orchestration, and transaction recovery at scale.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: High-Volume Recurring Billing Without Visible Orchestration
**Evidence:** 100K+ active contracts × weekly billing = potentially 400K+ transactions/month. No orchestration platform identified.
**Pain Point:** At this scale, single-PSP dependency creates approval rate risk and no failover capability. Weekly billing to underbanked riders (96% from disadvantaged neighborhoods) means high decline rates are likely.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart Routing for +7% approval uplift; 50% transaction recovery on failed recurring charges — critical for a weekly billing model where a failed charge triggers motorcycle blocking.
**Best Case:** InDrive achieved 90% approval and 4.5% recovery across 10 LATAM markets.
**Outreach Angle:** "With 100K+ riders on weekly billing, even a 5% improvement in approval rates means thousands more riders staying on the road instead of getting their bikes blocked."

### Insight #2: Mexico Expansion Creates Multi-Currency, Multi-PSP Complexity
**Evidence:** Active in Brazil (Pix, cards, authorized debit) and Mexico (OXXO, SPEI, cards needed). $30M facility specifically for Mexico growth.
**Pain Point:** Different payment ecosystems (Brazil: Pix-dominant, Mexico: OXXO/SPEI + cards), different currencies (BRL/MXN), different regulatory environments. Managing multiple PSPs across markets increases operational burden.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single API → local payment methods in both markets; no-code PSP enablement; new market live in weeks.
**Best Case:** InDrive went live in 10 LATAM markets in <8 months with Yuno.
**Outreach Angle:** "As you scale Mexico and plan beyond LATAM, Yuno's single API lets you activate local payment methods and PSPs in each new market without rebuilding your payment stack."

### Insight #3: Mottu Pay Launch Signals In-House Fintech Ambition
**Evidence:** Mottu Pay terms published Dec 2025 — building a wallet/fintech layer. QED Investors (fintech VC) is lead investor.
**Pain Point:** Building payment infrastructure from scratch is expensive and slow. As they add wallet, credit, and insurance products, payment complexity multiplies.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Yuno can serve as the orchestration backbone for Mottu Pay — routing, reconciliation, fraud management — so Mottu can focus on the product layer, not the plumbing.
**Best Case:** Rappi achieved zero implementation time and 80% less analyst resolution time with Yuno's real-time monitoring.
**Outreach Angle:** "Building Mottu Pay is the right move — Yuno can be the orchestration layer underneath it, handling routing, retry logic, and reconciliation so your team focuses on the product experience."

### Insight #4: Payment Complaints Reveal Billing Infrastructure Gaps
**Evidence:** Reclame Aqui shows systematic complaints about unauthorized charges, opaque billing, and refund friction — the #1 complaint category.
**Pain Point:** Poor billing transparency and refund automation damage rider trust and increase support costs. At 100K+ contracts, these issues scale poorly.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Real-time payment monitoring (à la Rappi: ms detection vs. 5-10 min manually), automated dispute tracking, and transparent transaction records.
**Best Case:** Rappi reduced analyst resolution time by 80% with Yuno's monitoring.
**Outreach Angle:** "Billing disputes are your top complaint on Reclame Aqui — real-time payment monitoring can flag issues in milliseconds instead of after riders complain."

### Insight #5: Rent-to-Own (Conquiste) Adds Installment Complexity
**Evidence:** 36-month rent-to-own plans launched in 2024 — a fundamentally different billing product from weekly rentals.
**Pain Point:** Managing two billing models (weekly rental + 36-month installments) across two countries requires sophisticated payment routing, retry logic, and reconciliation.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single platform to manage diverse billing models with smart routing and recovery across PSPs and payment methods.
**Outreach Angle:** "The Conquiste program means you're now managing 36-month installment plans alongside weekly rentals — Yuno simplifies both under one orchestration layer."
