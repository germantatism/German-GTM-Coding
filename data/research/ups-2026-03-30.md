# UPS (United Parcel Service) — SDR Research Brief
**Date:** 2026-03-30 | **Ticker:** NYSE: UPS | **HQ:** Atlanta, Georgia, USA | **Website:** ups.com

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

UPS is the world's largest package delivery company with ~$91B in annual revenue, operating in 220+ countries and processing ~5.2B packages annually. Their payment infrastructure appears to be fully in-house through the proprietary "UPS Billing Centre" — no third-party PSP or payment orchestrator was publicly identified. The strongest Yuno opportunity lies in UPS's consumer-facing subsidiaries (Happy Returns, Roadie) and their international billing portals, where APM coverage is limited outside of Asia-Pacific (no PIX in Brazil, no UPI in India, no local wallets in Mexico). UPS's active push to reduce Amazon volume by >50% by June 2026 creates urgency to monetize SMB/DTC merchant relationships that require more sophisticated payment acceptance.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Amazon Volume Cliff Creates Payment Modernization Urgency
**Evidence:** S6 — Amazon volume reduction >50% by June 2026 + SMB grew to 30%+ of US volume
**Pain Point:** UPS must rapidly onboard and retain SMB/DTC merchants who expect modern checkout experiences (digital wallets, BNPL, local APMs) — not legacy invoice-based billing.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single API to enable modern payment acceptance across Happy Returns, Roadie, and UPS.com SMB portals without building infrastructure from scratch.
**Best Case:** Rappi-style implementation with zero implementation time and real-time monitoring.
**Outreach Angle:** "As UPS pivots from Amazon to SMB/DTC, Yuno can power the consumer-grade payment experience these merchants expect — live in weeks, not quarters."

### Insight #2: Asia-Pacific APM Leadership vs. Western Market Gaps
**Evidence:** S4 — Hong Kong/China have 10+ APMs each (Alipay, WeChat Pay, FPS, Apple Pay, Google Pay); US/UK/DE limited to cards + ACH/direct debit + PayPal.
**Pain Point:** Inconsistent payment experience across regions. Western markets lack local payment methods that drive conversion for cross-border shippers.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single integration to unify APM coverage globally — replicate Asia-Pacific breadth in LATAM (PIX, OXXO), Europe (iDEAL, Bancontact), and India (UPI).
**Best Case:** InDrive model — 10+ markets live in <8 months with 90% approval rates.
**Outreach Angle:** "UPS Hong Kong offers Alipay, WeChat Pay, and FPS. But your Mexico and Brazil billing portals are card-only. Yuno can close that gap with a single API."

### Insight #3: In-House Payment Stack = High Maintenance, Low Agility
**Evidence:** S3 — No named PSP; proprietary paymentportal.ups.com; 2% card surcharge to offset processing costs.
**Pain Point:** Building and maintaining an in-house payment stack across 200+ countries is expensive and slow to adapt. The 2% surcharge signals margin pressure on card acceptance.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart Routing can reduce processing costs (lower interchange via local acquiring) and improve approval rates (+7% uplift), potentially eliminating the need for the card surcharge.
**Best Case:** Livelo — +5% approval rate with 50% transaction recovery.
**Outreach Angle:** "The 2% card surcharge tells me processing costs are a pain point. Yuno's smart routing optimizes for the lowest-cost acquiring path — our clients see 7% approval uplift and significant cost savings."

### Insight #4: Post-Payment Friction (Refunds & Chargebacks)
**Evidence:** S5 — 60+ day refund delays, consignee billing chargebacks, reconciliation errors.
**Pain Point:** B2B and B2C refund experience damages customer retention and increases support costs.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Real-time payment monitoring and automated reconciliation. Rappi case: ms-level detection vs. 5-10 min manually; 80% less analyst resolution time.
**Best Case:** Rappi — real-time monitoring, zero implementation time, 80% reduction in analyst workload.
**Outreach Angle:** "I saw reports of 60-day refund delays for approved claims. Rappi had similar reconciliation challenges — Yuno's real-time monitoring cut their analyst resolution time by 80%."

### Insight #5: Consumer-Facing Subsidiaries as Entry Point
**Evidence:** S6 — Happy Returns (10K+ drop-off points) and Roadie (same-day delivery to most US households) are consumer-facing with checkout flows distinct from B2B billing.
**Pain Point:** These products need modern consumer checkout (digital wallets, BNPL) but likely inherit UPS's legacy billing infrastructure.
**Yuno Value Prop:** No-code PSP enablement for new products — new market live in weeks. Can start with Happy Returns or Roadie as proof of concept before expanding to core UPS billing.
**Best Case:** Land-and-expand strategy starting with consumer-facing products.
**Outreach Angle:** "Happy Returns processes millions of consumer refunds. Are those running through the same billing centre as your B2B invoicing? Yuno can power a purpose-built checkout for consumer products — live in weeks."
