## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

VGW (Virtual Gaming Worlds, HQ Perth, Australia) is one of the world's largest social-casino / sweepstakes operators, running **Chumba Casino, LuckyLand Slots, Global Poker** (and new brands LuckyLand Casino / United Slots), with **FY-June-2025 revenue of A$7.3B (~US$5.2B, +19% YoY)** and Chumba alone at ~US$3.7B. Its business is almost entirely payments: players buy Gold Coins (deposits) and redeem Sweeps Coins prizes (payouts). The single most important finding is that VGW **recently consolidated onto Worldpay (FIS) across all brands, explicitly moving "beyond reliance on a single payment processor"** — confirming both a single-PSP history and an active appetite for payment choice/redundancy, with **no third-party orchestrator detected**. The clearest Yuno opportunity: a single orchestration layer over their card + Trustly (ACH) + Skrill + payout rails to lift deposit auth rates, accelerate redemptions (their #1 complaint), add cross-acquirer failover, and cut the per-brand/per-state re-integration cost they keep incurring as they relaunch brands and shift geographies.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

**Insight #1: Single-PSP history + active diversification = orchestration-ready, no orchestrator yet**
Evidence: Worldpay case study explicitly says VGW moved "beyond reliance on a single payment processor" (S11); no orchestrator detected (S3B).
Pain: Adding/managing processors per brand and per rail manually; no cross-acquirer failover.
Yuno value: One API to route across Worldpay + future acquirers, no-code PSP enablement, +7% approval uplift.
Best case: **InDrive** (10 LATAM markets <8 months, 90% approval).
Outreach angle: "You've already decided one processor isn't enough — an orchestration layer is how you add the next three without re-integrating each brand."

**Insight #2: Redemption (payout) speed is the #1 customer complaint**
Evidence: AskGamblers/PissedConsumer/BBB — unpaid/slow redemptions, $1.5k–$4k stuck, "approved but unpaid" (S21–S25).
Pain: 7–10 day first payouts, KYC loops, opaque status; directly drives churn and reputation/legal risk.
Yuno value: Payout routing + 50% transaction recovery + real-time monitors (Rappi: ms detection vs 5–10 min).
Best case: **Livelo** (+5% approval, 50% recovery).
Outreach angle: "Redemption delays are your loudest complaint — routing payouts across rails with real-time status is exactly what we fixed for high-volume payout businesses."

**Insight #3: Constant brand relaunches + state shifts = repeated re-integration cost**
Evidence: New brands (LuckyLand Casino, United Slots) + 12+ state exits + Canada exit (S6, S35–S38).
Pain: Each new brand/geo re-wires payments; slow time-to-market.
Yuno value: New brand/market live in weeks on one integration.
Best case: **Reserva** (+4% approval <3 months).
Outreach angle: "Every new brand you launch shouldn't be a new payments build — one integration powers all of them."

**Insight #4: New CFO + Acting CEO post-going-private = margin scrutiny window**
Evidence: Angus Tiet CFO Nov 2025 (S33); Mats Johnson Acting CEO Feb 2026 (S32); A$5.2B revenue but only ~A$3.2B valuation (regulatory discount) (S27).
Pain: Pressure to optimize cost-of-payments and approval economics.
Yuno value: Auth-rate uplift + least-cost routing = direct margin.
Outreach angle: Time the approach to fresh finance leadership optimizing post-buyout economics.

**Insight #5: Payment/KYC vendors are litigation co-defendants**
Evidence: Trustly, Yodlee, Jumio named in Aug 2025 CA class action (S14).
Pain: Concentration risk on named vendors.
Yuno value: Abstract the stack — swap/route vendors via one layer without re-platforming.
Outreach angle: "When your payment and KYC vendors are named in suits, an abstraction layer lets you change them in days, not quarters."
