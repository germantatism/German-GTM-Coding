- No saved wallet / tokenization mentioned beyond basic card storage
- MTG Arena and D&D Beyond payment infrastructure not publicly documented
- Shopify Plus limits PSP flexibility vs. custom checkout builds

- Subscription service + one-time digital content purchases

- [ESTIMATE — not confirmed]: Subscription billing likely handled by a single PSP

## 6. Yuno Relevance Assessment

### Why Hasbro Could Be a Fit
- $4.7B company with rapidly growing digital revenue ($2.19B from Wizards/Digital Gaming)
- Multiple digital payment surfaces: MTG Arena (PC + mobile), D&D Beyond (subscriptions), Hasbro Pulse (DTC ecommerce)
- International presence across US, UK, EU stores — each with different payment method gaps
- Hasbro Pulse EU/UK stores are notably thin on local APMs
- High-value collector transactions ($50-250+) on Hasbro Pulse are BNPL candidates
- "Scale Digital" is a core strategic pillar — payment infrastructure modernization aligns
- 46% operating margins on digital gaming = high willingness to invest in conversion optimization
- Cross-border complexity: games available in 100+ countries, but DTC stores limited to US/UK/EU

### Potential Pain Points
- Hasbro Pulse EU store has zero local European payment methods beyond cards + PayPal
- No BNPL anywhere despite collector-priced items
- MTG Arena PC payments likely running through a single PSP — approval rate optimization opportunity
- D&D Beyond subscription billing — churn reduction through smart retry/routing
- Three separate regional stores (US, UK, EU) suggest fragmented payment infrastructure
- Apple/Google 30% commission on mobile MTG Arena — Hasbro incentivized to drive PC purchases (where they control payments)

### Risks / Anti-ICP Signals
- Much of Hasbro's revenue comes from wholesale to retailers (Walmart, Target, Amazon) — not direct digital transactions
- Monopoly GO! revenue goes through Scopely, not Hasbro directly
- Mobile game payments constrained by app store billing
- Consumer Products segment (physical toys) is low-margin and declining — not the growth vector
- Payment modernization may be a lower priority vs. game development and IP licensing
