The Yuno opportunity lies in displacing DEUNA in a company processing billions in transactions across 18 markets with severe payment UX issues. **[SDR intel: Cinépolis is with DEUNA — confirmed 2026-04-20]**

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Serial Orchestrator Switcher — DEUNA Still Underperforming

**Evidence:** Cinépolis switched from Spreedly (Dec 2022) to DEUNA. Despite two orchestrators, 76.7% of surveyed users still report transaction failures; Error 307 is trending on TikTok; double charges indicate timeout/retry mishandling.  
**Pain Point:** Cinépolis has already switched orchestrators once (Spreedly → DEUNA) and is still experiencing catastrophic payment failures. They know orchestration matters but haven't found the right partner.  
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart Routing with real-time failover (+7% approval uplift) + transaction monitoring (Rappi: ms-level detection vs. manual 5-10 min). Yuno is LATAM-native like DEUNA but with deeper multi-region coverage (200+ countries vs. DEUNA's LATAM focus).  
**Best Case:** Rappi-style implementation — zero implementation time, 80% less analyst resolution time, real-time anomaly detection for the Error 307 pattern.  
**Outreach Angle:** "Your team invested in orchestration 3 years ago, but Error 307 is still trending on TikTok — we help companies like Rappi catch these patterns in milliseconds instead of waiting for customer complaints."

### Insight #2: 18-Country Complexity with LATAM-Native Needs

**Evidence:** Section 2 — Operations in Mexico, Colombia, Peru, Brazil, Chile, Guatemala, Costa Rica, Panama + India, Indonesia, Middle East, Spain.  
**Pain Point:** Each market requires local acquirers, local APMs (OXXO, Pix, SPEI, UPI, GoPay), and different fraud profiles. Spreedly is US-headquartered with less LATAM depth.  
**Yuno Value Prop:** 300+ payment methods, 60+ connectors, born in LATAM (Colombia), native understanding of regional payment ecosystems.  
**Best Case:** InDrive-style: 10 LATAM markets live in <8 months, 90% approval rates, 4.5% transaction recovery.  
**Outreach Angle:** "We were built for the exact markets you operate in — Mexico, Colombia, Peru, Brazil. Our LATAM-native routing logic understands local bank behavior that US-built orchestrators miss."

### Insight #3: Seat Reservation Timeout Creates Unique Payment Urgency

**Evidence:** Section 8 — Seats are held for limited time during checkout. Failed payments = lost seats = restart entire flow.  
**Pain Point:** In cinema ticketing, a failed transaction doesn't just lose a sale — it loses the specific seats the customer chose, creating abandonment + frustration that compounds with each retry.  
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart Routing ensures first-attempt success; 50% transaction recovery catches failures before seat timeout expires.  
**Best Case:** Livelo-style: +5% approval means thousands fewer customers losing their seats daily, 50% recovery of the remainder.  
**Outreach Angle:** "Every failed payment isn't just a lost transaction — it's a customer losing their seats and starting over. We help recover 50% of those failures before the reservation timer runs out."

### Insight #4: India Expansion + Infrastructure Gaps

**Evidence:** Section 6 — 489 screens, 80 new screens planned, 8 "Future Ready Cinemas." Yet cinepolis.co.in is unreachable (ECONNREFUSED). No UPI confirmation despite India being a UPI-dominant market.  
**Pain Point:** Rapid India expansion with potential digital infrastructure gaps. India requires UPI, Paytm, PhonePe, RuPay, Net Banking — unclear if DEUNA covers India (DEUNA is primarily LATAM-focused).  
**Yuno Value Prop:** Deep APAC connectivity, UPI and local wallet integrations, new market live in weeks.  
**Outreach Angle:** "You're adding 80 screens in India — are you confident your checkout captures UPI users who represent 80%+ of digital payments there?"

### Insight #5: No BNPL Across Any Market

**Evidence:** Section 4/8 — No evidence of Kueski Pay (Mexico), Mercado Crédito, Afterpay, or any installment option in any market.  
**Pain Point:** Premium cinema (Cinépolis is luxury-positioned) with $15-30 tickets + concessions = natural BNPL use case, especially in LATAM where installment payments represent 40-60% of e-commerce transactions.  
**Yuno Value Prop:** No-code PSP/APM enablement — add BNPL across markets without engineering work.  
**Outreach Angle:** Use cautiously — not verified that BNPL is absent, only that it wasn't confirmed.
