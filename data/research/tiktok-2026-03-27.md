**Date:** 2026-03-27 | **Framework:** v8.0 | **Analyst:** Claude AI

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

TikTok, owned by ByteDance (~$155B revenue, 2024), operates TikTok Shop — a social commerce platform live in 17 countries with ~$33B GMV in 2024 and explosive growth (Q1 2025 US GMV +91% YoY). The platform currently relies on **Stripe, Adyen, PayPal, and Worldpay** for payment processing, with no public evidence of a payment orchestration layer. ByteDance is actively building centralized payments infrastructure in-house (PIPO Luxembourg for EU licensing, J.P. Morgan partnership for real-time payments), but the rapid multi-market expansion across Southeast Asia, Europe, and LATAM — each requiring local APMs, COD, and BNPL — creates a strong use case for payment orchestration to manage complexity, optimize routing, and improve approval rates across 17+ markets.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Cross-Border Decline Problem at Scale
**Evidence:** S5 — widespread card decline complaints; items shipping from overseas trigger bank blocks. S1 — top markets (Indonesia, Vietnam, Philippines) without confirmed local entities suggest cross-border acquiring.
**Pain Point:** High decline rates on cross-border transactions directly impact GMV conversion. With $33B+ GMV, even 1% improvement = $330M+ recovered.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart Routing with local acquiring connections in each market → +7% approval uplift. Local-to-local routing eliminates bank-flagged cross-border signals.
**Best Case:** InDrive achieved 90% approval rates across 10 LATAM markets with Yuno.
**Outreach Angle:** "With TikTok Shop expanding to 17 markets, cross-border declines are likely eating into your GMV — we helped InDrive hit 90% approval across 10 LATAM markets by routing locally."

### Insight #2: Multi-PSP Complexity Without Orchestration
**Evidence:** S3 — confirmed use of Stripe, Adyen, PayPal, Worldpay + J.P. Morgan. No orchestration layer detected.
**Pain Point:** Managing 5+ PSPs across 17 markets without orchestration creates operational overhead, inconsistent reporting, and suboptimal routing decisions.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single API to manage all PSPs, no-code enablement of new providers, real-time monitoring dashboard.
**Best Case:** Rappi achieved zero implementation time for new PSPs and 80% less analyst resolution time with Yuno's monitoring.
**Outreach Angle:** "Managing Stripe, Adyen, and Worldpay across 17 markets without a unified layer — we see this a lot. Rappi consolidated their stack through Yuno and cut analyst resolution time by 80%."

### Insight #3: LATAM Expansion Without Local APMs
**Evidence:** S4 — Mexico and Brazil launched but no confirmed local APMs (Pix, OXXO, SPEI, Boleto). S1 — Brazil #3 traffic (106M MAU), Mexico #4 (74M MAU).
**Pain Point:** LATAM consumers heavily prefer local payment methods. Without Pix in Brazil or OXXO in Mexico, TikTok Shop is leaving significant GMV on the table.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single integration to activate 300+ LATAM payment methods. New market live in weeks, no-code PSP enablement.
**Best Case:** InDrive went live in 10 LATAM markets in <8 months with 4.5% transaction recovery.
**Outreach Angle:** "Brazil and Mexico are your #3 and #4 markets by users — are Pix and OXXO live at checkout? We enabled InDrive across 10 LATAM markets in under 8 months."

### Insight #4: In-Housing Strategy Creates Integration Window
**Evidence:** S7 — PIPO Luxembourg entity for EU licensing. S6 — J.P. Morgan real-time payments partnership. ByteDance building Douyin-model centralized infrastructure.
**Pain Point:** Building payments in-house is a multi-year effort. During the transition, TikTok needs to manage both legacy PSPs and new in-house capabilities across 17+ markets.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Yuno can serve as the orchestration layer that manages both external PSPs and internal payment rails, providing flexibility during the transition and beyond.
**Best Case:** Livelo achieved +5% approval and 50% transaction recovery while managing multiple payment providers through Yuno.
**Outreach Angle:** "Building payments in-house across 17 markets is a multi-year play. An orchestration layer lets you manage both your existing PSPs and new internal rails from one API — that's exactly what Livelo did with Yuno."

### Insight #5: Southeast Asia COD Dependency
**Evidence:** S4 — COD confirmed as primary APM across Indonesia, Thailand, Vietnam, Malaysia, Philippines. S1 — These 5 markets represent ~380M MAU combined.
**Pain Point:** COD is operationally expensive (failed deliveries, cash handling) and limits margins. Shifting to digital payments in SEA requires deep local APM integration (GoPay, OVO, Dana, GrabPay, TrueMoney, MoMo, GCash).
**Yuno Value Prop:** 1,000+ payment methods including all major SEA wallets and BNPL. Smart routing optimizes conversion per market.
**Best Case:** Single Yuno integration to activate local wallets across all 5 SEA markets simultaneously.
**Outreach Angle:** "COD in Southeast Asia eats into margins — shifting to digital payments requires local wallet integration across 5 markets. One Yuno integration activates GoPay, GrabPay, TrueMoney, and 50+ more."
