## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Gameloft SE is a ~EUR 303M/year (FY2025, ~$330M) mobile/PC/console game publisher and the core operating asset of publicly traded Vivendi SE since the December 2024 spin-off. Its confirmed direct-billing stack is split across at least two merchants of record with no orchestration layer: **FastSpring is the named Merchant of Record for Gameloft Club billing** (re-confirmed first-party in the privacy notice on 2026-06-05), while the **web store / in-game token purchases run on Xsolla** (Pay Station, geo-localized methods). The platform mix keeps shifting to PC/console, now **51% of revenue in Q1 2026** (up from 47.5% in FY2025), and Gameloft now runs a **live direct-to-consumer store at d2c.gameloft.com** that bypasses app stores. That fragmented, MoR-dependent D2C setup, plus a narrow Gameloft Club method set and a heavy public complaint record around unauthorized charges and refund friction, is the Yuno wedge: smart routing, decline recovery, local methods, and unified monitoring across rails. No payment orchestrator found. 🔴 **High-priority target (score 14).**

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: PC/console now 51% of revenue — D2C volume is accelerating
**Evidence:** §6 — Vivendi Q1 2026: PC/Console = 51% of revenue (up from 47.5% FY25), +11% YoY; live D2C store at d2c.gameloft.com.
**Pain Point:** As direct-billing volume grows outside app-store rails, the Xsolla + FastSpring MoR setup limits routing flexibility, cost control, and unified visibility.
**Yuno Value Prop:** One API above every PSP/MoR with smart routing (+7% approval uplift) and a single reconciliation layer.
**Best Case:** InDrive (10 markets in <8 months, 90% approval).
**Outreach Angle:** "With PC/console past half your revenue and a live D2C store, the time to put one routing and reconciliation layer above Xsolla and FastSpring is before the next launch wave."

### Insight #2: Dual-MoR dependency = visibility gaps
**Evidence:** §3 — FastSpring (Club billing) + Xsolla (store), two intermediaries, no unified view; no orchestrator.
**Pain Point:** Gameloft cannot see unified approval rates or decline reasons across both MoRs; each manages its own PSP relationships.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single API orchestrating all flows with real-time monitoring and unified analytics.
**Best Case:** Rappi (zero implementation time for new providers, 80% less analyst resolution time).
**Outreach Angle:** "Running Xsolla for the store and FastSpring for Club billing — are you getting one view of approval rates and declines across both?"

### Insight #3: Unauthorized charges + refund friction signal weak retry/monitoring
**Evidence:** §5 — high volume of unauthorized/double-charge and subscription-billing complaints; refunds capped at 3 months / 12 weeks; BBB non-response.
**Pain Point:** Payment failures, circular refunds, and DCB disputes erode player trust and LTV.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Real-time monitors (millisecond anomaly detection), network tokenization, 50% transaction recovery.
**Best Case:** Rappi (real-time detection vs 5-10 min manual).
**Outreach Angle:** "The unauthorized-charge and refund complaints are exactly what a unified monitoring and retry layer is built to cut down."

### Insight #4: Post-spin-off margin pressure makes payment efficiency a P&L lever
**Evidence:** §12 — EBITA EUR 19M on EUR 303M = 6.3% margin; Gameloft is Vivendi's flagship public asset.
**Pain Point:** At a 6.3% margin, every basis point of payment efficiency hits investor-facing metrics.
**Yuno Value Prop:** +7% approval uplift; on an estimated EUR 120M direct payment volume, even 1% = ~EUR 1.2M recovered.
**Best Case:** InDrive (4.5% recovery across markets).
**Outreach Angle:** "As Vivendi's flagship asset, D2C payment efficiency lands straight in the numbers investors are watching."
