# Pika AI — SDR Research Brief
**Date:** 2026-04-01 | **Domain:** pika.art | **Vertical:** AI Video Generation (SaaS)

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Pika AI (legal name: Mellis, Inc.) is a Palo Alto-based AI video generation platform valued at ~$900M with ~$48M ARR (2025) and $130M+ projected for 2026. The company operates globally through a single domain (pika.art) with no known regional entities, creating cross-border acquiring risk in key traffic markets. Their payment stack appears to be single-PSP (likely Stripe — **[INFERENCE]**), with limited APM coverage (cards, Apple Pay, Cash App, bank accounts — no PayPal, no BNPL, no local APMs). Significant billing complaints on Trustpilot around refund friction, unclear cancellation flows, and unresponsive support represent a clear Yuno opportunity for payment optimization and improved checkout experience as Pika scales internationally.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Single-PSP Dependency Creates Risk at Scale
**Evidence:** Section 3 — only one PSP signal (Stripe), no orchestrator detected
**Pain Point:** As Pika scales from ~$48M to $130M+ ARR, single-PSP dependency means no failover, no routing optimization, and full exposure to Stripe's pricing and decisions
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart Routing across multiple PSPs → +7% approval uplift, automatic failover, reduced processing costs
**Best Case:** InDrive achieved 90% approval rates across 10 LATAM markets with Yuno's multi-PSP routing
**Outreach Angle:** "As you scale past $100M ARR, having a single payment processor creates a ceiling on approval rates and a single point of failure."

### Insight #2: Cross-Border Acquiring Losses on Global Traffic
**Evidence:** Section 1 + Section 2 — global user base operating through a single US entity with no regional subsidiaries
**Pain Point:** Every non-US transaction is cross-border acquired, meaning higher interchange fees (1-2% premium), higher decline rates, and currency conversion friction
**Yuno Value Prop:** Local acquiring in 200+ countries through Yuno's PSP network → reduces cross-border fees and improves approval rates
**Best Case:** Reserva saw +4% approval uplift in <3 months by switching to local acquiring via Yuno
**Outreach Angle:** "With users across multiple continents, each non-US transaction is paying cross-border premiums and seeing lower approval rates than necessary."

### Insight #3: Billing Complaints Signal Payment Infrastructure Gaps
**Evidence:** Section 5 — multiple Trustpilot complaints about refunds, cancellation, chargebacks
**Pain Point:** Poor subscription management UX + no-refund policy → chargebacks → PSP penalties and lost customers
**Yuno Value Prop:** Real-time transaction monitoring (like Rappi's ms-level detection), subscription management tools, and flexible retry/refund logic
**Best Case:** Rappi achieved 80% less analyst resolution time with Yuno's monitoring; Livelo recovered 50% of failed transactions
**Outreach Angle:** "Trustpilot reviews mention billing friction and chargebacks — Yuno's monitoring and smart retry can reduce involuntary churn while protecting your PSP relationship."

### Insight #4: Limited APM Coverage in a Global Product
**Evidence:** Section 4 — only cards, Apple Pay, Cash App, bank accounts confirmed; no PayPal, no BNPL, no regional APMs
**Pain Point:** Users in LATAM, Europe, and APAC who prefer local payment methods (PIX, iDEAL, Klarna, UPI) face checkout friction or abandonment
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single API → 1,000+ payment methods, no-code enablement of local APMs per market
**Best Case:** New market live in weeks with no-code PSP enablement
**Outreach Angle:** "As Pika grows internationally, adding local payment methods in key markets can directly reduce cart abandonment."

### Insight #5: Rapid Growth Phase = Ideal Time for Payment Infrastructure Investment
**Evidence:** Sections 6-7 — $135M raised, ~$900M valuation, ARR projected to nearly 3x in 2026, Adobe partnership
**Pain Point:** Payment infrastructure decisions made now will compound as volume grows; retrofitting is harder post-scale
**Yuno Value Prop:** Future-proof payment infrastructure that scales with the business, adding PSPs and APMs without engineering effort
**Best Case:** InDrive launched 10 markets in <8 months with Yuno
**Outreach Angle:** "You're at an inflection point — the payment decisions you make now at $50M ARR will define your cost structure at $500M."
