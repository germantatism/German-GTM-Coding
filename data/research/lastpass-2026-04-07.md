# LastPass — SDR Research Brief
**Date:** 2026-04-07 | **Analyst:** Yuno Payments Intelligence

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

LastPass is a global password management SaaS (33M+ users, ~$90.5M ARR) that became independent from GoTo in May 2024, backed by Francisco Partners and Elliott Management. Agent D's checkout verification revealed a **multi-PSP stack (Stripe + Adyen)** with PayPal and Google Pay as wallet options — a more sophisticated setup than expected for a cybersecurity SaaS. However, LastPass has **zero local payment methods** (no SEPA, iDEAL, Boleto, Pix, Alipay) despite significant traffic from the UK (5.93%), Germany (3.29%), and other international markets. Documented payment friction (double charges FAQ, decline FAQ, refund complexity) combined with declining market share (21% → 11%) and no payment orchestration layer present a clear Yuno opportunity for approval optimization and APM expansion.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Multi-PSP Without Orchestration — Optimization Gap
**Evidence:** Section 3 — Stripe + Adyen confirmed via CSP headers, no orchestrator detected
**Pain Point:** Running two major PSPs without orchestration means manual routing rules, no dynamic failover, and suboptimal approval rates
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart Routing across existing Stripe + Adyen connections with automatic failover, +7% approval uplift potential
**Best Case:** Yuno sits on top of existing PSP stack, optimizes routing without requiring PSP migration
**Outreach Angle:** "You already have Stripe and Adyen — Yuno's smart routing layer can optimize which PSP handles each transaction for maximum approval rates, with zero migration risk."

### Insight #2: Global Traffic, Zero Local APMs
**Evidence:** Section 1 (UK 5.93%, Germany 3.29%, Canada 3.68%, Australia 3.22%) + Section 4 (no local APMs confirmed)
**Pain Point:** Users in UK, Germany, and other international markets only have card payments + potentially Google Pay. No SEPA, Open Banking, Klarna, or other preferred local methods
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single API enables SEPA, Open Banking, Klarna, iDEAL, and 1,000+ APMs per market — no-code enablement
**Best Case:** 5-10% conversion uplift in international markets by offering locally preferred payment methods
**Outreach Angle:** "Your UK and European users represent nearly 10% of traffic — offering SEPA, Open Banking, and Klarna could meaningfully improve conversion in those markets."

### Insight #3: Documented Payment Failures Signal Processing Issues
**Evidence:** Section 5 — dedicated FAQ pages for payment declines AND double charges
**Pain Point:** Volume of payment issues is significant enough to warrant dedicated support articles, suggesting non-trivial decline rates and processing errors
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart Routing + transaction recovery (50% recovery rate from Livelo case study) could recover significant failed revenue
**Best Case:** Even a 2-3% improvement on a ~$90M revenue base represents $1.8-2.7M in recovered revenue
**Outreach Angle:** "We noticed LastPass has dedicated support pages for payment declines and double charges — Yuno's smart routing has helped companies like Livelo recover 50% of failed transactions."

### Insight #4: Declining Market Share Increases Revenue Optimization Urgency
**Evidence:** Section 6 — market share dropped from 21% to 11% (2021-2024), 2022 breach headwind
**Pain Point:** With user base under competitive pressure from free built-in solutions (Google 32%, Apple 23%), maximizing revenue from existing paying users is critical
**Yuno Value Prop:** Approval optimization ensures every willing customer can actually complete payment; APM expansion removes friction for international customers
**Best Case:** Payment optimization becomes a lever for retention and international growth at a time when the US market is under pressure
**Outreach Angle:** "As competition intensifies in password management, ensuring frictionless payments — especially for your growing international user base — is a direct lever for retention and revenue."

### Insight #5: Post-Spin-Off Modernization Window
**Evidence:** Section 6 — independence from GoTo completed May 2024, new leadership team, MSP channel growing 200%
**Pain Point:** Post-spin-off is the ideal moment to modernize inherited payment infrastructure (likely legacy GoTo/LogMeIn stack)
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single API replaces legacy payment complexity; new market enablement in weeks
**Best Case:** Yuno becomes the payment orchestration layer for LastPass's independent payment stack
**Outreach Angle:** "Now that LastPass is fully independent, it's a natural moment to audit and optimize your payment infrastructure — Yuno can layer on top of your existing Stripe + Adyen setup."
