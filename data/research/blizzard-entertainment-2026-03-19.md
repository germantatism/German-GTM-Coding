## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Activision Blizzard (now part of Microsoft Gaming following the $68.7B acquisition completed Oct 2023) is one of gaming's largest publishers, operating World of Warcraft, Call of Duty, Diablo, Overwatch, Hearthstone, Candy Crush (King), and more. Activision Blizzard's TTM revenue is $8.70B as of March 2026, contributing to Microsoft Gaming's record $23.45B annual gaming revenue in FY2025. Blizzard runs its own digital storefront (Battle.net) across all major regions with direct-to-consumer payment processing. Battle.net payment methods include cards, PayPal, Battle.net Balance, Afterpay (select regions), PayU installments (LATAM), and recently added AliPay + WeChat Pay (APAC). Microsoft Gaming underwent massive restructuring in 2025 (9,000+ layoffs, studio closures) driven by AI investment pivot — creating both budget pressure and potential openness to consolidation tools. The Yuno opportunity centers on: (1) post-acquisition payment stack consolidation across Activision + Blizzard + King, (2) Battle.net cross-border checkout optimization with limited local APM coverage in key growth markets, (3) massive recurring subscription + microtransaction volume.

## SECTION 9 — Yuno Opportunity Assessment

### Pain Points for Activision Blizzard

1. **Post-Acquisition Payment Stack Consolidation:** Three separate businesses (Activision, Blizzard, King) each with their own payment infrastructure now need to be rationalized under Microsoft Gaming. After 15,000+ layoffs in 2025 and an efficiency mandate driven by $80B AI capex, consolidating via orchestration (vs. rip-and-replace) is the path of least resistance.

2. **Limited APMs on Battle.net:** Cards + PayPal + Battle.net Balance covers core markets. AliPay/WeChat added for China, PayU for LATAM installments, Afterpay for BNPL — but still leaves significant conversion on the table in Brazil (PIX), DACH (SOFORT/Klarna), Netherlands (iDEAL), SEA (e-wallets).

3. **Cross-Border Processing in LATAM/APAC:** No confirmed local entities in Brazil, Mexico, or most of SEA. Likely processing cross-border with higher decline rates and fees.

4. **Massive Microtransaction Volume:** Millions of $0.99-$25 transactions across Candy Crush, Hearthstone, OW2, Diablo 4, CoD store, WoW shop. Smart routing could meaningfully improve approval rates on high-volume, low-value transactions.

5. **Subscription Churn from Failed Payments:** WoW's $14.99/mo subscription across millions of users + Game Pass conversions mean involuntary churn from card declines is a material revenue leak. Network tokenization + intelligent retry logic = direct revenue recovery.

6. **2026 Revenue Events Creating Payment Pressure:** WoW: Midnight (Mar 2026) + Diablo 4: Lord of Hatred (Apr 2026) + BlizzCon 2026 (Sep) — three major launches in one year mean peak transaction volume and peak sensitivity to checkout conversion.

### Best Angles for Outreach

- Lead with post-acquisition consolidation angle (3 payment stacks -> 1 orchestration layer) — especially resonant after 15K layoffs and efficiency mandate
- Secondary: LATAM/Brazil conversion uplift via local acquiring + PIX
- Tertiary: subscription payment recovery for WoW (failed payment retry optimization)

### ICP Fit Score: 8/10

- Digital transaction volume: VERY HIGH (millions of monthly subs + microtransactions across WoW, CoD, Candy Crush, Diablo, OW2)
- Cross-border complexity: HIGH (190+ countries, Battle.net is global)
- PSP complexity: HIGH (three separate business units with independent payment stacks)
- Payment method gaps: MEDIUM-HIGH (added AliPay/WeChat/PayU/Afterpay but still missing key local methods)
- Deduction: Microsoft parent may prefer to build internally or use existing Microsoft Commerce stack; massive layoff environment may slow procurement
