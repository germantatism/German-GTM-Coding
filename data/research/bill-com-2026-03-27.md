# SDR Research Brief — Bill.com (BILL Holdings, Inc.)
**Date:** 2026-03-27 | **Analyst:** Yuno Payments Intelligence | **Framework:** v8.0

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

BILL Holdings, Inc. (NYSE: BILL) is the largest digital B2B payments platform in the United States, processing **$95B in payment volume per quarter** (~$380B annualized) and serving **498,500 businesses**. FY2025 revenue was **$1.46B** (+13% YoY). The company operates a proprietary payment stack built on banking partnerships (JPMorgan Chase, Cross River Bank, Marqeta/Visa) with no third-party payment orchestration layer identified. **Key Yuno opportunity:** BILL's cross-border payments (137 countries, 106 currencies) rely on limited international rails — local acquiring and alternative payment methods in LATAM/APAC/MENA could significantly reduce costs and improve vendor payment speed. Additionally, the company faces potential acquisition by Hellman & Friedman (Feb 2026), activist investor pressure, and growing payment complaints — all creating urgency around payment optimization.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Cross-Border Payment Rail Optimization
**Evidence:** S4 — BILL supports 137 countries / 106 currencies but only via wire transfer and "Local Transfer" (limited rails). No local APMs (PIX, UPI, SPEI, SEPA DD) confirmed.
**Pain Point:** US businesses paying vendors in LATAM, APAC, Africa face higher costs and slower settlement on wire-only rails. BILL charges $19.99 per USD wire.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Yuno's 1,000+ payment methods include local rails in 200+ countries — PIX in Brazil, SPEI in Mexico, UPI in India — at lower cost than international wires with same-day settlement.
**Best Case:** InDrive model — 10 LATAM markets in <8 months, 90% approval rates, 4.5% transaction recovery.
**Outreach Angle:** "BILL processes $95B/quarter across 137 countries. For every vendor payment routed through local rails instead of international wire, you save $10–20 per transaction and deliver funds same-day."

### Insight #2: Potential Acquisition = Payment Stack Review
**Evidence:** S6/S7 — Hellman & Friedman in buyout talks (Feb 2026). Activist investors (Starboard 8.5%, Elliott 5%+, Barington) pushing for profitability.
**Pain Point:** New PE ownership will audit every cost line. Payment processing costs on $380B+ annual volume are a massive optimization target.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart routing across multiple PSPs/acquirers can reduce per-transaction costs by optimizing interchange and routing. At BILL's volume, even 1 basis point = $38M/year.
**Best Case:** Livelo model — +5% approval uplift, 50% transaction recovery.
**Outreach Angle:** "With activist investors pushing for margin improvement and a potential PE buyout, payment cost optimization on $380B+ annual volume is low-hanging fruit. Yuno's smart routing can save basis points at scale."

### Insight #3: Single-PSP Dependency on Cross River / Marqeta for Card Programs
**Evidence:** S3A — Divvy cards issued exclusively by Cross River Bank, processed by Marqeta, on Visa network. No redundancy detected.
**Pain Point:** Single-issuer dependency creates concentration risk. Marqeta or Cross River disruptions would halt all Divvy card operations for 498K+ businesses.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Yuno's multi-PSP orchestration provides failover and redundancy. Real-time monitoring (like Rappi: ms detection vs. 5–10 min manually) ensures uptime.
**Best Case:** Rappi model — zero implementation time, 80% less analyst resolution time.
**Outreach Angle:** "Your Divvy card program depends on a single issuer and processor. Yuno adds redundancy and real-time monitoring so a partner outage doesn't become a customer crisis."

### Insight #4: Growing Payment Complaints Post-Layoffs
**Evidence:** S5 — Trustpilot rating 2.8–3.1/5, complaints about held funds, unauthorized charges, unresponsive support. Service quality declined after 2025 layoffs.
**Pain Point:** Payment failures and delays damage vendor relationships and business trust. Manual resolution is expensive and slow.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Automated payment orchestration reduces failed payments via smart retry and routing. Real-time monitoring catches issues before they escalate.
**Best Case:** Rappi — 80% less analyst resolution time with Yuno's real-time monitoring.
**Outreach Angle:** "We noticed BILL users reporting payment delays and support bottlenecks. Automated payment routing and real-time monitoring can resolve most issues before they reach support."

### Insight #5: ERP Integration Expansion Without Payment Innovation
**Evidence:** S6 — NetSuite, Acumatica, Paychex partnerships all embed BILL's existing payment rails into new channels.
**Pain Point:** More distribution channels = more payment volume on the same limited rails. International vendor payments still wire-only across all integrations.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Yuno can sit behind BILL's API to add local payment methods, smart routing, and multi-PSP redundancy across all integration channels — one integration, all channels upgraded.
**Best Case:** New market live in weeks, no-code PSP enablement.
**Outreach Angle:** "As BILL embeds into NetSuite, Acumatica, and Paychex, every new channel amplifies the same cross-border payment limitations. One Yuno integration upgrades payments across all channels."
