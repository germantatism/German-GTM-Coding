## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

AppLovin (NASDAQ: APP) is an AI-powered mobile advertising and ad tech platform headquartered in Palo Alto, CA, with $4.71B in FY2024 revenue and operations in 17 countries. Their core business is B2B: advertisers (primarily mobile game developers, and increasingly e-commerce brands) pay AppLovin to run performance ads across its 1.4B daily active user network. Their payment stack is bifurcated — advertisers pay in via credit/debit card (prepay, daily billing) or wire/ACH (postpay/invoice for select accounts), while publishers receive payouts via ACH, wire, check, or PayPal, processed through a third-party payout provider (Tipalti). There is no evidence of a payment orchestration platform in use. The primary Yuno opportunity is on the **advertiser-side billing layer** — as AppLovin aggressively expands into e-commerce (now a $1B+ annualized run rate), Japan, South Korea, and LATAM, the complexity of accepting payments across those markets will grow rapidly, creating a compelling case for payment orchestration.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights & Outreach Angles

**Insight #1: E-Commerce Expansion Creates New Payment Complexity Without Infrastructure**
**Evidence**: Section 6 — AppLovin's e-commerce vertical hit $1B+ annualized run rate in 2025 (Q3 2025 results). Self-service platform launching in 2026 targeting global markets including Japan, South Korea, LATAM, and MENA. Currently invite-only for brands spending $600K+/mo on Meta.
**Pain Point**: Onboarding e-commerce advertisers globally means accepting payments from buyers in India (UPI), Brazil (PIX), Japan (bank transfer), and MENA — none of which are supported by their current credit card + wire/ACH billing stack. Each new market requires a new payment rail without orchestration.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Single API to connect local payment methods per market, enabling AppLovin to accept PIX, UPI, SPEI, etc. without building each integration from scratch. New market live in weeks.
**Best Success Case**: InDrive — 10 LATAM markets in <8 months, 90% approval rate. Directly parallels AppLovin's LATAM expansion ambitions.
**Outreach Angle**: "You're scaling e-commerce to LATAM and APAC in 2026. Your current billing stack (card + wire) will create friction for local advertisers in Brazil, India, and Japan. We've done this for InDrive across 10 LATAM markets in under 8 months."

---

**Insight #2: EU Expansion Blocked by GDPR — Payment Localization Is the Next Blocker**
**Evidence**: Section 6 item #7 — AppLovin explicitly deferred EU e-commerce due to GDPR. Section 7 item #5.
**Pain Point**: Once GDPR compliance is resolved, AppLovin will need EU-local payment methods (iDEAL, Bancontact, SEPA direct debit, Sofort/Klarna) to accept payments from German, Dutch, and Belgian e-commerce advertisers. Their current stack has no EU APM layer.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Unified checkout builder that adapts APMs by region, including all EU local methods via a single integration.
**Best Success Case**: Reserva (EU-adjacent complexity) — +4% approval rate in <3 months.
**Outreach Angle**: "When you unlock EU, iDEAL and SEPA direct debit will be table stakes for Dutch and German advertisers. Yuno's EU APM coverage means you won't have to build that layer yourself."

---

**Insight #3: Publisher Payout Complaints Signal Operational Payment Stress**
**Evidence**: Section 5 — Multiple Trustpilot reports of publisher payments withheld, delayed, or ignored (Feb–2024). Despite using Tipalti, support quality for payment issues is poor.
**Pain Point**: At scale, Tipalti alone may not provide the control, transparency, or recovery tools AppLovin needs for global publisher payouts. Payment failure visibility is limited.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Real-time monitoring (as deployed at Rappi: anomaly detection in milliseconds vs. 5–10 minutes manually). 50% transaction recovery rate.
**Best Success Case**: Rappi — 80% reduction in analyst resolution time; zero implementation time for new providers.
**Outreach Angle**: "Your Trustpilot reviews flag payment delays to publishers as a recurring issue. Rappi reduced their payment analyst resolution time by 80% using Yuno's real-time monitors."

---

**Insight #4: Single PSP Dependency on Advertiser-Side Billing**
**Evidence**: Section 3 — Advertiser billing is exclusively credit card (via one undisclosed processor) + wire/ACH. No multi-PSP structure found. No orchestration layer confirmed.
**Pain Point**: A single card processor creates concentration risk. If the processor has downtime or declines certain card types (non-US issuing banks), ad campaigns are interrupted. Daily billing failures mean campaign interruptions — which directly impacts AppLovin's revenue.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Smart Routing across multiple PSPs = up to +7% approval rate uplift. Failover routing prevents campaign interruption from processor downtime.
**Best Success Case**: Livelo — +5% approval rate, 50% transaction recovery.
**Outreach Angle**: "With daily billing across thousands of advertiser accounts, a single card processor failure or approval rate drop directly hits your revenue. Livelo added +5% approval rate with Yuno's smart routing in one integration."

---

**Insight #5: No Confirmed Payment Orchestrator — Greenfield Opportunity**
**Evidence**: Section 3B — No public evidence of Spreedly, Primer, Gr4vy, CellPoint, APEXX, Nuvei orchestration, Airwallex, or Yuno in use.
**Pain Point**: As AppLovin moves from a single-market (US-centric) billing stack to global self-service for e-commerce advertisers, the absence of orchestration means every new payment method or PSP is a custom integration project.
**Yuno Value Prop**: No-code PSP enablement, 1,000+ payment methods, 200+ countries via one API.
**Best Success Case**: InDrive — from 0 to 10 LATAM markets in <8 months without rebuilding payment infrastructure.
**Outreach Angle**: "You're building a self-service global ad platform without a payment orchestration layer. That's the exact problem Yuno solved for InDrive — 10 markets, one integration."
