# SDR Research Brief — Cettire
**Date:** 2026-04-07 | **Analyst:** Yuno GTM Intelligence

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Cettire (ASX: CTT) is an Australian-headquartered luxury fashion e-commerce platform with ~4.3M monthly visits, A$975M gross revenue (FY2025), and operations spanning 19+ currencies across 200+ countries. Agent D's source code analysis revealed a **multi-PSP architecture** with Checkout.com (primary), Worldpay (secondary), and Adyen (select markets + APMs) — but **no payment orchestration layer** managing routing, failover, or optimization across these PSPs. With a A$961 AOV, emerging markets growing 21% YoY (now 45% of gross revenue), and active cross-border expansion into China and the Middle East, Cettire is a strong fit for Yuno's smart routing and orchestration capabilities.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: DIY Multi-PSP Without Orchestration
**Evidence:** S3 — Checkout.com (primary), Worldpay (secondary), Adyen (select markets), eWay (AU backup) all managed via custom Apollo GraphQL routing.
**Pain Point:** Managing 4 PSPs manually without an orchestration layer means no automated failover, no dynamic routing optimization, and no centralized analytics. Engineering overhead is high.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single API to orchestrate all existing PSPs + add new ones. Smart routing for optimal approval rates across markets. Real-time failover without custom code.
**Best Case:** InDrive achieved 90% approval rates across 10 LATAM markets in <8 months with Yuno's orchestration.
**Outreach Angle:** "You've already built multi-PSP routing in-house — Yuno can take that operational burden off your team while improving approval rates with smart routing across Checkout.com, Worldpay, and Adyen."

### Insight #2: Cross-Border Revenue at Risk — US Market Decline
**Evidence:** S1/S2 — US is #1 market (~50% revenue) but declining 13% YoY. No US legal entity found — all transactions appear cross-border from Australia. US tariff changes squeezing margins.
**Pain Point:** Cross-border acquiring in the US means higher interchange fees, lower approval rates, and exposure to tariff/regulatory risk. No local entity = no local acquiring.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart routing to local acquirers in the US market. Cross-border optimization without requiring a local entity.
**Best Case:** Reserva achieved +4% approval uplift in <3 months through local acquiring optimization.
**Outreach Angle:** "With US revenue declining and cross-border costs rising, local acquiring optimization could recover significant margin. Yuno routes to local acquirers in 200+ countries."

### Insight #3: Emerging Markets Growing Fast Without Local APMs
**Evidence:** S1/S6 — Emerging markets now 45% of gross revenue (+21% YoY). S4B — No local APMs configured for South Korea (KakaoPay), Japan (PayPay), UAE (Mada), Saudi (STC Pay).
**Pain Point:** High-growth markets without local payment methods = conversion leakage. Customers in these markets expect local APMs.
**Yuno Value Prop:** No-code APM enablement across 1,000+ payment methods. Go live in new markets in weeks, not months.
**Best Case:** InDrive launched 10 LATAM markets in <8 months with Yuno.
**Outreach Angle:** "Your emerging markets are growing 21% YoY — adding local payment methods like KakaoPay, PayPay, and Mada could accelerate that growth without engineering overhead."

### Insight #4: No PayPal at A$961 AOV
**Evidence:** S4B — PayPal confirmed absent from source code. AOV is A$961 (S12).
**Pain Point:** PayPal's buyer protection is a critical trust signal for high-AOV luxury purchases. Its absence may contribute to cart abandonment, especially for first-time buyers and cross-border shoppers.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Enable PayPal (and 1,000+ other methods) via single integration. No separate PSP contract needed.
**Outreach Angle:** Reference confirmed APMs and suggest expanding coverage — do NOT claim "you don't have PayPal."

### Insight #5: Refund Friction Damaging Brand Trust
**Evidence:** S5 — Dominant complaint pattern is delayed/partial refunds across Trustpilot (3.6/5, 139K reviews) and complaint platforms (1.2–2.1 stars).
**Pain Point:** Refund processing issues erode customer trust in a luxury segment where reputation is everything. 69% repeat rate suggests retention matters enormously.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Real-time transaction monitoring (Rappi case: ms detection vs. 5–10 min manually) + centralized refund processing across PSPs.
**Best Case:** Rappi achieved 80% less analyst resolution time with Yuno's real-time monitoring.
**Outreach Angle:** "Real-time transaction monitoring across your PSPs could help your ops team catch and resolve payment issues before they become Trustpilot reviews."
