# SDR Research Brief — Airtable
**Date:** 2026-03-17
**Researcher:** Claude (Framework v8.0)
**Company:** Airtable (airtable.com)

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Airtable is a San Francisco-based no-code collaborative database platform with ~$478M ARR, 28M+ monthly visits, and 500K+ organizations including 80% of the Fortune 100. Their entire payment stack runs on a single PSP (Stripe) billing exclusively in USD, with 3D Secure explicitly unsupported — a notable limitation for European customers subject to SCA/PSD2 mandates. However, Airtable is a B2B SaaS subscription business with per-seat billing, placing it firmly in Yuno's anti-ICP: no high-volume digital transactions, no cross-border payment complexity, and no APM needs. **Yuno fit: LOW.**

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights & Outreach Angles

**⚠️ IMPORTANT: Airtable falls into Yuno's anti-ICP territory.** This is a B2B SaaS subscription platform with per-seat billing — not a high-volume digital transaction business. The insights below are noted for completeness but do not represent strong outreach angles.

**Insight #1: Single PSP Dependency on Stripe**
**Evidence**: Section 3 — Stripe is the sole payment processor
**Pain Point**: No redundancy if Stripe has downtime; no ability to route transactions for optimization
**Yuno Value Prop**: Multi-PSP orchestration for redundancy and smart routing
**Best Success Case**: N/A — Airtable's billing volume doesn't warrant multi-PSP
**Outreach Angle**: Weak — SaaS subscription billing doesn't need orchestration

**Insight #2: 3D Secure Incompatibility Hurting EU Customers**
**Evidence**: Section 4 — "Credit cards with 3D Secure enabled are not compatible with Airtable's payment processor"
**Pain Point**: European customers with 3DS-mandatory cards (SCA/PSD2) cannot self-serve subscribe
**Yuno Value Prop**: Yuno's checkout handles 3DS natively across all PSPs
**Best Success Case**: N/A
**Outreach Angle**: This is a Stripe configuration issue, not a payment orchestration problem. Airtable would fix this within Stripe, not by adding an orchestrator.

**Insight #3: USD-Only Billing for a Global User Base**
**Evidence**: Section 4 — Terms of Service confirm USD-only billing; 57% of traffic from outside US
**Pain Point**: International customers face FX fees from their card issuers
**Yuno Value Prop**: Multi-currency billing and local acquiring
**Best Success Case**: N/A
**Outreach Angle**: Weak — multi-currency billing is a Stripe feature they could enable without orchestration

**Insight #4: No Local Payment Methods for International Markets**
**Evidence**: Section 4 — No SEPA, PIX, UPI, iDEAL confirmed; only cards + Apple/Google Pay
**Pain Point**: Potential friction for international enterprise customers preferring local methods
**Yuno Value Prop**: 1,000+ payment methods across 200+ countries
**Best Success Case**: N/A
**Outreach Angle**: Weak — B2B SaaS enterprise deals close via invoice/wire, not local APMs

**OVERALL ASSESSMENT: DO NOT PRIORITIZE.** Airtable is not in Yuno's ICP. Their payment needs are simple subscription billing handled adequately by Stripe. The 3DS issue is the only notable gap but is solvable within Stripe. Recommend focusing SDR efforts on companies with high-volume digital transactions, multi-PSP needs, and cross-border payment complexity.
