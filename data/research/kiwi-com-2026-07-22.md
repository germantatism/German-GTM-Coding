## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Kiwi.com is a Czech flight OTA built on virtual interlining, combining flights from carriers that have no agreement with each other. FY2025 revenue was €227M on **€2.04bn of gross bookings**, a take rate of roughly 11.1%, with a near-breakeven net loss after two rounds of layoffs.

**The decisive finding is that Kiwi.com already runs payment orchestration, and appears to run two layers of it at once.** IXOPAY publishes a customer case study dated **21 May 2026** naming Kiwi.com, quoting Senior Fintech Specialist Eliska Hruba, and citing 9 payment adapters and 6 payment methods. Independently, today's production bundle still carries `"zooz_id":"skypicker_new"`, and ZOOZ (PayU Hub) is still named in Kiwi.com's own privacy policy effective November 2025. Any approach premised on "you have no orchestrator" is wrong and will be dismissed in the first minute.

The real opening is **portfolio fragmentation and refund economics**. Kiwi.com's own privacy policy names **12 acquirers and processors**, one of which (Wirecard Bank AG) has been insolvent since 2020, plus **three fraud vendors running concurrently** (Forter, Signifyd, Riskified). It paid **€14.05M in instant credits and refunds in Q1 2026 alone** against disrupted bookings that doubled year over year, while sitting under a binding European Commission commitment to move refunds within a fixed window. And because virtual interlining makes Kiwi.com pay ~800 airlines itself through virtual cards, it is a two-sided payments company whose payout leg no incumbent in its stack covers.

### The merchant-of-record switch, and why it is a payments problem

Kiwi.com's legal terms define the contracting entity **by payment instrument, not by customer location**:

> **Kiwi.com, Inc.** (US) contracts where payment is in **USD via a US-issued Visa, Mastercard, Diners or Discover card, a US PayPal account, or Venmo**. **Kiwi.com s.r.o.** (Czech Republic) is the default for every other user.
> Source: https://www.kiwi.com/us/pages/content/legal/

**[INFERENCE — not confirmed]** A merchant-of-record switch conditioned on card BIN plus currency is a routing decision executed at checkout. Getting it wrong on any single transaction produces the wrong acquirer, cross-border interchange, and a refund that cannot return down the path it came from. This is a concrete orchestration surface and a strong discovery question.

### 3B. Orchestrator

**Kiwi.com runs orchestration today, and the evidence points to two layers.**

**Layer 1, IXOPAY. CONFIRMED, verified directly by the analyst.**
Case study *"How Kiwi.com Scaled Multi-Acquiring with IXOPAY"*, **dated 21 May 2026**, industry given as "Global online travel agency". Stated scale: **9 payment adapters, 6 payment methods**. Named quote:

> *"For us, IXOPAY was a key enabler of our multi-acquiring strategy. It gave us flexibility to route more intelligently, test providers more effectively, and build resilient, optimized payment setup."*
> **Eliska Hruba, Senior Fintech Specialist, Kiwi.com**

The case study states the trigger: COVID-19 exposed fragility in payment operations **particularly around refunds and reconciliation**, and a single-gateway model limited risk management and traffic rebalancing.
Source: https://www.ixopay.com/case-studies/kiwi
IxoPay is independently named in Kiwi.com's own privacy policy under Data Processors.

**Layer 2, ZOOZ / PayU Hub. CONFIRMED present in today's production build.**
The live `www.kiwi.com/en/` bundle, build `dpl=v2026-07-22_3`, contains `"payments":{"zooz_id":"skypicker_new", ...}`. Skypicker was Kiwi.com's brand name before 2016. ZOOZ is a payment-orchestration platform acquired by PayU in July 2018; PayU's own press release names Kiwi.com as a merchant on the ZOOZ-designed **PayU Hub**.
Sources: live bundle · https://corporate.payu.com/news/payu-acquires-israeli-payment-technology-provider-zooz/

A ZOOZ case study on Kiwi.com (vendor-published, 2018 data, © 2019) gives historical numbers, verbatim: *"Kiwi works with 5 processors"*, *"5 APMs are through ZOOZ"*, *"a 5% increase of the overall approval rate"*, and quoting CEO Oliver Dlouhý: *"Kiwi sent a mere 13.18% of the failed transactions to the Instant Retry feature and managed to save 4.6% which equaled 8.3M USD."*

> **[INFERENCE — not confirmed]** Whether ZOOZ is actively routing traffic in 2026 or is a legacy key left in config cannot be determined from outside. **This is the single best discovery question in the account:** "Are you still routing through PayU Hub alongside IXOPAY, or is that legacy?"

### 3C. Payouts, the leg no incumbent covers

Careers copy confirms a dedicated **Payout Solutions team** that *"leverages cutting-edge payment processing technologies, including virtual cards, provided by leading banks and FinTech companies worldwide."* The FinTech tribe is described as *"integrating payment gateways and modern payment methods like Revolut, fraud prevention systems, and trying to invent a smart routing mechanism."*

### Refund mechanics, the mechanism behind the largest cluster

Confirmed from Kiwi.com's own refund page and T&Cs:
- **Kiwi.com Credit lands "within a few minutes." Cash to original payment method takes 7 to 10 business days.** Airline-assisted refunds take *"around 14 days (7 for the airline and 7 for us)."*
- Credit **expires in 24 months**, is *"not refundable, convertible to money"*, and *"The currencies of the Kiwi.com Credit are not exchangeable."*
- **Default-to-credit rule, verbatim:** if a refund offer expires without customer response, *"Kiwi.com may issue the refund as Kiwi.com Credit."*
- **Chargeback penalty clause, T&C 9.4.3, verbatim:** *"If you attempt to recover any amount you paid to us through the chargeback system with your bank, and your chargeback request is denied… we will have the right to reimbursement of the necessary and appropriate costs… up to €20."*

**[INFERENCE — not confirmed]** The instant-credit path being minutes while the cash path is 7 to 10 business days is a **refund-rail latency gap**, and it is the mechanism behind the biggest complaint cluster. €14.05M of instant credit in Q1 2026 alone, against doubled disruption volume, sizes the exposure.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: They already bought orchestration, and may be running two layers
**Evidence:** Section 3B. IXOPAY case study dated 21 May 2026 with a named Kiwi.com employee, 9 adapters, 6 methods. Simultaneously `zooz_id` sits in today's production build and ZOOZ is on the November 2025 privacy policy.
**Pain point:** paying for two orchestration layers, or carrying dead weight in the payment path, while still running 12 direct acquirer relationships and three fraud vendors underneath.
**Yuno value prop:** consolidation. One layer, one reconciliation surface, one vendor register, and routing that a payments team changes without engineering.
**Best case:** Rappi, zero implementation time and 80% less analyst resolution time.
**Outreach angle:** "I read the IXOPAY case study. The question I would ask is what is still running through PayU Hub, because `zooz_id` is still in your production bundle as of this week."

### Insight #2: €14M of refunds in one quarter, against a binding EU refund SLA
**Evidence:** Sections 5 and 7. €14.05M in instant credits and refunds in Q1 2026, disrupted bookings doubled YoY, March alone over €9.17M. Kiwi.com is bound by a June 2023 European Commission commitment to move refunds within 7 days of receipt. BBB and ComplaintsBoard cases from June 2026 show refunds pushed to credit instead of cash and blocked behind open disputes.
**Pain point:** the instant path is credit in minutes; the cash path is 7 to 10 business days on the original instrument. The gap is a refund-rail problem, and it is the mechanism behind the largest complaint cluster and a regulatory exposure.
**Yuno value prop:** refunds routed back down the original rail across every acquirer from one API, with real-time visibility on which ones are lagging.
**Best case:** Livelo, +5% approvals and 50% recovery within 3 months.
**Outreach angle:** "You disclosed €14.05M of instant credits in Q1 with disrupted bookings doubling. The credit path is minutes and the cash path is 7 to 10 days. That gap is where your Trustpilot one-star reviews live."

### Insight #3: The decline-remediation page is a confession
**Evidence:** Section 8. Kiwi.com's official advice to a customer whose card fails is to update their browser, use incognito mode, disable ad blockers, and try again later.
**Pain point:** no smart retry, no cascade to a second acquirer, no network tokenisation, and no fare hold while the customer resolves it. Complaints confirm the consequence: bookings cancelled because the price moved while payment was pending, and customers reporting three different cards rejected across three days.
**Yuno value prop:** Smart Routing plus automatic failover, with up to 50% recovery on failed transactions.
**Best case:** InDrive, 10 LATAM markets live in under 8 months at 90% approval. Kiwi.com's own historical ZOOZ data showed retries on just 13.18% of failed transactions saved $8.3M.
**Outreach angle:** "Your own help page tells customers to disable ad blockers and try later. Kiwi.com's 2019 numbers said retrying 13% of failures saved $8.3M. What would retrying all of them be worth now?"

### Insight #4: A two-sided payments company whose payout leg nobody covers
**Evidence:** Section 3C. Virtual interlining forces Kiwi.com to pay ~200+ airline co-operations itself via virtual cards, run by a dedicated Payout Solutions team. Neither IXOPAY nor ZOOZ addresses payouts.
**Pain point:** the payout leg is built and operated in-house, with the issuer and BIN sponsor never named publicly.
**Yuno value prop:** pay-ins and payouts orchestrated through one platform instead of two separate vendor estates.
**Outreach angle:** discovery-first. "Who issues the virtual cards you use to pay carriers, and is that in the same reconciliation as your pay-ins?"

### Insight #5: Cost pressure makes consolidation an easier internal sell
**Evidence:** Section 6. Two layoff rounds at roughly 18% each, most recently 250 roles in January 2026. FY2025 revenue down 6% to €227M while gross bookings held roughly flat at €2.04bn, meaning the squeeze is margin, not demand. Q1 2026 bookings +80% YoY.
**Pain point:** they must serve growing volume with a materially smaller team, including a smaller payments team.
**Yuno value prop:** routing and provider changes made by payments people without engineering, which is precisely what the IXOPAY case study says they valued.
**Outreach angle:** frame consolidation as headcount leverage, not as a rip and replace.
