## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Kalshi is a US-based event contracts / prediction market platform regulated by the CFTC, operating as a Designated Contract Market (DCM) and Derivatives Clearing Organization (DCO). They processed $22.88B in trading volume in 2025 (1,108% YoY growth), recently raised $1B at an $11B valuation, and expanded to 140 countries in October 2025. Their payment stack relies on 5+ direct PSP integrations (Stripe, Checkout.com, Zero Hash, Plaid, Aeropay) with **no payment orchestration layer** — despite massive volume growth, international expansion, and recurring platform outages during peak events. Kalshi is actively hiring a Payments Lead, signaling a payments stack rebuild — this is a high-priority Yuno opportunity.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights & Outreach Angles

**Insight #1: No Payment Orchestration at $22.88B Annual Volume**
**Evidence**: Section 3 — 5+ direct PSP integrations (Stripe, Checkout.com, Zero Hash, Plaid, Aeropay) with no orchestration layer.
**Pain Point**: Managing 5+ payment partners through individual integrations creates operational overhead, inconsistent monitoring, and no unified failover mechanism. At $22.88B volume, even a 0.1% improvement in routing = $22.88M recovered.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Single API to orchestrate all existing PSPs + add new ones. Smart routing optimizes which PSP handles each transaction based on success rates, cost, and speed.
**Best Success Case**: InDrive — integrated 10 LATAM markets in <8 months, 90% approval rate, 4.5%+ recovery rate. Similar multi-PSP complexity.
**Outreach Angle**: "You're processing ~$2B/week across Stripe, Checkout.com, Zero Hash, and more — without an orchestration layer. At that volume, even small routing improvements represent millions in recovered revenue."

**Insight #2: Peak Event Payment Failures (Super Bowl Incident)**
**Evidence**: Section 5 — Super Bowl Feb 2026: deposits showed as failed while banks charged users. 3 major outages in 5 months.
**Pain Point**: Payment failures during highest-traffic events (when user trust matters most). Failed deposit reconciliation damages brand and causes regulatory scrutiny for a CFTC-regulated entity.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Real-time monitoring with anomaly detection in milliseconds (vs. 5-10 min manually, per Rappi case). Automatic PSP failover ensures deposits route to a working provider when one fails.
**Best Success Case**: Rappi — zero implementation time for new providers, 80% reduction in analyst resolution time. Real-time anomaly detection prevented cascading failures.
**Outreach Angle**: "The Super Bowl deposit failures made headlines — when your primary PSP drops under peak load, an orchestration layer with automatic failover could route those deposits through a backup provider in real-time."

**Insight #3: 140-Country Expansion With No Local APMs**
**Evidence**: Section 4B — International users limited to debit card (Checkout.com), wire (USD only), and crypto. No local APMs confirmed for any non-US market.
**Pain Point**: Users in UK, Canada, Australia, and soon Brazil can only deposit via card (2% fee), wire ($1K min), or crypto. No local payment methods = friction + abandonment. Brazil entry without PIX is a non-starter.
**Yuno Value Prop**: 1,000+ payment methods across 200+ countries. Unified Checkout Builder adapts per region. New market live in weeks with no-code PSP enablement.
**Best Success Case**: InDrive — launched across 10 LATAM markets in <8 months with local APMs in each. Kalshi's Brazil expansion (via XP Inc) could follow the same playbook.
**Outreach Angle**: "With 140 countries now accessible and Brazil launching via XP Inc, local payment methods become table stakes. Yuno could enable PIX, Boleto, and other local methods without building each integration from scratch."

**Insight #4: 2% Debit Card Fee Signals Routing Optimization Opportunity**
**Evidence**: Section 4A — 2% fee on debit card and Apple Pay/Google Pay deposits.
**Pain Point**: 2% is high for debit card processing. At their volume, even a small reduction in processing costs = significant savings. Users on Reddit/Trustpilot complain about the fee.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Smart Routing across multiple acquirers can reduce costs by routing to the lowest-cost provider per transaction. Multi-acquirer setup also improves approval rates.
**Best Success Case**: Livelo — +5% approval rate, 50% transaction recovery through smart routing.
**Outreach Angle**: "At $22.88B in volume with a 2% card deposit fee, there's room to optimize processing costs through multi-acquirer routing — and pass savings on to users or improve margins."

**Insight #5: Actively Hiring Payments Lead = Window of Opportunity**
**Evidence**: Section 6 — Active job listing for Payments Lead (March 2026).
**Pain Point**: The hire signals Kalshi knows their payments stack needs a strategic overhaul. They're building a payments team, likely evaluating the build-vs-buy decision for orchestration.
**Yuno Value Prop**: Yuno can be the orchestration layer the new Payments Lead implements — faster time-to-value than building in-house, with proven enterprise scale.
**Best Success Case**: InDrive — launched Yuno's orchestration layer across 10 markets in <8 months vs. years to build in-house.
**Outreach Angle**: "Saw you're hiring a Payments Lead — whoever takes that role is going to inherit 5+ PSP integrations and a 140-country footprint. A conversation about how orchestration could simplify that stack might be timely."
