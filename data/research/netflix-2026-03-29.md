## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Netflix is the world's largest streaming service with 325M+ paid subscribers across 190+ countries, generating $45.18B in annual revenue (2025). Their confirmed payment stack includes **Adyen** and **Checkout.com** as PSPs, with no public evidence of a payment orchestration layer. Netflix processes recurring subscription payments in multiple currencies with cards, PayPal, UPI (India), Efecty (Colombia), direct debit, and carrier billing (Claro, Movistar) — yet well-documented payment decline patterns and territory-based card rejection errors suggest authorization optimization gaps. With 4+ active payments hiring roles (including "Principal PM, Payment Platform" focused on modernizing payments infrastructure) and aggressive LATAM/APAC growth, Netflix represents a high-value orchestration opportunity for Yuno.

| "Card not supported in this territory" | Netflix Help Center | Frequent — affects expats/travelers | Ongoing | [S13] |
| Bank-side blocks on recurring intl charges | Third-party guides | Common | 2024-2026 | [S14] |
| No self-service refund flow | Netflix Help Center | Structural | Ongoing | [S10] |

**Analysis:**
- **Involuntary churn from payment failures:** Netflix has dedicated help articles for recurring payment errors, indicating a significant volume of failed subscription renewals. **Yuno's smart retry logic and cascading across PSPs** could recover a meaningful percentage of these failures.
- **Territory-based card rejections:** The "card not supported in this territory" error indicates geo-based card filtering that rejects foreign-issued cards. **Yuno's local acquiring network** and intelligent routing could match cards to optimal local acquirers, reducing these rejections.
- **Bank-side declines on recurring international charges:** Banks flagging Netflix recurring charges as suspicious indicates cross-border acquiring friction. **Yuno's local-first routing** could process domestically in key markets, reducing bank-side declines.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: No Payment Orchestration Layer Detected
**Evidence:** Section 3B — No public evidence of Spreedly, Primer, Gr4vy, or any orchestrator. Multiple confirmed PSPs (Adyen, Checkout.com) suggest multi-PSP routing managed internally.
**Pain Point:** Managing multi-PSP integrations, routing logic, and failover in-house across 190+ countries creates engineering overhead and sub-optimal routing.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single API replacing custom integrations. Smart routing optimizes authorization rates per market. No-code PSP enablement for new markets.
**Best Case:** InDrive went live in 10 LATAM markets in <8 months with 90% approval rates.
**Outreach Angle:** "Netflix's scale across 190+ markets likely means your payments team is managing multiple PSP integrations internally — Yuno consolidates that into a single API with smart routing that companies like Rappi use to achieve real-time monitoring and 80% less analyst resolution time."

### Insight #2: Payment Decline Pattern = Revenue Leakage
**Evidence:** Section 5 — Multiple dedicated help articles for payment errors (M7111-5005, 3-5005, "account on hold"), territory-based card rejections, bank-side recurring charge blocks.
**Pain Point:** Failed recurring payments cause involuntary churn. At Netflix's scale ($45B revenue), even a 1% improvement in authorization rates = ~$450M recovered.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart retry logic, cascading across PSPs, 50% transaction recovery rate.
**Best Case:** Livelo achieved +5% approval rates and 50% transaction recovery with Yuno.
**Outreach Angle:** "The volume of payment decline errors your help center addresses suggests authorization optimization could recover significant revenue — Livelo saw +5% approval and 50% recovery after implementing Yuno's smart routing."

### Insight #3: 170+ Countries Without Local Entities = Cross-Border Acquiring
**Evidence:** Section 2 — Netflix has entities in ~22 countries but operates in 190+. Section 5 — "Card not supported in this territory" errors confirm geo-based friction.
**Pain Point:** Cross-border acquiring carries higher decline rates, higher fees, and poorer customer experience. Territory card rejections frustrate subscribers.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Local acquiring through 300+ PSP connections. Route transactions domestically even in markets without Netflix entities.
**Best Case:** InDrive achieved 90% approval rates across 10 LATAM markets through local acquiring.
**Outreach Angle:** "With subscribers in 190+ countries but entities in ~22, there's likely cross-border acquiring friction in many markets — Yuno's local acquiring network could process domestically, reducing the 'card not supported in territory' errors your help center documents."

### Insight #4: Active Payments Infrastructure Investment
**Evidence:** Section 6 — 4+ dedicated payments roles open (Principal PM Payment Platform, Group PM Payments Product Journey, Sr Product Designer Payments). Role description: "lead the evolution of Netflix's payments infrastructure."
**Pain Point:** Rebuilding payments infrastructure from scratch is expensive and slow. Netflix needs scalability and innovation.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Accelerate payments modernization without building from scratch. New market live in weeks vs. months. No-code PSP enablement.
**Best Case:** Rappi achieved zero implementation time and 80% less analyst resolution.
**Outreach Angle:** "I noticed Netflix is hiring a Principal PM to 'lead the evolution of payments infrastructure' — Yuno could accelerate that modernization. Rappi plugged in with zero implementation time, and their team spends 80% less time resolving payment issues."

### Insight #5: LATAM & APAC APM Coverage Gaps
**Evidence:** Section 4 — Efecty (Colombia) and carrier billing confirmed, but PIX (Brazil), OXXO (Mexico), and many APAC methods unverified. India has UPI, Indonesia has GoPay, but coverage appears limited.
**Pain Point:** LATAM (53M subscribers) and APAC (57M subscribers) are Netflix's highest-growth regions with the lowest ARPU — maximizing conversion through local APMs is critical.
**Yuno Value Prop:** 1,000+ payment methods including deep LATAM coverage (PIX, OXXO, PSE, Nequi, Mercado Pago). No-code APM enablement.
**Best Case:** InDrive's LATAM rollout with Yuno: 10 markets in <8 months.
**Outreach Angle:** "Netflix's LATAM and APAC subscriber base is growing fast — Yuno's 1,000+ payment method coverage could help ensure every subscriber can pay locally, the way InDrive did across 10 LATAM markets."
