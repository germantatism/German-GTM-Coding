## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Kaseya** is a Miami-based, Insight Partners-backed IT management platform serving 35,000+ MSPs worldwide through 30+ products (VSA, Datto, IT Glue, Autotask, ConnectBooster). Their payment infrastructure is fragmented across **Stripe** (Quote Manager) and **BNG Payment Gateway** (ConnectBooster/Autotask), with no orchestration layer. The CEO publicly apologized for 15+ months of billing chaos post-Datto acquisition, and the company is actively hiring a **Lead Product Owner for Billing, Collections & Payments** — signaling a major rearchitecture of payment flows across their portfolio. This billing consolidation need, combined with $1.3–1.5B ARR and global MSP customer base, makes Kaseya a high-value orchestration prospect.

## SECTION 10 — Strategic Insights

### Insight #1: Billing Infrastructure Rearchitecture in Progress
**Evidence:** S6 — Hiring Lead Product Owner for Billing, Collections & Payments; Dec 2025 pricing model overhaul; CEO publicly apologized for billing chaos.
**Pain Point:** 18 acquisitions = 18+ billing systems. Payment processing fragmented across Stripe (Quote Manager), BNG Gateway (ConnectBooster/Autotask), and an unidentified processor for the Kaseya Store. No unified orchestration.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Single API to unify all payment flows across 30+ products. Smart Routing for approval optimization. Real-time monitoring to prevent the billing failures that cost them customer trust.
**Best Case:** Rappi — zero implementation time, 80% less analyst resolution time for billing issues.
**Outreach Angle:** "Saw you're hiring a Lead PO for Billing & Payments — when companies consolidate 18+ acquired billing systems, orchestration is usually the first unlock. Happy to share how others at similar scale handled it."

### Insight #2: US-Only ACH = Cross-Border Payment Gap
**Evidence:** S4 — ACH confirmed US-only. No SEPA, iDEAL, BACS, UPI, or local APMs for EMEA/APAC offices.
**Pain Point:** 35,000+ MSPs globally, but payment methods limited to credit card outside the US. This increases transaction costs and decline rates for non-US MSPs.
**Yuno Value Prop:** 1,000+ payment methods across 200+ countries. Enable local payment methods (SEPA for EU, BACS for UK, UPI for India) without building each integration individually.
**Best Case:** InDrive — 10 markets in <8 months, 90% approval rates with local acquiring.
**Outreach Angle:** "Your MSPs in Europe and APAC are likely paying by card when local methods could cut costs and reduce declines."

### Insight #3: ConnectBooster's BNG Gateway = Single-Gateway Dependency
**Evidence:** S3 — ConnectBooster and Autotask both rely solely on BNG Payment Gateway. No fallback processor.
**Pain Point:** Single-gateway dependency means any BNG outage or decline spike impacts payment collection across two core products. No smart routing or failover.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Smart Routing across multiple gateways → +7% approval uplift, 50% transaction recovery. Automatic failover if primary gateway degrades.
**Best Case:** Livelo — +5% approval, 50% recovery with multi-gateway routing.
**Outreach Angle:** "Having ConnectBooster and Autotask both on a single gateway means one downtime event affects payment collection across your entire MSP base."

### Insight #4: Consumption-Based Billing Needs Real-Time Payment Infrastructure
**Evidence:** S6 — Dec 2025 shift from High Watermark to Committed Minimum + Variable Consumption pricing.
**Pain Point:** Variable consumption billing requires real-time metering, flexible invoicing, and robust payment processing that scales with usage. This is harder to execute on legacy billing infrastructure.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Real-time transaction monitoring and analytics. Flexible payment orchestration that scales with variable transaction volumes.
**Best Case:** Rappi — millisecond detection vs. 5–10 min manual monitoring.
**Outreach Angle:** "Moving to consumption pricing is a big infrastructure shift — the payment layer needs to be as flexible as the pricing model."

### Insight #5: 35,000 MSPs = Embedded Payments Opportunity
**Evidence:** S3 — ConnectBooster enables MSPs to collect payments from their end-clients. Quote Manager lets MSPs build branded storefronts with Stripe.
**Pain Point:** Kaseya is effectively a payments platform for 35,000 MSPs, but the payment infrastructure (BNG + Stripe) is fragmented and limited in APM coverage.
**Yuno Value Prop:** Embedded payment orchestration could power a unified payments experience for all MSPs — one integration, all methods, all markets. This turns payment processing into a revenue line for Kaseya.
**Best Case:** InDrive — single integration, 10 markets, 90% approval.
**Outreach Angle:** "You're already powering payments for 35,000 MSPs through ConnectBooster — orchestration could turn that into a competitive moat."
