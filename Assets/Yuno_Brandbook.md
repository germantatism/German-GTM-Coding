## 01 Logo

### 1.1 Our Logo

Through its platform, Yuno builds invisible yet essential bridges — connecting businesses with providers, merchants with their earnings, and customers with their purchases. That's why the logo features a rounded, geometric typeface, and it has a technological design.

The logo is the lowercase word **yuno** rendered in a custom rounded geometric typeface.

### 1.2 Logo Variations

The logo must be used without the symbol. It is available in the following color versions: Yuno Blue on light backgrounds, black on light/gray backgrounds, and white on dark backgrounds.

### 1.3 Our Symbol

In a fragmented payments ecosystem, Yuno emerges as the bridge that brings everything together. This idea of connection and continuity is the foundation of the logo: a sequence of dots coming together to form the brand's initial, symbolizing how Yuno turns every piece of the process into a unified and seamless experience.

The symbol is a dot matrix forming a "Y" shape. It should not be used with the logo. Its use is reserved for a limited number of strategic occasions, specifically when the logo cannot be applied.

### 1.4 Safe Zone

**Logo:** Keep the minimum distance to place elements around the logo following the safe area. The safe zone is defined by the height of the letter "u" in the logotype (referred to as "X"), applied equally on all four sides.

**Symbol:** The same safe-zone principle applies — use the corner quadrant dimension as the minimum clearance around the symbol.

### 1.5 Minimum Size

The logo should never be used at a width smaller than:

| Format | Logo Minimum | Symbol Minimum |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Digital | 64 px | 30 px |
| Print | 1.6 cm | 1 cm |

### 1.6 Misuses

The logo must not be modified. Do not alter its design. The symbol should not be used with the logo.

**Do not:**

- Combine the symbol with the logo
- Apply gradients to the logo
- Distort or stretch the logo
- Change the orientation (e.g., capitalize letters)
- Contain within shapes (circles, rounded rectangles, etc.)
- Mix outlines with filled shapes (symbol)
- Apply drop shadows (symbol)
- Mix colors (symbol)
- Apply inner patterns (symbol)

### 1.7 Spacing System

To ensure visual consistency and alignment across all layouts, the brand uses an 8-point spacing system.

All padding, margins, and distances between elements must be based on multiples of 8 pixels. This creates a structured, predictable rhythm and improves scalability across screen sizes and devices.

**Base unit:** 8px
**Allowed spacing values:** 8px, 16px, 24px, 32px, 40px, 48px, 56px, 64px, 72px, 80px, 88px, 96px, 104px ...

### 1.8 Placement

The elements and positions within the composition must be arranged with specific logic. Five key placement positions are defined within a layout:

1. Top-left corner
2. Top-right corner
3. Center
4. Bottom-left corner
5. Bottom-right corner

### 1.9 Favicon

The brand's favicon is a simplified version of the symbol designed for extremely small spaces without compromising readability. The simplicity and spacing of the brand symbol allow it to be used as a favicon without any modifications.

### 1.10 Color Variations

It is recommended to always use the following combinations:

| Background | Symbol Color | Logo Color |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| Harmony Lilac (light) | Yuno Blue | Yuno Blue |
| Yuno Blue | White | White |
| Unity Black (dark) | White | White |

### 1.11 Co-branding Guidelines

**Collaboration:**
When the Yuno logo is displayed alongside a partner brand:

- Logos must be separated by a vertical divider with a height equal to 2u, where Y represents the height of the "Y" in the Yuno logotype
- Both logos must be optically balanced, maintaining equal visual weight and aligned to the vertical center of the lockup
- The space between the Yuno logo and the divider must be equal to Y
- The divider must use a thin stroke, visually balanced with the logos and scaled proportionally

**Clients & Partnership:**
When presenting clients and partnerships on social media, the same co-branding rules must be followed, using the symbols below to indicate the type of collaboration:

- **"+"** is used for partnerships (e.g., `yuno + unlimit`)
- **"x"** is used for clients (e.g., `yuno x floid`)

The symbol must be positioned between both logos and aligned to the vertical center of the lockup. Spacing and proportions must follow the Y-based construction system.

## 02 Typography

### 2.1 Brand Typeface — Titillium Web

The brand typeface has been chosen for use across all applications — from headlines and body text to indexes and bullet points. Its slim, refined structure evokes the connections and lines made possible by Yuno. Likewise, its rounded edges harmonize with the spheres and circular elements of the visual system.

**Available weights:**

| Weight | Style Name |
|--------|-----------|
| 200 | ExtraLight |
| 300 | Light |
| 400 | Regular |
| 600 | SemiBold |
| 700 | Bold |
| 900 | Black |

**Character set:** Full Latin alphabet (uppercase & lowercase), numerals 0–9, and standard punctuation/special characters.

### 2.2 Uses

The correct use of typography and usage hierarchies is crucial for accurately conveying any type of message.

| Element | Font | Weight | Case | Line Spacing | Letter Spacing |
|---------|------|--------|------|-------------|----------------|
| Titles | Titillium Web | Regular (400) | lowercase | 0 | 0 |
| Subtitles | Titillium Web | Regular (400) | UPPERCASE | 2:1 ratio | 0 |
| Body text | Titillium Web | Regular (400) | lowercase | 3:4 ratio | 0 |
| Charts, indexes, bullets | Titillium Web | SemiBold (600) | UPPERCASE | 0 | 0 |
| Legal text, captions | Titillium Web | Light (300) | lowercase | 0 | 0 |

All text is left-aligned.

### 2.3 Languages

The typography system supports multiple languages and scripts, adapting to their specific typographic rules while preserving visual consistency, clarity, and brand integrity.

| Script | Font |
|--------|------|
| Latin Alphabet (English) | Titillium Web |
| Arabic Script | Noto Sans Arabic |
| Chinese Writing System | Noto Sans SC |
| Japanese Writing System | Noto Sans Japanese |

For Arabic, Chinese, and Japanese: typographic hierarchy is expressed through size and weight (these scripts do not use uppercase/lowercase letterforms).

### 3.3 Color Hierarchy — Usage Order

The color hierarchy defines the priority and functional role of each color within the brand palette. Colors are organized into primary, secondary, and supporting tones to ensure consistent, intentional use across all brand touchpoints.

Supporting and neutral colors are used for backgrounds, typography, and interface elements to maintain clarity and readability.

**Priority order (by visual area):**

1. **Yuno Blue** `#3E4FE0` — dominant, largest area
2. **Harmony Lilac** `#E8EAF5` — second, large background areas
3. **Security Gray** `#92959B` — supporting, small accents
4. **Unity Black** `#282A30` — supporting, small accents and text

### 3.4 Color Contrast

Color contrast ensures legibility and visual clarity across all brand applications.

- High-contrast color combinations must be used for primary content and essential information
- Reduced contrast may be applied to secondary or non-critical elements — such as pagination or metadata — provided readability is maintained
- On dark backgrounds, use light or high-contrast text colors
- On light backgrounds, use dark text colors
- All combinations must align with the brand's color hierarchy and accessibility principles
- The lime/green color (`#E0ED80`) should only be used for small details on Unity Black or solid black backgrounds — it should never be used together with Yuno Blue

### 3.5 Gradients

The selected gradients are a key part of the brand's visual identity — they convey energy, depth, and modernity. Always use the approved gradient styles.

| Gradient Name | From | To |
|--------------|------|-----|
| Black to Blue | `#000000` | `#3E4FE0` |
| Blue to Deep Blue | `#3E4FE0` | `#1227AD` |
| Blue to Cyan | `#3E4FE0` | `#38ADFF` |
| Blue to White | `#3E4FE0` | `#FFFFFF` |

### 3.6 Patterns

These abstract backgrounds are designed to express Yuno's core principles of scale, flow, and orchestration. Built using halftone dot systems, soft blur gradients, and depth transitions, they visually represent data movement, connectivity, and layered infrastructure.

**Pattern types:**

- **Halftone Grid** — Dot-based pattern conveying data and connectivity
- **Blur Gradient** — Soft gradient backgrounds with depth
- **Dynamic Shapes Pattern** — Abstract flowing forms

The patterns create a sense of motion and dimensionality while maintaining a controlled, minimal aesthetic.

## 04 Photography & Imagery

### 4.1 Images

The photography style is modern and atmospheric, blending human presence with abstract and digital elements. Imagery focuses on silhouettes, close-ups, and partial views to create a sense of depth and intrigue.

Lighting is low-key and controlled, using gradients, reflections, and soft glows. A cool color palette dominated by blues reinforces a futuristic, premium feel. Shadows and contrast are used intentionally to shape forms and guide attention.

### 4.2 3D Elements

This 3D abstract minimalism style conveys a sense of innovation, clarity, and forward thinking. The use of simple geometric forms with dynamic lighting and spatial depth reflects a modern, tech-driven mindset while maintaining a playful and approachable tone.

It helps the brand stand out visually, suggesting structure, creativity, and a bold vision for the future.

### 4.3 Misuses

It is important to avoid using generic or stock photographs, as they diminish the emotion and credibility of the brand.

**Do not:**

- Use generic stock photos (business handshakes, suited people posing, etc.)
- Use scattered 3D objects without clear order
- Show people posing directly at the camera
- Use warm-toned, overly lit, or generic business imagery
- Use cluttered or unrelated imagery

## 05 Iconography & Charts

### 5.1 Icons

Icons play a vital role in the brand manual, designed to represent various aspects of the brand identity. It is essential to use these icons in moderation and maintain their original size, as they are intended to serve as subtle details rather than dominant elements.

The icon set is rendered in Yuno Blue (`#3E4FE0`) with a consistent outline/stroke style. It includes icons for: devices, payments, currencies ($, EUR, GBP, JPY, INR, etc.), global/regional elements, people/teams, security, calendar, and more.

Icons are available in both light (on white) and dark (on dark/gradient) background versions in white.

### 5.2 Charts

Charts are a core part of the visual system and should communicate data clearly and consistently.

- Use the brand color palette for all chart elements
- Use simple labels and clean layouts
- Highlight key insights
- Avoid mixing different chart styles within the same presentation

**Supported chart types:** bar charts, stacked bar charts, horizontal percentage bars, line charts, area charts, and progress indicators.

Charts are available for both light and dark themes, using the primary and secondary blue palette for data differentiation.

## 06 Composition & Grid

### 6.1 Margin Construction

As outlined in the Spacing System section, the layout is based on an 8-pixel grid. All margins and padding must use multiples of 8 to ensure consistency across components and screens.

| Aspect Ratio | Resolution | Margin |
|-------------|-----------|--------|
| 1:1 | 1080 x 1080 px | 80 px |
| 16:9 | 1920 x 1080 px | 80 px |
| 4:5 | 1080 x 1350 px | 80 px width |
| 9:16 | 1080 x 1920 px | 56 px width |

### 6.2 Container Construction

It is important to adhere to this composition framework for any communication piece of Yuno. In this section, specific grids are defined for the development of graphics in various formats. It is recommended to make use of the margins and sections outlined.

Containers use rounded corners and follow the 8px grid system. Layouts are divided into primary content areas (header/hero) and secondary content areas (supporting info, CTAs).

### 6.3 Layout Variations

Available grid variations for different aspect ratios:

- **16:9** — Two layout options for landscape presentations and screens
- **9:16** — Two layout options for vertical/mobile/stories content
- **4:5** — One layout option for social feed posts

### 6.4 Content Application

The main content — logo, text, photography, and graphic assets — should be placed in specific areas of the composition:

- Titles in lowercase (Titillium Web Regular)
- Subtitles in UPPERCASE
- Logo or symbol placed in designated positions (corners or footer)
- Photography/imagery fills designated container areas
- Charts and data visualizations follow the grid alignment

### 6.5 Photography Application

The visual system consists of a grid that delineates spaces in the background, ensuring a balanced use of images and photography. It is imperative to adhere to the recommendations outlined in this manual and position these components consistently.
